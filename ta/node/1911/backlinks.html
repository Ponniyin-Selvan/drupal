Dear All,

I read an article is last week Kungkumam weekly magazine that most of
the kalvettus found in TamilNadu are still unread.

The article was an article by Pulavar.Rasu who is ex-head of Kalvettu
Reading Dept,Tanjore Thamizh University. He has assured that we are 95
years trailing in reading the kalvettus.

Almost 1 lakh kalvettus are found in TamilNadu alone and among which
not even 50% is read. Most pitiable to hear is, for many of the
kalvettus whose first copy taken are lost it seems.

He has mentioned that the history may be totally upside down to
whatever we read as history now. This is really scaring !!

Also, a book called "Thamizhar Varalaarum Panpaadum" but
Ra.Pi.SethuPillai too says that more than 30,000 kalvettus are unread
in TN.

I was stunned to read that article. Did any of you had a chance to
read the same? How far is this info true ??

Rgds
Suganya

takes a right round about and hence the name. There is a famous
Ashtabuja Kali of Chozha origin near Ambal sanctum. Behind
Kodimaram, is the WHITE (MINI) VINAYAGAR (Swetha Vinayagar) temple -
equivalent to the size of a normal Pillayar temple we all come across
along the road sides - again of Chozha origin - with Chozha
inscriptions - but of Hoysala architecture (through Vimaladithan ?
son-in-law of sanctum there is a statue of a bearded King.

On the Northern side of the Main Entrance of the temple (North -
East) there is a water tank (with inscriptions and small statues
inside). On the Southern side is (WAS) the kshethra baalar temple
(Kala Bairavar) - size of a small Pillayar temple we normally see.
Silpi has portraited this about 50 yrs back which was published in
Kumbakonam M.Malar recently. This shows the temple, Kshethra baalar,
and so many other statues scattered around. The Kshethra baalar is
NOW in Rajarajan Manimandapam, Thanjavur (near Sangam Hotel, on
Vallam Road) along with other statues of Thiruvalanchuzhi. In the
temple, we have all seen and read the inscriptions of various grants,

Almost 1 lakh kalvettus are found in TamilNadu alone and among which
not even 50% is read.

ABOUT 15000 are published by ASI / State Archlogy under South Indian
Inscriptions;

About 30000 are copied (estempo) and kept in Tracing sheets for the
past 70 / 80 yrs in MYSORE UNIVERSITY.

ASI was originally functioning at Ooty during the British period. An
officer was posted after Independence who hailed from Karnataka. He
preferred his office in Mysore and hence ASI Mysore. After lot of
persuation, ASI Chennai was formed in Fort St George in the sixties.
But these copied tracings are still avl only in ASI Mysore and no one
there now is capable of reading Tamil - leave alone Tamil
inscriptions of 10 Centuries back! Every Tamil Historian is aware of
these details.. but helpless.. For the past 10 yrs only Tamilnadu is
having adequate representation in the Centre - Save RV, TTK & the
alike. Slowly these matters are being taken up.

But ASI is having fund problems. Some of us in the group are working
out details to approach ASI for Private Sector paticipation (1) to
publish those copied, but not published yet (2) to arrange copies
of un-copied inscriptions (3) Using high tech cameras preserving
inscriptions (4)Ditigalising published inscriptions for easy cross-
references.

There are more than 20000 temples and likely to have atleast 10% of
such number of sources. First we are planning to concentrate on
periods between 6 AD and 12 AD - Pandyas - Thevara / Divyadesa
temples apart from temples of Archelogical importance - say about
400. Eliminate published and copied and concentrate on unpublished.
Then concentrate on Vijayanagara / Nayaks built.
