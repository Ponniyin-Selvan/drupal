This '90 is thon-paththu or thon-nooru?900' subject is
a matter of discussion even with Tamil scholars.
Please see this link:
http://www.treasurehouseofagathiyar.net/02100/2127.htm
This is in unicode Tamil.

Widely accepted and believed explanation is that
9= thoL+pathu (meaning reduced ten by one)
90= thoL+nooRu (meaning reduced hundred by ten)
and so on.

2. Synopsis - surukkam
3. Literature theory - ilakkiya kootru (or) kattu
4. Literature survey - ilakkiya mathippaaivu
5. Corollary - mudivu (or) vilaivu

Coming to the numbers ...
I have no clue as why this indifference...
but whenever I come across 900 in tamil, i remember one incident my
father told me.

My father retired as chief cashier from Uco bank some 6 years back.
around 8 years back when he was doing his rural service in a village
near arakkonam,this peculiar situation occured.
A village woman wanted to withdraw money and gave a withdrawal slip,
and as per the slip the cashier gave her 100Rs. There was a dispute
and the matter came to my father.

the lady argued that she wanted 'ayiram rooba' and the cashier is
cheating her by giving only nooru rooba. The slip has only Rs.100
written in it. When questioned, the lady said, 'yaara yematha
pakkareenga...pona varam thollayiram rooba edukka 9 pakkathula rendu
cipher poda sonneenga. ippo ayiram rooba venumna mattum moon cipher
poda sollareenga. enna emathareengala?"

whole office was dumb stuck!!!

so its a question worth pondering. any comments?

Regards,
Satish
