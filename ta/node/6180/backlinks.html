My observation is - all those who take temporary residence (students,
professional assignments, job contracts etc.) in US and those who stay
for longer periods, talk with American accent; and the accent is picked
up very fast, in less than a year. Even the British who visit US for a
reasonable period, return with strong US accents. Indians settled in UK
, in general, still retain Indian accent.

Americans who come to UK do not switch to local accent.

This is my personal experience.

On the lighter side, perhaps Macauley had the American interests in
mind, and anticipated the sojourn to US by Indians.
Sampath
