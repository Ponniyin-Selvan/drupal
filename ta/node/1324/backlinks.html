Dear First of all, wish you all a very happy and peaceful 2004.
Second of all, I have something to say that's probably not very
pleasant to read but I just have to get it off my chest as it has
been bothering me quite a I hate to start the new year on a discordant note but thought if
it's taken in the right sense and spirit, you'll agree that
it's a positive change.
Ok - I'll come to the point: I have noticed of late more and more
mentions to "heavy-weights" and "important players", etc etc. While
this is good in the sense that we give credit where it's due, and
I'll be the first one to admit that a lot of us know very little
about history and are fascinated and learning from these people, we
should also make sure that we do not discourage others from
contributing.
Please don't misunderstand what I'm saying. I think we have some
great researchers here and I have no problems referring to them as
such. But when we say (appr. Example) "Mr. So and So who's an
important player/heavyweight is not contributing - please do so", I
think some of us lightweights might feel a little
intimidated to write. (apparently not me :-) - that has never
stopped me from writing. It is human nature to feel that way. I
love this group and the way it operates. Let's keep it democratic
and free for all, of course minding our language and making sure we
don't hurt any group/individual's feelings.
While we're on that, I also noticed a few ill-chosen words written
in a few emails with strong sentiments by one of our new members.
I waited for the moderator to speak up but since he/they didn't, I
am doing so. We should mind our language and the choice of words
when speaking in a forum like this.
That's all folks!
I realize I am not the moderator and will probably not get any
response from anyone for this, but had to speak up.
Always the trouble-maker,
Latha
