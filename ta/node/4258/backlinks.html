Attention New Members.

hmm...me too native of Vellore. To be precise ancestory is a village
called Kadaladi near Polur. My father was born in Tiruvannamalai and
hence named Arunachalam, a common name in NA, Tiruvannamalai
district. My grandparets were settled in Vellore for more than half
a century. Vellore Jalakandeswarar temple, a master piece of
sculptures is worth seeing. Its inside the fort and hence called
Kottai koil.

Heard stories that the lingam was removed from the temple during
muslim invasion (if I remember during Tipu sultans invasion. correct
me if I am wrong) and was protected in a secret place in
sathuvachari, outskirts of Vellore. Only during early 1980's the
lingam was brought back and consecrated.

Legend says that the English were so obsessed with the art work of
the Kalyana mandapam in the vellore fort temple that they marked the
pices and wanted to dismantle it and take it to England. When all
the pieces were marked and all set for dismantling, the ship which
came to take the pieces sank midway and hence the project was
shelved. Not sure how far its true.

Regards,
Satish
