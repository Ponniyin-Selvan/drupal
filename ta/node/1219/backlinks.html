Dear Gokul,
When you spoke to me y'day, I was literally watching Periyakoil in
Thanjai. I didn't want to tell and cause you anxiety!

For Navarathri - Durga- first 3 days - I visited Praythingara -
Patteeswaram - Nizumbasoodhani - Kodikkaraiamman - Mariamman - Ae
Gouri and of course Thiruvarur - Erisinakkotravai. On the route, I
visited innumerable Grama Devathas - near Udayalur - Nandhipuram -
Ariyappadai veedu, Pambaippadaiveedu on Friday night. I visited
Rajarajan maninadabam (in the basement of the new Tower I worshipped
our Thiruvalnchuzhi Kshethrabalagar - in the small temple bearing the
insciption of ammangai the diety is removed and kept there - that is
Chola's Kaaval Deivam Bairavar - (Poor thing standing there without
worship) and collected some old books of Tam. Arch. Deptt - more of
Nadana kasinathan (Esalam) & Nagasamy. I felt the influence of
Rajendra as well.

While returning, suddenly it dawned on me near Veeranam -
Udayarkudi - that Sembianmadevi (after GKCpm you will find Vanamadevi village - I was
crossing and thinking of Panjavanmadevi!) but was suspecting
Madurantaka @ Uthama ( Mazhavarayar Magalar Sembian Madevi
Thiruvayiru uditha .... ) and she also reciprocated the same for her
& her son's good!!

A friend of mine told me that the prasannam confirmed that indeed
Rajarajeswaram is Akasalingam!!!

I sought for an appointment with Prince Bonsle Sahib of Tanjore
through Kudavoil Balu.

Incidentally, in Thiruvarur there is an Aksharapeedam! If you look
at it, you can make out a 2-D sivalingam carved and removed from the
stone and the remaining stone is called Aksharapeedam. Means another
Akasalingam in 2-D effect.

As you are aware, when Vishnu takes his famous slanting posture in
Thirupparkadal and breathing, on his abdomen, Siva sits and performs
penance and his movement is related to Vishnu's breathing movement.
Since this form of movement - dance - is effortless, it is known as
AJABA (Jaba - with effort) Nadanam. This takes place in Thiruvarur.
To symbolise this only, in Chidambaram, Govindaraja Perumal and
Nataraja are side-by-side in Chidambaram.

Probably, in Rajarajeswaram, in the First floor dancing hall, in the
present court yard, which is uncovered, Rangaraja!! Imagine our beloved Rangaraja lying there idly when the
reverbrent Siva is performing 108 poses of dancing !! That would have
been a feast!

I am yet to read Pullamangai. Shall reply in detail in due course.

Fondly, SB

having way in and way out for blood. The number of breathes we make,
no. of bones, etc., are statiscally depicted in the Kodungai,
rivettes etc. Nataraj had conquered Kali - bay raising his left leg.
Even though Poombuhar was Early Chola capital, Chidambaram Nataraja
was their Kula-Deyvam.

There are also similarities in RRswam. One reaches the Garbagraham
through the side-ways. He chose Mahudabaranam! As per the process,
the Lingam is put in position in the initial stage and then the
temple is constructed - but Poojas were conducted on day-to-day
basis. Imagine now: Such Lingam was probably under the SKY .. Good
heavens!!

In this temple there are 108 (of course 81 carved) dancing postures
of LORD SIVA - no other Model ?! Imagine the Combination of
Akasalingam and each form of Dancing Siva :
RAHASYAM is in the form of Golden Vilvams in Chidambaram:
Here it is the space between the top of Lingam and Bottom of Uchi. As
this is in 3 dimn, this is even supposed to be better than
Chidambaram. Know another thing also: Present Chidambaram is after
the renovation of Thribuvanachakravarthy ?! Sarabeswarar, Sun chariot
and what not.. Latter to of Thiruvidaimaruthur - Kailasam of Sembianmadevi in Thiruvaiyaru
and Chidambaram Akasalingam and overwhelmed them.

I am inclined to think that the Konerirajapuram Nataraja might have
been from RRswm - 9 feet dia - biggest in the World! (Chidambaram is
8 feet dia)

I prefer to speculate that Chidambaram should have been another
Keezhachalai - Martial Art University like Kandalurchalai - full of
Cheras in the East. There was one such University comparable to those
of Nalanda & the "Nagareshu" Kanchi - allegedly where Adisheshan
taught!
