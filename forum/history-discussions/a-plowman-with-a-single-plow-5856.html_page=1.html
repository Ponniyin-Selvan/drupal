Ha Ha Ha, if this is intended towards me, I am no expert Ravi. I

compare Andal and Ayana siRpi, I believe, they both had great
passions on their objectives, only that Andal was more determined
and the destiny favoured Her ("un manathAl en ninaindhirundhAi")
while Ayana siRpi was less determined and so was his destiny. We can
easily compare the passion of both. Andal's passion made her go
against the ritualistic practice of not adorning the garland, before
offering it to God, while Ayana siRpi's passion, made him mad, even
to the extent that he struck a deal with the enemy of His king
(Mahendravarman) who he respected so much.

devotion. Periyazhwar, father of Andal, incidentally describes this
state of mind in his periyAzhwAr thirumozhi says "atthA ariyE endRu

prabhandhams "siRiya thirumaDal" and "periya thirumaDal" in nAyaki
bhAvam, means, thinking of him as a women. So a women should not be
doing this as per the customs prevalent in those times. But his love
for reaching God and the frustration that He is not showing up, made
him go well against the customs and rituals, prevalent at those
times.
