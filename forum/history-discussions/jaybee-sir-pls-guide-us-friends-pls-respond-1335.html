What do you want us to say?...

our jaybee sir has expressed his views on the current
youth.... so it is his liberty to do so..he has every
right to do it... for all the hard work he has done
has come into our wasteful hands ... and what do you
want me to say???????

every man who has scaled the heights have experienced
the same toil and misery that jaybee sir has come
across....can u just recollect what the great
"Subrahmanya bharati" sufferrred when he was alive?
he just utterred a single word "Nenju porukkudhillaye
indha nilai ketta manidharai ninaithuvittaal"... that
is what he said and said no more.....

I accept that he feels we are not the youth for whom
his work are intended....but it does not mean that we
are misfits and laggards who lack their identity...
this has been the case in ancient times where
Aristotle and Plato have expressed dissatisfaction
over the youth.. but the society did not stop there
... there were huge advances after that....

So My feeling is that we give the due respect to Our
jaybee sir's feelings ..a t the same time there should
not be any worry over what ourjaybee sir has said...
Leave what he said as his opinion and do not take that
to great heights and make it a great issue.. brush it
aside with due respects.

with regards
B.Dhiwakar

I will write in detail as we go along.

Perhaps I should tell you about my founding of youth movements
among the Tamils in backwater areas in Malaysia. Those movements are
doing well. And those earst-while youths are also doing well.

My involvement with youths has a long history.

I have been involved since my college days when I was the
student chairman of the Madurai Medical College. I was around
for two years - 1966 and 1967. Why two years? Because they could not
hold elections during those turbulent times. I am sure you would
recall what was happening at that time.
:-)

Until this day, I have close contact with the Tamilian youth of
Malaysia.

I have also made futuristic surveys on Malaysian Tamilians and
Hinduism.

My study titled 'Hinduism - Past, Present, Future' is a
ground-breaker. It had a lot of repercussions.
The predictions made on that study are found to be correct
after just 7 years. It has - what you would call
'pin-point accuracy'.

That is why I worry.

So.....

I am not just a religious scholar or a cultural scholar or a
historian.

I am involved deeply with our society as well.

That is why I took the liberty to write all that stuff in my
previous letter.

I am sorry.....

But all this is not in the scope of PS.

I should'nt be writing all this here.

I hesitate because PS is something for Cholziyan history.

Soon I will write somewhat in detail privately?

I don't brook any hard feelings:-)

Regards

JayBee
