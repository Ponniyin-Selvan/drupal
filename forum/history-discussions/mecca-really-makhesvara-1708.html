JayBee Date: Tue, 05 Oct 2004

I do not know whether this is the right platform to discuss, yet some
learned told me long time back that Muslims' religeous recitals formed part
of "Adarvana". We see them cure lot of things using mantras - chanting
something repeatedly!

The mantras from Atharva are entirely different from what
the Moslems use.
The Moslems chant verses of Quran for specific purposes.
They are known as 'Ayath al Quaran'.
They are repeated for a certain number of times.
The Fateha is one such ritual.
Allah has one hundred names. These names have methods
of repeating. They use a special type of japamala known as
Taqbiz. It has one hundred beads - unlike the Hindu japamala which
has 108 or 51 or 21.
Allah has names like Al Aziz, Al Hakim, Al Huq, Al Wahid,
etc. All the names have meanings like the Hindu namavaLis.
Azis is Conqueror, Huq is Truth, Hakim is Judge, Wahid is the First.
These names are also chanted and repeated.
A aqbiz which has been used many many times for constant
recitations of the namas are beleived have developed powers of their
own. The very presence of the Taqbiz is beleived to drive away
evil spirits.

I thought that it would be only appropriate to pass correct
information where a wrong beleif is held.
