I beg to stick to my impression:

I have been reading PRIMARY SOURCES (inscriptions / copper plates etc)
- in the form of Archelogical Volumes.

These are inscriptions which were copied during 1880 - 1910 (about 30
years of work) and published / being published under about 27 / 28
Volumes... as South Indian Inscriptions ..(with copies of copper
plates as well).

Tamilnadu State Archeology has also brought out several volumes and
has been brining out also.

Private sources like Aavanam (Tholliyal, Thanjai) Dr. RMHRC (Trichy)
have also brought out several inscriptions in print ..

But NONE of these are incorporated in the SCHOOL BOOKS - for History
subject or in BA History or MA History in any of the Universities !

On top, several Colleges have CLOSED History Department ..

One single instance I will tell :

There have been mentions of Udayalur (Near Kumbakonam - Pazhayarai )
inscriptions ::

"Udayar Sri Rajarajadevar Ezhundaruliya mandapthu thirumundril
jeernotharanamapadiyal .. " (As the front yard of a Mandap where
Rajrajadeva Ezhundaruli - was dilapidated......) the persons
renovated it performed VRIDHAM and did so ...
--- This was interpreted as to be a Memorial Temple of Rajaraja!

Businessman- Historian Kudanthai Sethuraman (of Raman & Raman Bus
Services) published a paper on this during `1980s..

Kudavoil Dr. Balasubramanian wrote on this subsequently ..

Ezhuthu Chithar Balakumaran followed the same line later ..

Another Writer (from Kumbknm) published a paper on the same line ...

Collector of Thanjavur enquired Dr, Nagaswamy during 1997 Thanjavur
Big Temple Kumbabishekham ...

In varalaaru.com first issue this subject figured ...

The impression gained is about 30 - 40 years back, some freak has
reported an obsolate inscription on a village temple pillar, over
which Historians around Kumbakonam and Thanjavur worked and put foward
views " ayes" and "nos" ..!

Believe it - THIS IS REPORTED IN THE ARCHEOLOGICAL VOLUMES WAY BACK IN
1927 !

And it has taken about 50/60 years to DRAW the attention of Historians
of THAT AREA and another 20 / 30 years for Public views and Privately
the rightness of these details need to be worked into ...

WHEN DO YOU THINK THIS INFOMATION "YES" OR "NO" WILL REACH THE SCHOOL
BOOK ?!

We must definitely sustain our Motherland feeling (South Indian) -
culturally bonded - Tamil / Telugu / Keralite and Kannada and then
Indian feeling and then can call the whole World as our Brothers & Sisters .. No issue.

This is what Jews / Koreans / Japanese / Chinese - our Asian allies
are doing !

thanks & regards / sps

History is now non-subject for students: -
When I had the opportunity to talk about this subject with Dr. KA PA Aravanan, the ex-v.c of Manonmaniam Sundaranar University when he came here for our centenary celebrations, he regretted that History subject meant only for passing out graduation -

But the possible factor is being developed in our country in another way of history reading - that is updating general knowledge through various methods of Quiz, etc. and in this media, any people irrespective of their basic qualification, had to come the arena with proper knowledge to face the stiff competition.

School text books (belong to any state government) are not helpful for them but a costly GK books from rich delhi publishers gives them the guidence by giving eloborate details on our ancient history. This is not a joke, I myself surprised to find a detailed version of big temple from one such book my daughter was going through.

But there are always difference between school text books reading and taking a immediate knowledge source for competitive exams. Surely, as SPS pointed out, text books gives immense benefits of deeper knowledge of historical incidents for long time where as these costly books satisfy us for immediate benefits. After passing out competitive exams, the next day the student will surely forget what he has written about big temple.

The political and present educational system should come forward to change this type of knowledge phobia. Even now so many people (elders too) talk deeply about shakesphere and other English and American based peots but when it comes to our own Bharati or Kamban they just escape.
Dhivakar
