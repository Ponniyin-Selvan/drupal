A Historical Study - on the years of conquests of countries

listed in the Rajaraja Chola's Meikeerththi

The Chola king Arumolidevan (also known as Arumolivarman) son of Sundera
Cholan and (periya)Vaanavan Mahaadevi ascended the Chola throne in the
year A.D.985 with the crown title - the Rajakesari, after 17 years of
rule of Uththama Cholan.

Note: Sundera Cholan's Queen "Vaanavan Mahaadevi" has been
differentiated in the inscriptions from "Vaanavan Mahaadevi alias
Thiribhuvanamaadevi" the Queen of Rajaraja Chola ? 1, by a
prefix "periya" meaning senior among them.

In my efforts to arrive at an almost accurate - "years of
conquests" of countries by Rajaraja Chola - 1(A.D.985-1014), I have
first made a schedule of inscriptions of Rajaraja sequenced by their

Summary

at the following conclusions in our study of the "years of his
conquest" of other countries mentioned in his Meikeerththi.

Note: Criteria followed in dating the year of each expedition somewhat
accurately, was by deducting one year, from the year each country has
been included in the Meikeerththi of Rajaraja Chola - 1, as the practice
was only after an expedition is fully complete with the forces having
returned to the Chola country, the name of the country was included in
same.

-------------

Rajaraja Chola - 1 commenced his war expeditions with his capture of
Pandiya Nadu, in in south Tamil Nadu, Kandalursaalai and Vilignam in
south Kerala, in the year A.D.987 (985 + [3-1]).

In the year A.D.990 (985 + [6-1]) he captured the regions of
Seetpulinaadu and Paakinaadu in south Andhra Pradesh,

After a lapse of eight years he turned his attention to Vengi Mandalam
in north Andhra Pradesh, and after its capture proceeded to the regions
Gangapaadi, Nulambapaadi and Thadigaipaadi the south, and central
regions of Karanataka, which were all captured in one single expedition
in the year A.D.998 (985 + [14-1]). It appears in this same expedition
king Rajaraja Chola captured also the Tuluva Nadu in the east Karnataka
adjacent to the arabian sea.

Then he turned his attention to Kudamalainaadu in west Kerala, and sent
an expedition in his A.D.999 (985 + [15-1]). It appears it was in this
same expedition he won over the Udagai in the west Tamilnadu bordering
Kerala.

In the early part of the following year in A.D.1000 (985 + [16-1] he
sent an expedition and conquered the Kollam of Kerala. In the second
part of this same year a separate expedition was sent to Kalinga of
Orissa which too was captured. It appears it was in this same expedition
he captured Odda of Orissa and Magadha of Bihar, Vanga & Aratta of West
Bengal, all regions being in the north of the earlier conquered region
of Vengi Mandalam.

In the year A.D.1001 (985 + [17-1] he sent his forces which crossed the
seas and captured the northern half of Elamandalam the Sri Lanka.

In the year A.D.1006 (985 + [22-1] he sent an expedition which captured
Irrattaipaadi Elaraiillakkam encompassing the north Karnataka and south
Maharashtra, and the Sourashtra of north Maharashtra. It appears in this
same expedition Rajaraja Chola captured also the Konkana in the east
Maharashtra adjacent to the arabian sea.

Thereafter for a period of seven years Rajaraja Chola didnot continue
with further expeditions.

However in the year A.D.1013 (985 + [29-1]) his forces again crossed the
seas and captured the Munneer Palanthivu Panneeraayiram the Maldives & Lakshadweep islands.

The End

Dear Sir

As usual, you have painstakingly summarized a complicated chronology
of wars of Rajaraja-I. Excellent work

Few thoughts :
- Rajaraja was almost certainly his abhiseka nama with which he
assumed throne. Not something that came out of other titles. It is not
customary to use other titles as one's name.

- Till his 15th year his meikeerthi was not formatted. Hence he was
mentioned only as "Kanthaloor chalai kalamarutha ko rajakesarivarman".
This was not a part of meikeerthi but just a title he assumed. His
meykeerthi should be considered only with appearance of "Thirumagal
pola".

- Udagai conquest should not be taken as "Kongu" conquest. Both are
different countries. Udagai is Mahodagai and not Udagamandalam as
earlier thought. His Udagai conquest - considered as a pinnacle by
Ottakoothar and Jayamkondar - does not appear in his meykeerthi at all.

- In order to place nolambapadi and gangapadi wars it is better to
considered Ganga and Nolamba inscriptions also.

Summary

at the following conclusions in our study of the "years of his
conquest" of other countries mentioned in his Meikeerththi.

Note: Criteria followed in dating the year of each expedition somewhat
accurately, was by deducting one year, from the year each country has
been included in the Meikeerththi of Rajaraja Chola - 1, as the practice
was only after an expedition is fully complete with the forces having
returned to the Chola country, the name of the country was included in
same.

-------------

Rajaraja Chola - 1 commenced his war expeditions with his capture of
Pandiya Nadu, in in south Tamil Nadu, Kandalursaalai and Vilignam in
south Kerala, in the year A.D.987 (985 + [3-1]).

In the year A.D.990 (985 + [6-1]) he captured the regions of
Seetpulinaadu and Paakinaadu in south Andhra Pradesh,

After a lapse of eight years he turned his attention to Vengi Mandalam
in north Andhra Pradesh, and after its capture proceeded to the regions
Gangapaadi, Nulambapaadi and Thadigaipaadi the south, and central
regions of Karanataka, which were all captured in one single expedition
in the year A.D.998 (985 + [14-1]). It appears in this same expedition
king Rajaraja Chola captured also the Tuluva Nadu in the east Karnataka
adjacent to the arabian sea.

Then he turned his attention to Kudamalainaadu in west Kerala, and sent
an expedition in his A.D.999 (985 + [15-1]). It appears it was in this
same expedition he won over the Udagai in the west Tamilnadu bordering
Kerala.

In the early part of the following year in A.D.1000 (985 + [16-1] he
sent an expedition and conquered the Kollam of Kerala. In the second
part of this same year a separate expedition was sent to Kalinga of
Orissa which too was captured. It appears it was in this same expedition
he captured Odda of Orissa and Magadha of Bihar, Vanga & Aratta of West
Bengal, all regions being in the north of the earlier conquered region
of Vengi Mandalam.

In the year A.D.1001 (985 + [17-1] he sent his forces which crossed the
seas and captured the northern half of Elamandalam the Sri Lanka.

In the year A.D.1006 (985 + [22-1] he sent an expedition which captured
Irrattaipaadi Elaraiillakkam encompassing the north Karnataka and south
Maharashtra, and the Sourashtra of north Maharashtra. It appears in this
same expedition Rajaraja Chola captured also the Konkana in the east
Maharashtra adjacent to the arabian sea.

Thereafter for a period of seven years Rajaraja Chola didnot continue
with further expeditions.

However in the year A.D.1013 (985 + [29-1]) his forces again crossed the
seas and captured the Munneer Palanthivu Panneeraayiram the Maldives & Lakshadweep islands.
