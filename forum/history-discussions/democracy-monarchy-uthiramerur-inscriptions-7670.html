To answer at least one question, Gandhi was never given the Peace Prize
because he was scheduled to get it in 1948, but he was shot before it couldbe
awarded. There's a condition in Alfred Nobel's will that says that only living
persons should be awarded the prize. The 1948 prize was _not_ awarded because
"there was no suitable living candidate". They definitely had decided to give
him the prize (which they'd failed to do until then), but they tried to make
up for it.
(http://nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/peace/articles/gandhi/index.html for a
citation)...

With regards to science and technology, this is definitely a field in whichwe
lag today. Most (not all, obviously) of our scientific "achievements" over the
last few decades seem to be aping the results of others, not original work.
This is mostly due to lack of funding for research. We lack good courses in
either pure or applied sciences at the university level (as opposed to
engineering and technology courses). Arts and science colleges are considered
a step (or three) below "good" engineering colleges, with diluted syllabus and
lackluster staff. I don't expect any great leaps and bounds from this system
as it stands...

Regards,
Shashwath

(Kind Attention :: Shoba - Narendran - Satheesh .. )

Cultivable land was scarce in Chola empire during RRC / RjC. (Means optimum utility carriedout. Rice export should have been peak) Reclamation of land from Cuvery bed was regular practice (ref: Pazhuvur - near Gunaseelam /trichy inscriptions too).. such reclaimed land was PUT ON AUCTION.. by theEstablishment ..
NOT JUST ACQUIRED FOR PUBLIC PURPOSE !

And the highest bidders were ::

Kundavai Vandiyathevan - sister of the Emperor.
Olagamadevi Rajarajan - Crown Empress of the Emperor.
