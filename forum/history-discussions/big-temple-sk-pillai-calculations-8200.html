Briefly, if we have years, given in some common era like Saka or Kali Yuga, we
can figure out when it was - Saka refers to the Shalivahana era, which is 78
CE, and Kali Yuga is 3101 (or 3102) BCE. The problem is, our ancestors weren't
so helpful as that - maybe they wanted to leave us with some mystery to enrich
our lives... Typically, Chola inscriptions don't carry a date from a common
era, and instead, they are dated within the king's reign.

To further confuse matters, there's the usual flip-flop title of
Parakesarivarman / Rajakesarivarman, and quite a few inscriptions carry these
and no other title. So, when we read an inscription that says "In the fourth
year of the reign of Parakesarivarman...", we are left wondering which
Parakesarivarman it refers to.

Sometimes however, these inscriptions (especially more important ones) also
come with a few dozen pieces of astrological information (naal, tithi,
nakshatram, and most usefully, eclipses), which we can use to date an event.
Certain combinations only occur in certain years, and with even the most basic
software of today, it's possible to go back in time and figure out when that
configuration of stars, planets and others occurred.

Also, if inscriptions refer to other events, which have been dated, and
especially when they've been dated from other places (like say, a conquest of
Vengi mentioned in records from the North), we can correlate and say when that
was.

Ultimately, the whole thing is an educated guessing game. Maybe if the rest of
those thousands of inscriptions that SPS and others mention every so often are
translated and published, we can learn more. Maybe some information is totally
lost to history - some of it in our own time, thanks to horrible temple
"renovation" that our temple administrations undertake...

One ultimate source of these calculations is the Surya Siddantha, which is
probably the most important root source from which our Panchangams are
calculated. If we look at it as a scientific rather than mystical and
astrological text, we could probably understand a whole lot more.
