Thiru,

Thanks a lot for taking this out. In fact I was so dejected at the condition of the Devanagari Inscription which, according to Prof. Swaminathan,probably could be the first ever Devanagari Inscription of India. Similarly the Inscriptions in Dharmaraja Mandapam and Ramanuja Mandapam are almost eroded away. Are these not the testimonies to the grand past of our Tamizh country. Why could we not preservethese.

I am pretty sure Awareness is the only problem. I am sure even most of the natives of Mallai do not care for it. Well even I did not realize that until I got initiated to these by our Vijay and then by Prof. Swaminathan.So creating awareness is the need of the time. 

Request members to pool in their ideas as to how do create and sustainthis awareness. 
 
Regards,
Venkatesh

The trip was really excellent. Got a lot of insights in to the wonders of Mamallapuram. But as aptly said by Prof. Swaminathan, we had not even completed 40% of Mallai in this trip. So there should be a sequel to this in the near future.
 
Prof. Swaminathan' s guidance and impressive way of presenting the works, added more flair to the entire day. Believe me, in the morning session, we were spell bound for 3 hrs when he took us around starting from the mini elephant bas  to Trimurthy Caves, to Durga Cave, to Koneri Mandapam, the Varaha Cave, Ganesa Ratham, the Citadel on the top, Ramanuja Mandapam (a complete Misnomer) and Dharmaraja Mandapam.
 
Though considered as a protected site, the negligence that we saw yesterday, on the preservation of important inscriptions in the Ramanuja Mandapam and Dharmaraja Mandapam are really heart-breaking.
 
One question, very aptly asked by Prof. Swaminathan at the end of the day is, "WHAT IS THE STEP FORWARD AFTER THIS INDUCTION". We need to do some thing about all these and I plan to discuss more on this in the upcoming days.
