Dear Sampath
Thank you for starting a wonderful thread and respecting the wishes

We all read in PS and many historical novels that queens like
kundavai married commoners by loving.My doubt is whether it would

have been possible.I thought that they never had any sort of movement
outside the palace or access to commoners.They must have only met the
princes who come in as guests and must have fallen in love.

Kundavai married Vandhiyadevan its a historic fact..He was a prince
and may be a pauper....

Kalkis potrayal of vaname koorai and bhoomiye veedu might be his
romanticised version....

Historically I have seldom heard of a princess marrying a commoner
or even falling in love.Prithviraj samyuktha story is love story,but

even there she met him when her father and prithviraj were friends.
except small kurunila mannars this princess,commoner love story
seldom happened i guess.

Secondly in atleast Chola Kimgdom the women were involved in many
social events including charity and building temples and athura
saalais hospitals Thats well recorded in history...

I understand only the Pandiya dynasty had the ruling rights for the
females as well I may be wrong read it somewhere...

As for a historic novels are concerned they always use a person of
lesser prominence of history as the hero with the main historic
figure more n the background because the author can then use his
imaginations widely...

Examples are countless

Vandiyadevan RR Chola PS
Paranchothi Mammallar SS
Vikraman Mammallar PK
Ilanchezian Karikalam Yavanarani
Ilaya Pallavan Kulothungan Kadalpura
Vijayakumaran Clive Rajaperigai

the list is countless
