Dear Venkateswaran,

On behalf of our Group, a copy of the Magudagamam book was handed
over to Dr. Kudavoil Balu and also Dr. Kalaikkovan. Gokul from
Singapore and Kamal from Japan coordinated on this.

Dr. Kudavoil has confirmed the following :

1. Pondichery Institute of Indology has published Magudagamam with
30 photographs; 2. Saraswathi Mahal Catelogue Vol XVIII - no: 15269 is related to
PPS Sastri, Saraswathi Mahal Library - it is seen that Dr.
A.C.Burnell's Catelogue No: 11428 - paper 17.5" x 1" - 87 leaves - 4
to 5 lines each page - in about 2000 Grantha - beginning
with "Skanthaya namaha .. " is bearing the remarks "QUOTE" : "The
manuscript is in a fairly good condition. Dr. Burnell thinks that
this work deal with ritual to be observed in the big temple at
Tanjore ". UNQUOTE.
4. Dr. R.Nagaswamy in his Article " Iconography and Significance of
the Brahadeswara Temple, Thanjavur " (University of Pennsylvania,
Philadelphia) has made the follg observations (QUOTE - summarised)
i) Srikantha - Dakshinameru - Mahameru :: Are those of mounts
Meru - Mandara - Kailasa. Rajasimha Pallava erected Kailasanatha
Temple of Kanchi liken to the Kailasa Mountain. The term
Dakshinameru is significant - apart from being in Southern India,
the Philosophy followed was that of Dakshina School and not of the
Vama School.
ii) MAKUTAGAMA : Saiva Doctrine according to Makudagama is divided
into Saiva - Pasupata, Soma and Lakula. ... (xxxxx )
iii) The same Magutakama enumerates the Siva "sristi" as follows :

In the middle of Bindu is Nada comes Dhvani. In the middle of Dhvani
is Santa. That which is beyond Santa is Paratpara who is NATESVARA.
THIS SANTYATITA, the beloved of Amba, is Dakshineswara, who resides
in Sabha, for the protection of the Universe, performing constantly
the cosmic dance : "palanartham .... xxxx .... paramakaranam "

iv) Concept of Panchadehamurthy .. then Nrittesvara @ Adalvallan !
v) Makudagama graphically sums up this concept of " Navatattva"
worshipped in the followinbg order from bottom to top:
Brahma - Vishnu - Rudra - Maheswara - bindu - Nadha - Sakthi - Siva.
Finally with flower in hand, the priest invokes Tandavesvara !
" Brahmatattvam adhonysasya ... 14th line pushpahastaih sukhasana - "
vi) Tandavamurthy worship as follows :
" Brahma Visnusca ... 9 lines - navatathvamayam sthanam
sarvasampatkaram -param - Ityadi Makuta .. "

Dr. R.Nagaswamy opines :

QUOTE : It is there that the Thandavamurthi is manifest; his space is the Urdhvasiras where the cosmic dance takes place; It is therefore appropriate that the 108 nritta Karanas are depicted
around the wall of the first floor of Thanjavur temple " (Unquote)

Dr. Kalaikkovan thanked our Group for envisaging interest and will
persue the data ..

Dear SPS uncle, Sorry to differ also from you. The temple is not named after the King. The King was named after the Rajarajeswaram Temple.
     The Big Temple has the Maha Meru on its top.There are 8 important gods standing on it looking on 8 directions. They are  Anandhan, Sukchuman, Sivothaman, Ekanethran, Ekaruthran, Thrimurthy, Seekandan, Sikandi and these gods assist the Lord Sivam to control and operate the Ichai, Kiriyai and Gnanam sakthis of the universe. They are called  Rajarajeswarars. Rajarjeswara  is the nameof the post given bySivam for his assistants. He orders and control them.  That is why the temple is called Rajarajeswaram and Arulmozhi Varman was named Rajarajacholan.
     "Sree Rajarajechurm udayar Sree Vimanam Pon meivithan Sree Rajaraja Devar" was the sentence inscribed in the Gopuram inside wall which was found by Dr.K.Nagasamy and Kudavasal Uncle around 1997 e the Kerala temple also named like this.
