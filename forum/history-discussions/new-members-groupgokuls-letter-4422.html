--- In ponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, SARAVANAN APS

Dear Saravanan,

Except Nanian's GNANA BHOOMI there were not exclusive magazines for
devotional details in those days (say 15 years back).

Vikadan and Kalki accommodated about couple of pages each week for
this purpose.

Bharaneedharan wrote in Vikadan. He wrote on AMMA - Saradhamani
Devi w/o Ramakrishna Paramahamsa .. And on several temples .. That
was excellent forerunner for today's Sakthi Vikadan, Kumudham
Bakthi, Amman Darsanam etc..

Kalki published Mahaperiyava articles.. I recall them being very
interesting and informative. to cite a few :

1. Siva, in the heart of Rangaraja (Srirangam), moving (dancing) at
par with inhaling - exhaling - is known as AJABA Nadanam - which
Vidangan is in Thiruvarur and comparisons between Rangaraja and
Nataraja !

2. Pallikonda Siva in Suruttapalli;

3. Maragatha lingam near Thirukkazhukundram

4. Saptha vidanga kshethrams etc., etc

In our Group, Venkat and Mangal Ganesh have covered most of the
Thevara Paadal petra sthalanagal and a couple of others have covered
Divya desams. I have covered several Thevara - Divyadesa
sthalangal in Thondai and Chozha naadu !

Thank you Saravanan for responding.

regards / sps

Dear Vaidyanathan,

"Deyvathin Kural" - 7 Volumes ..
and his works by Vanathi Padippagam are available..

Bharaneedaran's works are also impressive.

Are you talking about the series that appeared in
Ananda Vikadan?

That was wonder full. It was about many small /
unknown temples in Tamil Nadu, with a lot black and
white pictures. Talking about these articles,it takes
me back to the good old days. I too (I am sure many
more of us) wanted to go about all these temples on
foot, singing Devaram and Thiruvasagam, enjoying the
air, atmosphere. But practically we are pressed with
mundane daily work. As Lalu Prasad Yadav hopes to be
the PM "...one day... some day...". Let me also hope
"one day ... some day... I will be able to go around
these temple and places.
Lucky - you could do with a two wheeler.

Nice to start the day with good old memories.

Anbudan

Saravanan AP

Dear Vaidyanathan,

Chandrasekarendra Saraswathi Swamingal's writings were published in
serial in Kalki in late 80s and he was narrating almost every temple
from Mayavaram (now Mayiladuthurai) to Thanjavur .. with such finer
details !

I felt the same way to visit each temple after reading an article, when
I was in a Defence Establishment Chennai, to travel by passenger train
with a Jolna bag and get down at each temple and spend time leisurely ..

Almost after 5 years, I quit my job and could do this in my Two wheeler
and spent months in the Chozha heart land wandering ..

Chidambaram - Veeranam (kaatumannargudi) - Kadambur - Thiruvarur to
Kodikkarai ! .. stretched to Thanjai - Pudukottai - Narthamalai -
Kodumbalur - Pillayarpatti - Thirukkottiyur - Sivaganga!

Those interested in this, should definitely have the feel of these
places, breath the .. which can be recalled and cherished for rest of their life.

anbudan / sps

Re Vatapi (presently known as BADAMI), this was the Capital of Chalukyas
from about 540 AD until 757 AD when Rashtrakutas took over. The
surroundings hills are dotted with temples, fortifications, carvings and
inscriptions dating not just from the Chalukyan period, but from other
times when the site was occupied as a fortress. Western Chalukyas (from
Kalyan - present Mumbai Kalyani), Kalachuryas, Yadavas of Devagiri,
Vijayanagar Empire, the Adil Shah kings and Marathas subsequently ruled
this province. There is a 642 AD dated Narasimha I Pallava inscription,
who occupied for about 13 years !

Aihole is about 43 Kms from Badami which was Chalukyan Cpital from 4th
