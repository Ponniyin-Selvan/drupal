One small hint.

The Cholzas were not alone.

The Tamilian kingdoms were also not alone or isolated.

Regards

JayBee

One example - the Cholzas had Chinese connections.

The Chinese junks used to come to the Cholziyan ports. Cholzas
used to send embassies to China.

Raja Raja Cholza was well-known among the ancient Chinese. The
Cholzas were known as Chuu-Li-Yen. Raja Raja himself was known
as Chuu-Li Lot-Chaa Lot-Chaa.

------------

Maah-Maah-Thaah Lot-Cha
Maah-Maah-Thaah Lot-Cha

That would be the ancient Chinese version of

Manmatha Raasaa......
Manmatha Raasaa......

Regards

JayBee

I don't recollect any temple built by

But there were temples in China. One of them was in Canton.

Canton is the southernmost major port in China which was nearest to
the southern coutries, ie., SE Asia and India.

There was lesser royal central government control in Canton.

That temple had an inscription, only a fragment of which is available
now.

It contains Tamil portion which is signed in Chinese as well.

But that inscription is rather of later tims.

Some Tamil Hindu bigwigs and merchants have made a special festival
and worship at that temple for the well-being of the grandson of
Kublai Khan, the Chinese emperor. The date would be around 1300 plus.

Maravarman Kulasekara Pandya was a personal friend of Kublai Khan.
His prime minister Takiuddin Abdur Rahman al-Tibi and his nephew
Jamaluddin who was the minister for trade, had very close contacts
with Kublai Khan.

The next time His Holiness of Kanchi wants to make a trip to such
places, it would be better for HH to pick me up on the way.

After all, I am the only one in the world who edited and published the
only centenary 'malar' for the Paramachariyar SwamigaL before He shed
His material body.
