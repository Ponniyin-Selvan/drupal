Hi members,
This is Chandra (plasticschandra) writing. Althogh there are many
good things happening in this forum, I wonder what are we doing
in rebuilding the lost beauty- the temples of those great rulers?
One after the other, we get daily names and names of temples
which cry for attention.

You might be aware, that Dr.T.Satyamurthy, also a new member
of our forum, had started the REACH foundation (Rural Education
And Conservation of Heritage) recently and we have the trust now.

There are few great landmark temples which we immediately need
to reconstruct:-

1. Manimangalam (near tambaram) is one. There are3 temples, of
Pallavadynasty which were later retouched by Cholas too, which
have some intricate carvings and land mark stone sciptures.

http://www.hinduonnet.com/2003/12/31/stories/2003123107210400.htm

manimangalam.html

temple_cleaners (which has been registered as REACH - under
the guidance of Dr.T.S). the story in the first link appeared in
THE HINDU last Saturday. As myself and other REACH members
were tied up with some other work, member Ram Sunder @Chandru
took the initiative of visiting the place, documenting the photos,
details,legends and visiting local people to gain support to work
along with REACH for Uzhavaarappani and further reconstrucion
work! We estimate a figure of around 20 lakhs.

2. Uthiramerur (nr Kanchi) which also has one of the best and
stylisshly bult Kailasanathar temple which may ask for another 25
lakhs.

how may are going to be added. let's restrict to chola temples alone.

in the album i have added the photos of a late chola apsidal
temple - so many of them are in Tondaimandalam. the temple is at

Sorpanacheri (?) between Padappai and Walajabad, just 100m from the
road. It belongs to Vikrama Chola period (inf from Dr.TS / Rajavelu.
and another on the near Palaya sivaram on Kpuram-Chengai road. a
saptamatrika temple with pallava period matrikas.

it seeems that Dr TS took some initiative by advising the locals
to do something. thye promptly provided a asbestos roof over the linga.
if a restoration is attempted the temple can be completed in full
as all the stone members are available in situ.
but how?
the Dharmasthala trust by Vereendra Hegade in Karnataka is running
a organisation which is renovating dilapidated temples intially on

50/50 financing pattern, they did the work as per the norms laid out
by asi with the help of many retired asi officers chipping in with
voluntary service.Dharmasthala trust took care of their
travel/hospitality.

the programme was so dedicated that their work became extremely
popular, the karnataka govt now sharing the 40/50 ofpublic portion.
so much so that the quality of restoration is so good that they
are being askled by the government to repair the "protected temple

monumnets".

their simple work can be seen at www.sdmdharmothanatrust.org. it
gives a very simple account. but having seen and interacted with the

organisers and advisors, their work is hoghly commendable. they must
have restormed many ornate hoysala monumnets without bowing to modern
repair practices of adding unatural things to the old temple.

how is it possible? SHEAR DEDICATION AND GOOD SUPPLY OF MONEY AND
A LARGE HEART OF VEREENDRA HEGADE, A JAIN BUT MAINTAINING A

SIVAKSHETRA. HE NEVER ASKED WHY ARE YOU CONSERVING THIS OR THAT
TEMPLE. IF THE COMMITTE APPROVES HE SAYS YES.

IS THIS POSSIBLE IN TAMIL NADU? WE HAVE MANY DONORS. BUT THE
PROBLEM LIES IN THE FACT THEY GENERALLY REPEAT GENERALLY EXPECT

MILAGE/VISIBILITY. SO THEY PREFER A NEW TEMPLE RATHER THAN RESTORING
AN OLD ONE AS PER ORIGINAL. EVEN IF THEY REPAIR AN OLD ONE THEY FORCE
TO ADD MODERN ENTITIES TO THE OLD. IF HALF A DOZEN PERSON COMES OUT
WITH THE CONVICTION OF HEGGADE THEN THE SCENARIO WILL CHANGE DRASTICALLY.

HOW TO CHANGE THIS MIND SET? COME TO OUR EXPERIENCE. WHEN WE -ASI-
RESTORES A MONUMENT THE TEAM TAKES THE IMMEDIATE PLEASURE OF

ACIEVEMENT. IT IS A SIMPLE PSYCOLOGICAL THING. JUST THE CHILD IN OUR
MIND. IF THE DONORS ARE INCULCATED TO ENJOY THE PLEASURE ACHIEVEING
SOMETHING THAT OTHERS CAN NOT DO, THEY WILL COME FORWARD.

Secondly, we too must be professional like the active members of
the trust. dedicated and focussed. the money is there. the mind is

waiting to be "brainwashed". approach with dedication and focus. we
will succeed. even if we succeed in one case be happy. try next.

regards.
