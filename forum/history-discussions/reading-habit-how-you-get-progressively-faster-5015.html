The reading habit - how you get progressively faster

I am reminded of an anecdote of Dr. Issac Asimov, the well known science
fiction writer.

He was a refugee from Russia. His mother, enrolled for a course in
English after he became famous. She was a bright student and learnt
fast. Her teacher came to know that she was the mother of Asimov, and
commented: "No wonder, you are intelligent and learn fast as you are the
mother of Asimov." The lady was offended and retorted, "No wonder,
Asimov is intelligent and popular. He is my son." The genes flow from
parents to children.

Asimov had published his first novel when he was a student. During his
Ph. D. viva, the panel spent only ten minutes on his thesis, and spent
over 40 minutes on his science fiction novels. His apprehension, fueled
by his friends and colleagues, that the panel might doubt his research
credentials (because of science fiction mentality) was cleared in the
first fifteen minutes.

Sampath
