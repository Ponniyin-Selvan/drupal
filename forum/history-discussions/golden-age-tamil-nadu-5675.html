Dear SPS,

In a recent post, you have said that the ruling period of Rajaraja
Chola and his son Rajendra (985 CE to 1044) was said to be a golden

period.

In general to define a golden period, what are the credential
needed? The Gupta's period during Chandragupta-1 - Samudhra Gupta -

& Vikramathiya, was already mentioned in history books of India
as 'Golden Age of India'. So many sanscritic classics works were
born during that period including Kalidasa.

Rajaraja's rule was remarkably well during that period except some
warring times. The wars also were won, and the people said to be

living in happy, so that the King was having enough time in
spending his concentration on Big Temple and other connected works,
apart from the other administrative handlings. The period was
generally calm. The King brought out a democratic ruling culture in
village level. The able administration was another crediteria so
that to keep federal cheif's under the King's control. Most
importantly nature also have co-operated so that not many
calamities.

But this situation was extended till Kulottungan-2 and even his
son that was upto 1185 CE.

So can we say the years of 985 to 1185 CE or upto 1200 CE was the
Golden Age of

Tamilnadu? Like north India, the tamil classical works like
collections of Nambi-andar-Nambi, Periyapuranam, Kalingattu Parani

and Kambaramayanam were born in those periods. Scores of Temples
were built and art works displayed all over the state. Except some
small times during Vikraman ruling there were not much natural
calamities. Almost wars ended with Northern neighbores.

These 200 years, the whole Tamilnadu and Kerala as well of
eastern part of present Karnataka and Southern Andhra (almost like

British Madras State during Pre-independent India,) was under the
complete control of Cholas. Most importantly except some warring
times, the peace prevailed almost in all the part of this particular
chola country.

Great Philosophers like Ramanuja, Meykantar were born in this
period. And intellectual think-tank was highly seen as satisfactory

in this period. Naval strength was commandable. Sea pirates were not
seen in the eastern sea, so the merchandism was well handled at
ports. Economically most of 200 years the chola country was in
strongest terms of wealth management..

I have taken only positive happenings for defining the Golden Age.
You can add more inputs.

Dhivakar.
