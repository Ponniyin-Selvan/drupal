-Hi all

just a few words on the uthiramerur trip.
after a delightful breakfast at vandalur we crossed chingel pet.
on the way we tried to cajole sps into stopping at kolavai lake so
that we could climb the hills in the morning and the chola temple on
top.

however sps was firm that we see the temple on the return. he was
right . we would have missed some temple gurukals who had the keys if
we had been late to uthiramerur.

thiru puli vanam
but the trip to uthiramerur was indeed delayed because sriraman
suggested seeing the thiru puli vanam temple . we took a detour and a
whole some shiva temple with pond, 100 pillar mandapam designed as a
charriot on the outskirts. the temple was fabulous. wonderful
sculptures adorned the gajaprashta temple. on the outer wall were the
remnants of some age old paintings. a couple of faces were visible
and great

the speciality of the temple was that dakshinamurthy had a lion at
the feet.My memories went to malapadi where dakshinamurthy sits on a
bull

but overall there was some confusion in the temple over the ages.
the vishnu durgai looked very much like mahalakshmi and the pillaiyar
sat on an auvudiayar.

in one of the pillars is an engraving of a tiger clawing a linga. the
gurukkal showed us a claw mark on the lingam too. its a legend of
this temple.

the temple has been mentioned by appar peruman in another thevaram
.so it counts as a vaipu thalam.
but I am sure the holy trio must have visited this temple as it is in
the cross roads of other devara sthalams.
kanchi- thiru magaral- thiru idaicuram- thiru kalu kundram.

venketesh
