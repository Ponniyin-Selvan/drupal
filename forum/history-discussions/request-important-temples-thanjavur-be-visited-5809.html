hi all;
Just returned from a tour of some Shiva temples in
the Kumbakonam
Tanjore
belt. It too 4 days but was worth every minute.
Went to some really
remote
one.
Here's the list:

- *thingalur*-- moon temple
- *swami malai* -- murugan temple
- *thiruvazhansuzhi *-- vinayagar temple
- *patteeswaram* -- "durgai" temple
- *tharasuram* -- siva temple--UNESCO's done a
really classy job
with
this one.
- *thiruvarur*-- siva temple--incredible
spiritual experience
inspite
of not being a spiritual

person
myself. Only siva temple with the Lord in human
form,
why? the explanation given by the
priest was vague.
- *RRC samadhi,Udayalur*
- *thiruvarur* -- durgai temple for astham and
chitrai star
- *alangudi-*- guru temple
- *suriyanar* --sun temple
- *thirunageswaram*-- ragu temple
- *thirupuvanam* -- sarabeswarar temple-nice
temple.the inner
sanctum's pillars were more like the
Pallava,Mughal type.
Pandya,Chola
pillars are of rectangular cross section but
these were more
artistic. hope
some of our group can throw more light into
this.
- *uppiliappan* -- vishu temple
- *kanjanur*-- sukkiran temple
- *ayyavadi* -- prathyankara devi temple--This
goddess is said
to have
been worshipped by the Cholas before going to
war. Also
Agasthiyar prayed
here. Any more details?
- *Gangai konda cholapuram*-- siva temple
- *thirunallar*-- sani temple
- *keeghaperumpallam *-- budan temple
- *thirukadaiyur temple*-- abirami temple
- *thirumayanam temple*-- siva temple--This was
an incredible
experience. A must for people visiting that
side. A single
person is
maintaining this temple. This place was visited
by our group
sometime back
and i made it a point to visit this one after
reading about it
here. Thanks
to this group.
- *vaitheeswaran* -- chevvai & sivan temple
- *seerkhazhi *-- sattanathar & siva temple
- *chidambaram* -- siva temple--Surely the most
beautiful temple
of
the lot. The spadika linga pooja was wonderful.
The pillar
corners and
sanctum corners are so precise that it is
incredible to believe
that they
did thismanually with a chisel and hammer. We
are struggling for
simpler
designs with modern machinery. Incredible!
- *nachiar temple*-- vishnu and kal karudan
temple
- *tanjore* -- siva temple

Thiruvidaimaruthur!
@@@@ MAHALINGAM surrounded by 9 Parivara temples
for which Vinayaga
is Thiruvalanchuzhi - Muruga Swamimalai and Durgai -
Patteswaram !
===================

SOMASKANDA OF THIRUVARUR IS THE PARIVARA STHALAM FOR
THIRUVIDAI
MARUTHUR MAHALINGAM !
These figure within the 7 (SAPTHA) Vidanga Sthalams.
Dangam : Uli. Vidangam : Without using Uli. Created
by Mystic effect
(of Viswakarma). Musukundan, chola King (this is the
earliest
reference !)with his Capital in Thiruvarur, helped
Indira in
Devasura War in obtaining AMRITH and Indira wanted
to reward him.
Said to chose The Best, except Indirani, Indira
pathavi and
Airavatham ! Musukundhan wanted the LINGAM
worshipped by Indira to
establish in Thiruvarur. Taken aback (for not
including this in his
priority list !) Indira approached Viswakarma who
created similar
Lingam, which Indira gave to Musukundan. The Lingam
told the truth
and it was established in nearby place. Like wise
six were
established in Thirunallar, Thirukkaravasan,
Thiruvelvikudi,
Mayavaram etc., known as SAPTHA VIDANGA. In all
these temples, there
are (were) MARAGATHA LINGAMs (which is supposedly
created by
Viswakarma). In all these temples Navagrahas will be
standing
opposite to Siva (on the Eastern end, facing Siva,
in row, one next
to other).
The Seventh time, the ORIGINAL lingam worshipped by
Indira has come
down to earth on its own and established in
Thiruvarur.
Hence This Lingam is having WINGS !
Here the THIAGARAJAR is SOMASKANDAR !
and unfortunately, the Priests fail to impress upon
these details..

Hatageswararm - Place for performing Yoga; Erisinakotravai - Established by Rajendra
Kamambika - Muthuswamy Dikshidhar's Navabarana
Keerthanas
Aazhi Theru
Sibi - Chariot.

known as AJABA

Thiruvidaimaruthur!
@@@@ MAHALINGAM surrounded by 9 Parivara temples for which Vinayaga
is Thiruvalanchuzhi - Muruga Swamimalai and Durgai - Patteswaram !
===================

SOMASKANDA OF THIRUVARUR IS THE PARIVARA STHALAM FOR THIRUVIDAI
MARUTHUR MAHALINGAM !
These figure within the 7 (SAPTHA) Vidanga Sthalams.
Dangam : Uli. Vidangam : Without using Uli. Created by Mystic effect
(of Viswakarma). Musukundan, chola King (this is the earliest
reference !)with his Capital in Thiruvarur, helped Indira in
Devasura War in obtaining AMRITH and Indira wanted to reward him.
Said to chose The Best, except Indirani, Indira pathavi and
Airavatham ! Musukundhan wanted the LINGAM worshipped by Indira to
establish in Thiruvarur. Taken aback (for not including this in his
priority list !) Indira approached Viswakarma who created similar
Lingam, which Indira gave to Musukundan. The Lingam told the truth
and it was established in nearby place. Like wise six were
established in Thirunallar, Thirukkaravasan, Thiruvelvikudi,
Mayavaram etc., known as SAPTHA VIDANGA. In all these temples, there
are (were) MARAGATHA LINGAMs (which is supposedly created by
Viswakarma). In all these temples Navagrahas will be standing
opposite to Siva (on the Eastern end, facing Siva, in row, one next
to other).
The Seventh time, the ORIGINAL lingam worshipped by Indira has come
down to earth on its own and established in Thiruvarur.
Hence This Lingam is having WINGS !
Here the THIAGARAJAR is SOMASKANDAR !
and unfortunately, the Priests fail to impress upon these details..
Thivarur Temple has certain very special details :
Hatageswararm - Place for performing Yoga; Erisinakotravai - Established by Rajendra
Kamambika - Muthuswamy Dikshidhar's Navabarana Keerthanas
Aazhi Theru
Sibi - Chariot.
Siva's dance in Thiruvarur is also special. It is known as AJABA
NADANAM ! since Lord Vishnu also worships siva, while sleeping, the
movement of Siva - over the chest of sleeping Lord Vishnu - is
involuntary (Jaba: With dedication; ajaba - involuntary) and thus
AJABA.
This picture of Nataraja over sleeping Vishnu - is depicted in the
Temple while coming out of Thiyagarajan Sannidhi and turning right
(North-wards) to move around the sanctum !
