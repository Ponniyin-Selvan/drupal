It is interesting to read the details of inscription 529 of VOL. VIII
related to Kothandrarameswaram / Thondamanatrur (near Kalahasti / AP -
Pallippadai temple of Aditya I ::

The pallippadai temple was maintained by priest Vagisvara Pandita
Bhattarar, who conducted festivals in which Mahavrathis, along with
those practising Ganapthyam, Kaumaram, Vaishnavam, Saumaram,
Saktham,and Saivam also took part.

Vishnu Smriti enjoins austere life on the Mahavratins. They should
reside in simple Huts, perform prayers 3 times a day and should
survive by begging and collecting alms, move from one village to
other- writes DN Lorenzen,

MAHAVRITHAM means a great VOW and the greatness consisted in the
extraordinary nature, such as eating food placed in human skull,
besmering the body with the ashes of carcasses, writes Dr. M.
Rajamanickam in his book The Development of Saivism in south India
under the Pallvas of Kanchi and Imperial chozhas.

Pallippadai temples were dedicated to siva and they were controlled
by Mahavrathins. Pallimadai belonging to VIRAPANDIYA (our Aditha
Karikala fame ! 939 - 958 AD) speaks of a gift of sheep for a lamp in
the matha of MAHAVATHIGAL attached to the " Srimahavradhigal
madthinukku."

ThiruvotriyurChaturanana Panditha was an intimate friend of Prince of
Rajaditya, but could not die with him in Thakolam battle. Chaturana,
Mahavrathin, became head of the local Mada and installed Bhairva !
Inscription 162 ARE 1912 corroborates with Thevaram hymns that
Nandhivarman III fed Mahavradins here.

SII vol V - 578 in Thirukandiyur talks of " Acharyabhoga " to
Pasuptha Teacher LAGULEESA PANDITHAR by Rajendra I.

Interestingly, Thirukandiyur temple does comprise of pillars
mentioning of Sundara Chozha / Vaanavanmadeveeswaram !

More interestingly LAGULEESA PANDITHAR maintained Panchavanmadevi
Pallippadai and UDAYALUR SIVA TEMPLE also according to Kudanthai
Sethuraman.

Udayalur inscriptions needs closer study by the revered Scholars.

Dear Venkat and Vijay,

Spoke to Dr. Kalaikkovan related to his new finds.

Dr. Kalaikkovan and Dr. Nalini have been working on this alomost
for

about a year and have taken efforts and time to assemble the

fragments to read and understand their nature.

As we are aware, the VARALAARU Inscription publication is brought

out by the Dr. Rajamanickanar Research Centre, Trichy, once

necessary number of inscriptions are found, read and compiled.

Tne next issue is due for publication and hence the finds are
being

discussed openly and widely.

Re Udayalur inscriptions - certain inscriptions are copied already

by ASI and published.

ALREADY means most of them are found between say 1900 and 1930 or

so - by the British ! and hardly much work is done in our

Independent India !

We heard that there was ASI Ooty (in 1960s ) because it suited to
an

official and that became defunct and moved to ASI (Mysore) later

because that suited to another official... where our inscriptions

are lying for the past several decades !

TN State Archeology under Dr. Nagaswamy, Nadana Kasinathan, Dr.

Sridhar, Dr. Vasantha Kalyani and such officials under the

Chiefministership of Dr. MGR, Dr. JJ and Dr. Kalaignar have also

published inscriptions related to various Districts...

To cite an example, there are about 30 inscriptions were published

from Trivualanchuzhi in 1902 by the ASI... (Vol VIII)

State Archlgy under Dr. Vasantha kalyani brought out in about 1987

an exclusive edition (which I purchased in Rajarajan manimandapam

during our last Thanjai trip 2006 ?) covering about 57
inscriptions.

Dr. Kalaikkovan and Dr. Nalini have found about 20 inscriptions

which were not reported earlier... atleast 3 of them are after

escavation of Kshetrabalar temple there). These are reported in "

Varalaaru " book and also published as a separate book.

Put together, we have more than 107 inscriptions in this temple -

found over a period of 100 years !!

Udayalur - (with RRC Pallippadai speculations ) - does kindle

interest in such Scholars - and their contribution in the
direction

of finding more details is very much commendable.

regards/ sps
