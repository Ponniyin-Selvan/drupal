Names of years and months.

All Tamil years' names are in Sanskrit. The basis for
this can be derived from a Narathar-Vishnu
mythological story. In my opinion these names and the
story does not serve anything thing good to society.
thing to trace. But these are the only points said in
support of changing the new year start from
Ciththirai. Much importance to these names are given
just to change the new year start in a hurried manner.
But there are lot other issues to be considered.

All thamiz month names Chiththirai to Panguni are
Tamil words only. These names are used in Sangam
literature and Tholkaappiyam. The meaning of these
names are simple. The month name represents the name
of the star appears closer to the moon on the full
moon day of the month. For example Chiththirai on
Chiththirai and Visaakam on Vaikaasi and so on. All
Hindu festivals come on days based on celestial events
only. It may be a particular phase of moon (like
amaavasyai, thiruthiyai, chathurththi,etc.,) or may be
a star ( like Thiruvaathirai, Avittam, etc.,) or may
be sun's movement (like Chiththirai first and Thai
first) . Here comes a pattern of following both sun
movement and moon's movement by our ancestors.

In India there are many regional calenders are used.
They are based on lunar or solar movements. Tamil
calender is based on solar movent system. Every month
represents the zodiac of sun's location. The names of
zodiacs mesham to meenam is the name of the month in
many Indian calenders. Tamilnadu govt also suggests
this to be followed. Govt specified the list of month
names as suravam, etc., are Tamil words for the zodiac
signs. Here is an intersting point as to why ancient
Tamils named the months based on moon though they
followed a solar based calender. This suggests that in
the era much before Tholkaappiyam Tamils followed some
calender based on moon.

But I believe there is a good sense to follow the
existing Tamil month names even today.
