thanks sps sir for making us take note of this inscription. i am no
epigraphist so i turned to dr rajavelu. while he confirmed the
authenticity of the information that the epigraph belong to casted his doubts on the regnal year.

1. they have used numerals instead of words. therefore, the engraver
might have committed a mistake as the tamil numeral for 2 and 3 are more
or similar.

2. Tanjavur ends with 29th year. therefore the same was taken to be thelast
regnal year.

3, it is common fact that the regions located far away from the capitral
contiunue to use the regnal years of a king in spite of the fact he died
long back. for example the long regnal years for the pallava kings in
the chengal nadukarkkal.

4. this case being in the heartland sems to be a aberation. hence given
no importance by the historian.

5. the record is incomplete shows the unprofessional, and possibly the
no-backing of any royal authority.

6, rajavelu will come back with more concrete information. i hope.

sriraman
