A "follow-up-note" on the Historical Study - on the years of
conquests of countries, listed in the Rajaraja Chola's Meikeerththi

In my efforts to arrive at an almost accurate- "years of
conquests" of countries by RajarajaChola - 1(A.D.985-1014), I have
first made a Schedule of Inscriptions of Rajaraja - sequenced by their
respective years, for our study. Also I have provided a Summary on the

deductions made fromthe contents of these Inscriptions.
Rajaraja's expedition around Kalinga, and around Irattaipaadi are
provided below with further explanation.

Inscriptions

27 of 1906 from Thirukkadaiyur SII - XXII - 27

Meikeerththi includes - Kollam, Kalingam
- 16th year

Summary

In the early part of the following year in A.D.1000 (985 + [16-1] he
sent an expedition and conquered the Kollam of Kerala. In the second
part of this same year aseparate expedition was sent to Kalinga of
Orissa, which too was captured. Itappears it was in this same expedition
hecaptured Odda of Orissa and Magadha of Bihar, Vanga & Aratta of West
Bengal, all regions being in the north of the earlier conquered region
of Vengi Mandalam.

----------------

Fromthe Thiruvaalankadu Copper Plates & anInscription we note Rajaraja
Chola - 1 captured the regions of Irattaipaadi, Konkana, Sourashtra,
Kalingam, Odda, Magadha, Vanga& Aratta among others.

Even though the conquests of the above countries in the north of India
are clearly indicated in the Thiruvaalankadu Copper Plates, many
Historians have not discussed in respect of same in their writings.This
could possibly be in view - of their doubts on the authenticity of
these references to some countries of India, said to have been captured
by Rajaraja Chola - 1.

However we have further evidences toprove "that in fact" these
countries too have been won in warefareby Rajaraja Chola - 1, otherthan
the countries found in his Meikeerththi.

The references to the regions captured by Rajaraja in Karandai Thamil
SangamPlates are given as follows:

"?? Peruvaliyinai selvamaaka kondavanaana SeerRasarasan,
Singala, Pandiya, Keralaarasarkalaiyum, Kongesuvararkalaiyum,
Malavarkalaiyum, Thelungarkalaiyum, Gangarkalaiyum, Kalingarkalaiyum,
Vangarkalaiyum, Magadharkalaiyum, Chalukiya arasarkalaiyum, Kurunaattu
arasarkalaiyum, eanaiya ellaa arasarkalaiyum thoal valiyaal, poril
ventru (won in warfare), avarkaludaiya, yaanaikalaiyum,
kuthiraikalaiyum, naadukalaiyum, irraththinangalaiyum, selvangalaiyum,
kaippatrinaan??."

In the above reference, the countries that have been won in warefare
("poril ventru" in Tamil) by Rajaraja Chola - 1, there are three
new regions mentioned that are not found in the Thiruvaalangkadu Copper
plates. One is the region of the Chalukiya-arasar which we are aware was
the Irrataipaadi Elaraiilakkam of the Meikeerththi,and the other two
being the Malavar and Kurunaatu arasar.

Malavar (Malava) were a dynasty which was in existance in the northof
Maharashtra. It appears that Rajaraja's forces even won over the
Bhojas of Malava. There is a referance in this Karandai Thamil Sangam
Plates which confirms same as"?..(Rajaraja)?.. Bhojadevan
udauya thalaiyai thuniththaan??".

Also a correction has to be made on my previous articlewherin I had
mentioned the south Saurashtra was in north Maharashtra. But in theface
of more evidence it has to be correctly located in Gujarat. The
Kuru-naatu arasar needs further investigation and confirmation on its
precise location.

Now on investigating the contents of the Essaalam Copper Plates we have
the list of countries conquered by Rajaraja Chola - 1stated as follows:

"???Arumolivarman who with his long and beautiful arms (that)
bored the marks of sankhu andchakra in his palms. He conquered the
Gangas, Vangas, Kalingas, Magadhas, Malavas, Simhalas, Andhras, Rattas,
Oddas, Katakas, Keralas, Gaudas and Pandiyas. By the wealth obtained
through his conquests he erected at Thanjapuri a very great temple named
Rajarajaesvarem??."

Odda, and the new region Gauda are mentioned again as regions conquered
by Rajaraja Chola ? 1 in the Esaalam Copper Plates. "Gauda Desa
encompassed parts of West Bengal and Bangladesh".

mentioned in three Copper Plate Grants.Malavas are mentioned in two
Copper Plate Grants

Rattas, Oddas too are mentioned in two Copper Plate Grants and Gauda
only in one Copper Plate Grant.

Also another correction has to be now made on my previous article wherin
I had mentioned the Aratta (as appearing in Thiruvaalankadu Copper
Plates) was in West Bengal along with Vanga. But in the face of more
evidence it has to be taken correctly as the short form of Irattaipaadi.
This same region is mentioned in Essaalam Copper Plates as Rattas and in
Karandai Thamil Sangham Plates as Chalukiyaregion.

Hence we can safely conclude that in addition to the countries found in
Rajaraja'sMeikeerththi, the regions of Magadha, Vanga, Odda, and
Gauda, (inthe north-east India), and Saurashtra, Malava and Konkana(in
the north-west) were conquered by Rajaraja Chola - 1.

Then the question may be asked as to why the above regions are not
included in Rajaraja's Meikeerththi. The only explanation we can
give "for the time being" is as follows:

It could have been a possibillity that during the period of
Rajaraja's invasion of Irattaipaadi ? the king of Irattaipaadi
was already in authority also over Saurashtra, Malava, and Konkana -
being it's neighbouring regions. It appears that these three regions
too came under Cholas automatically when he conquered the
Irattaipaadi,but only the region Irattaipaadi Elarai Illakkam however
was mentioned in the Meikeerththi.

Likewise during the period of Rajaraja'sinvasion of Kalinga - the
king of Kalinga was already in authority also over Magadha, Vanga,
Gauda, and Odda - being its neighbouring regions. It appears that these
four regions too came under Cholas automatically when he conquered the
Kalinga, but onlythe region Kalinga however was mentioned in the
Meikeerththi.

Thisis an inferance only, and an indepth study on same is necessary
along with the study of the History of the above mentioned regions of
this period, to acertain whether this could have been the possible
reason for these regions - of not having been mentioned in the
Rajaraja's Meikeerththi.
