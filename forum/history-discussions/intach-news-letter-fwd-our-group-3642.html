INDIAN NATIONAL TRUST FOR ART AND CULTURAL HERITAGE

INTAChERITAGe-NEWS
July 2006

____________________________________________

Architectural Heritage

JNNURM Conference, Mysore: Following the conference of JNNURM at
Mysore on June 5, 2006, it was suggested that a supplementary toolkit
be prepared for the heritage cities and heritage zones of other
cities selected under Urban Renewal Mission. Several meetings were
held with experts to discuss and prepare this toolkit which was then
submitted to Secretary, Ministry of Urban Development. Presently, a
task force is reviewing the toolkit before it is sent to the local
urban bodies of all cities to incorporate the same in the preparation
of City Development Plan (CDP) and Development Project Reports (DRP).
Bundi Listing: Bundi listing has been initiated by a team of
architects from INTACH. Around 300 buildings have been identified in
the first field trip.
Tourism Circuit Project at Gwalior, Shivpuri and Chanderi, Madhya
Pradesh: A team from INTACH visited Gwalior for a meeting with the
Commissioner, Gwalior to discuss various issues concerning the
project. Work in Moti Mahal has already started. Necessary
permissions are being sought to start the work in other locations.
Community, Craft and Heritage: The Integrated Design and Technology
Development Project is in progress. Four workshops in embroidery
skill up-gradation have been completed and the project is scheduled
to finish in July/August 2006. The design workshop for stone crafts
is in progress.
Report on Kashmir earthquake: A report has been prepared on
the "INTACH Initiative: Study of the Impact of the Earthquake on the
Cultural Properties in Jammu, Poonch and Riasi, J & K". A copy of
this report was submitted to the UNESCO office in Delhi. The report
can be downloaded from INTACH website www.intach.org .

Natural Heritage
Rock Art Documentation: The second phase of rock art documentation
work in Almora and Nainital in Uttaranchal and Orissa has been
completed. 18 rock art sites in both the districts were covered and 7
villages were documented for the ethno archaeological study.
Ecological Development of Army Biodiversity Park: The project on
ecological development of 825 acres of Army Biodiversity park,
Mahipalpur has been entrusted to INTACH. GPS mapping has been carried
our and species list has been prepared.
Revitalization of the Pond at Garli, Himachal Pradesh: Under this
project, civil works are in progress. Desilting has been started and
till date 64000 cubic feet mud has been removed from the pond.

Indian Council of Conservation Institutes (ICCI)
National Mission for Manuscripts:
Lucknow: The Lucknow Centre carried out conservation of 1152 folios
of 31 manuscripts received from Akhil Bhartiya Sanskrit Parishad,
Lucknow and Sri Digambar Jain Mandir, Aashiyana, Lucknow. Preventive
conservation work at Sri Mahavir Saraswati Vidyapeeth, Yamuna
Terhati, Etawah was carried out. The team of conservators completed
preventive conservation of 235 manuscripts having 6990 folios and
emergency conservation treatment in 4 manuscripts having 199 folios
at the Vidyapeeth.
Bhubaneshwar: Preventive conservation was carried out in 237 palm
leaf manuscripts having 28,279 folios. Curative conservation of 73
palm leaf manuscripts having 5,575 folios was also done, which
included 2 illustrated manuscripts with 61 folios; and 1 paper
manuscript having 64 folios.
Tripunithura: Conservation of 15 palm leaf manuscripts having 1154
folios at Sukritindra Oriental Research Institute, Ernakulam; and 4
manuscripts having 413 folios at Chinmay International Foundation was
carried out. Preventive conservation of 5 palm leaf manuscripts
having 164 folios was carried out for Dr Sankaran, Kottayam.
Conservation team visited Chavara Hills (Ernakulam), Kerala
University, Sree Ramdas Ashramam, Mahakavi Kumaran Asan Memorial
Trust, Government Ayurveda College at Tiruvananthapuram to examine
the manuscript collection and sensitize the staff for proper
conservation of manuscript collection.
Bangalore: Curative conservation of 36 palm leaf manuscripts
belonging to a private owner was completed. Preventive conservation
work is in progress at Shrimad Veershaiva Shivayoga Mandir, Badami
Taluk, which has a large collection of palm leaf and paper
manuscripts. Preventive conservation was carried out for 66 bundles
of palm leaf manuscripts having 6990 folios. Two employees of the
Mandir were also trained in preventive conservation.
Conservation of Wall Paintings at Dada Sibba, Himachal Pradesh: The
second phase of this project undertaken by Delhi Centre involving
repair of plaster on outer walls is in progress.
Conservation of Hermitage, Nainital: The conservation work for wall
paper and tiles has been completed under this project of Delhi
Centre. An exhibition relating to different stages in conservation of
this historic building has been mounted by Delhi Centre.
K R Cama Oriental Institute, Mumbai: In the phase III of the project,
conservation of 4 manuscripts with 437 folios was done by
Bhubaneshwar Centre. Besides, 537 other folios were treated and 17
maps were lined.
Survey and Documentation of Manuscripts, NORAD: The total
manuscripts surveyed and documented so far by Bhubaneshwar centre
comes to over 1,75,000 palm leaf manuscripts and over 13,500 paper
manuscripts in 28 districts of Orissa.
Meharjirana Library, Navsari, Gujarat: Conservation of 5 manuscripts
having 801 folios was carried out and with this conservation of rare
manuscripts of the collection of this Library was successfully
completed by Lucknow Centre.
Kalari Kovalikam Palace, Kollangode, Kerala: One of each oil
painting, print and photograph received from this palace are being
conserved by Tripunithura Centre.
Mural Paintings at Thiru Kacham Kurriam Temple, Kollangode, Kerala:
Conservation of mural paintings at this temple by Tripunithura Centre
is in progress. Two panels of paintings in Garbha Graha have been
conserved and two are remaining. Cleaning of paintings on east and
south walls of Namashkara Mandapa is also being carried out. West
wall and some parts of the ceiling remain to be conserved.
Napier Museum, Thiruvananthapuram: Under this project of Tripunithura
Centre, 50 bronze images are under treatment.
Kolkata Centre: The recently established Kolkata Centre was partly
shifted to 4C Chapel Road, Hasting, Kolkata. The Honorable Governor
of West Bengal paid a visit to the new premises. The Centre conserved
1 oil painting, 3 paper paintings, and 1 each of tempera, acrylic and
pencil paintings.
Mumbai Centre: 10 Krishna miniature paintings, 2 sculptures, 13 clay
models and 2 stone tablets were conserved at the Centre.

Heritage Education

Teacher Training Workshops for Setting up Heritage Clubs in Schools:
HECS has received a positive response from Srinagar (July), Rohtak
(August), Chandigarh (September), Anantpur (September) and Bangalore
(September) Chapters for organizing the teacher training workshop.
Young INTACH Newsletter: The July-September issue of the newsletter
based on the theme Save Our Seas has been printed and sent to all the
Heritage Clubs, INTACH Chapter and other relevant institutions and
individuals. The Young INTACH Newsletter is now open for individual
subscriptions also @ Rs 50 per year (4 issues).
Festive Fun - Radio Programme, Chennai: Chennai Chapter is working
towards finalizing the broadcast of the radio programme. The text of
the workbook and translation in Tamil is nearing completion. Two
workshops for voice training and script writing were organized by the
Chapter for the schools in collaboration with Gyan Vani radio
station.
Festive Fun - Radio Programme, Kolkata: Kolkata Chapter is working in
collaboration with British Council to expand the outreach of the
radio programme.
Festive Fun - Radio Programme, Pune: Pune Chapter has conducted a
workshop with teachers and is looking for local sponsors.
Thursday 1 o' clock series: The following talks/presentations were
organized during the month of June at the INTACH Central
Office:
June 8,2006: Talk- Explorations in Egypt and Kenya by Shobita Punja
June 15, 2006: Talk - Glimpses of Romania by Corina Fazocas

Cultural Events

Friday Lecture /Performance Series
INTACH has agreed to collaborate with the ATTIC and IIC to organize a
series of lectures on Delhi by eminent people at the IIC and INTACH.
The invites will be sent out to all the e-group members.

Chapters' News

New INTACH Members: 15 Life Members, 5 Associate Life Members; 6
Associate Members and 6 Student Members were added to the list of
INTACH Members.
Conservation Workshop for Convenors/ Co-Convenors of the Eastern and
North-Eastern Chapters, organized by ICCI, will be held in
Bhubaneshwar on September 18-20, 2006.

The North Zone Convenors Meet will be held on September 20-24, 2006
at Delhi.

Chapter Guidelines have been translated in Hindi and published and
are under distribution to the Hindi speaking states.

Delhi Chapter
Sultan Garhi - INTACH Delhi Chapter in collaboration with Delhi
Development Authority has been working on the conservation of ruins
adjacent to the centrally protected monument of Sultan Garhi. Once
conserved the ruins will enhance the historic value of the site and
provide additional value. The Project is nearing completion.
Mehrauli Archaeological Conservation Initiative- Phase VI - Delhi
Chapter is now working on Phase VI of the Conservation of Unprotected
monuments located in Mehrauli Archaeological Park. INTACH Delhi
Chapter and Delhi Tourism have restored over 40 monuments in five
phases as part of the Mehrauli Archaeological Park Conservation
Initiative in collaboration with DDA, Archaeology, and MCD.

Signage at Heritage Buildings in Delhi - Signages have been installed
at 167 heritage buildings in Delhi by the Delhi Chapter in
collaboration with Delhi Tourism and with the support of DDA, MCD,
NDMC, Delhi Tourism and Transportation Development Corporation (DTTDC).
Conservation area boards have also been installed in 14 conservation
areas in Delhi.

Allahabad Chapter
The Chapter has received a grant from Deputy Municipal Commissioner
to undertake the listing of Allahabad. A preliminary preview of 200
monuments has already been done.

Bilaspur Chapter

The Chapter was inaugurated on June 18, 2006 and the inaugural
function was attended by around 100 Chapter members and other eminent
citizens. A brochure 'Bilaspur - through the history' was also
released. A copy of this brochure is available in the INTACH Library
for reference.

Kolkata Chapter
The Chapter is working in partnership with the local newspapers to
spread awareness about the heritage. Central Office has received the
book 'Shri Krishna R Parekh ki Patrikarta Yatra - 2003-06' written by
Dr H P Maheshwari, INTACH Gwalior Chapter Convenor and is available
in INTACH Library for reference.
