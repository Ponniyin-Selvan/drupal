that the department has brought out numerous publications in the past
two yeras is good effort for this eternally sleeping deparent::
======================================
THANKS FOR OPPORTUNITY TO HIGHLIGHT ::

1. STATE ARCHEOLGY commenced activities in 1965. Published inscription
from Chennai, Kanyakumari (5 Vols) Dt. Then Undivided Thanjavur
(Thiruthuraipoondi - Nannilam - Kumbknm - Tvlnchzhi Volumes.
Thgamaraipakkam - Perumukkal, Dharmapurai - Chengam Books.- Total
inscriptions from 1965 to Sept 2004:: 7646.

2. FAST TRACK works since Sept 2004 :: 9200 inscriptions copied till
March 2006 - inscriptions included by various teams employed to
almost every District. 2811 inscriptions digitalised.

3. 1215 from Mysore estempages FIRST READING completed.

4. Apart from inscriptions, URNS, coins etc., were also found /
studied.
=============================

but the identifcation of harappan script on the neolithic axe is the
wildest iagination of the departent and the great scholar

I.mahadevan.
====================================
TS SRIDHAR, IAS, Special Commissioner, in his Foreward dt: 15.5.2006
in the SPECIAL EDITION "KALVETTU" QUARTERLY ISSUE NO: 70 - APRIL 2006
Published byt TN State Archeology states : FOUR HARAPPAN SYMBOLS have
been identified from the Stone axe by Dr. N.Marxia Gandhi, Ast Suptt
and Dr. S.Rajagopal, Sr. Epigraphist as NO: 48, 342, 367, 307
respectively as per the Concordance of Iravatham Mahadevan. ...
On this MONUMENTOUS and JOYOUS Occasion, I wish to record our sense of
satisfaction in having discovered this outstanding artefact WHICH CAN
BE RIGHTLY CALLED THE DISCOVERY OF THE CENTURY...

He adds, of course, SEM, X Ray Diffraction and opther tests have to be
done.. Thereafter we propose to hold an International Seminar ... "
================================

If they had shown it to the Cm without conferring the discovery in
the acdemic circles, it is a huge mistake.
=====================================
Iravatham's Note on Muruku Sign published in the Journal of the
institute of Asian studies in 1999; Dinamani dt: 11.5.2006 has brought out an Editorial; The Hindu dt: 1.5.2006 has reported the Discovery of a Century; The New Indian Express dt: 2.5.06 wrote on Harappan influence in Tamil
country.
The Telegraph dt: 12,6.2006 - South to Indus Valley, axe is the link.

On 28.04.2006, T.S.Sridhar has presented a Power Point Programme and
among others - Dr. Thiaga. Sathiamurthy Suptt Arch ASI attendee the
same..
On 15.05.2006, TS Sridhar IAS has shown the axe to the Hon'ble Chief
Minister of Tamilnadu - Dr. Kalaignar - who observed in the Dinamani :
Dravidian culture was present upto Sind province and it is Tamils
Culture as established by Dr. AIravatham Mahadevan. Tamils should
take pledge to preserve the same ".
=====================================
i write with the experience of having

examined any neolithic axes ( as my formative years in ASI were
spent
in exploring neolithic sites) and being the custodian of the largest
collection of harappan seals in India for over three years.
====================================

Other interesting finds during Sept 2004 - March 2006 :

Measurement sticks - Pandia merchant in Thondai 10th Century - 9th
Century Chalukya Mandap - KARUVUR THEVAR KALVETTU in Karuvur (more
details in issue 65); - Diety's name in Sivalinga at Kovalam; -
CHOZHANAI ERIZHJA KO-VEERAPANDIAN (victim of AK) Perayur - Madurai.

=======================================

Harappa Script 48 (sitting posture of man -
Harappa 342 - resembles cup

Harappa 367 - Tri headed Soolam
Harappa 301 - Combined ring

and the scripts mean MURUGU and scripts 367 307 mean AN according to
Iravatham!
Period 2000 BC to 1000 BC - appxly 1500 BC.
