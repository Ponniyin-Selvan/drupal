Dear Gokul & friends,

My turn to thank you for being so nice and acknowledging.

As members are aware, Rajakesari was publihsed in www.varalaaru. com in
serial ... and I had problems with " Tamil fonts " in my computer and
could not read the whole thing in one go.

But Gokul has successfully set the trend in more than one way in his
way of telling the History Fiction ::

1. He has used CONTEMPORARY TERMS ( 11TH CENTURY Tamil words wherever
possible to describe things and clarifying them then and there) in his
work.

2. Important Facts he has narrated with supportives (inscriptions /
Copper plates etc., )

3. The style was closer to Kalki (assuming the reader to be in front
of the story teller) and nice crisp narrations too.

4. In any Fiction (and more in History related) more than 3 -4 leads
will be taken in the beginning and descriptions reach some point and
in climax all the leads meet. Gokul has handled this cleverly without
disregarding the related events in the earlier episodes.

Since I could not continue reading with the " Climax " I could not
summerise the story as a whole and appreciate.

Glad that Gokul and Palaniappa Brothers (through our dear Dhivakar)
have given this wonderful opportunity to me and of course to other
such History-story lovers too.

Same is the case with Cherankottai too (as the TITLE reminds me of
Kandhalur Chalai)..

Would love to read this in one go.

Dear all
Kindly accept my heartiest thanks for all the well wishes. But for
this group & its wonderful members, this novel would not have been
written or published.

My very special thanks to ....

My father - But for whom, I wouldn't have had any attitude towards
tamil literature. He is always my first reader and a merciless critic!!

SPS - who has always been a constant source of inspiration & support
to me. Throughout the novel, I pestered him with several questions & got beautiful answers as always. He was obviously not happy with my
portrayal of Rajaraja maarayar. But what to do? My story required that!

Mdm Seethalakshmi - She wanted to see rajakerari in print much more
than myself !

Dr.Kalaikkovan - Whose guidance is a boon to any student who ventures
into history. His books "Thalicherik kalvettukkal" and "Chozhar kaalai
aadar kalai" were extensively used for Rajakesari.

Dr.Kudavoil - But for Dr.Kudavoil's masterpiece book on "Thanjavoor",
which was provided to me by SPS, Rajakesari would have lost all of its
thanjavur colours.

To co members of varalaaru.com team (Kamal / Krupa / Ram / Lavanya) -
Whose association has helped me in more than one way. The launch of
varalaaru enabled wider readership of rajakesari(at that time were accessible only to group members).

To chapter by chapter feedbacks. To a writer venturing into fiction first
time, this is a tonic.

To my reader Muruganandam - Whose feedbacks were vitamins

To venkat & Dhiwakar - My seniors & wonderful writers. I am a big time
fan of their kollaikkarars and vamsatharas.

To Pavitra & Lavanya(samudrakumari) - For their encouraging initial
feedbacks

To Sri - who provided an elaborate feedback with the finale of rajakesari

To all members of this

To numerous readers of varalaaru.com - who read the novel and provided
feedbacks.

Anbudan
Gokul.S
