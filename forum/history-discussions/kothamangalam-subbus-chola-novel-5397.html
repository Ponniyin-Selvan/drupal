Hi all,

I have talked about kalaimani's novels in the past but no one seemed to bother. In this forum I am seeing only some specific persons' emals to get responses. Mine go largely ignored, for reasons unfathomable to me.

Still, I would like to pen my own thoughts on Kothamangalam Subbu who, in my opinion, wrote Tamil with as much felicity as Kalki did in his heydays. kalamiani's novels like Raobahadur Singaram and Thillana Mohanambal, both later made into movies with Sivaji Ganesan in the lead role, are ever-living examples of his prowess with the written word in our native language.

I have also talked about his novel "poonivananthu Poonguyil" in an earlier main, as I have mentioned above. The novel is a superb historical one, serialised in "Kalki (BTW both Thillana and Raobahadur were serialised in ananda vikatan when s s vasan was alive and Kothamangal subbu was part of Gemini Studios' story writers' group). I had read the novel when it was serialised in Kalki and later as a complete novel. It deals with the Marathan period in Thanjavur and his story telling skills come out in full measure. My personal favourite is Raobahadur Singaram, especially his characterisation of Marudappar (Sengamalam's pop) and Dharmalingam (Singaram's cunning paternal uncle) which are outstanding.

Regards

Ramaswamy

Hi all,

Your explanation accepted, Mr Venkat. Now some more abt Subbu.
Kindly try and read
the Thillana Mohambal novel. It certainly drags towards the end but
I had been told that
it was so populkar at that time that ananda vikatan proprietor
Vasan asked him to
keep extending it - like a mega serial - if one wants to relate it
to modern times. But the
way he wrote, his style and the language, were simply superb.
Anyone with interest in carnatic music and especially
nadaswaram and bharata natyam should read this for the way he's
eulogies both these
art forms.

And the characterisation! I am sure those who read the novel and
saw the film would have
been disappointed. Varadan, the mridangam player for Mohanambal,
virtually her big brother,
did not come out well in the film version. But not many novels can
be adopted well to the
small or big screeens.

And the character of Savadal Vaithy! It has been developed from Mr
Sampath which Gemini
produced in Hindi adopted from their own Miss Malini in Tamil, I am
told. Can anyone throw
deeper insight into this?
