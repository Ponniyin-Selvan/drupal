even now am mailing from a browsing centre in my village( yes swetha

uthama's rule was not a waste.
it was the asthivaram for a mammoth empire later.
a lull from infighting amidst the second level chieftans.
a consolidation of power

and as one side of history says overseas empire in these years. a main reason why hinduism spread
all over the far east
also the inter sea commerce seems to have stabilised in this period.
not to mention the temples.

imagine the scenario
when uthama dies he leaves a land of peace, a treasury which is
full and an energetic and experienced heir (

perhaps uthamas rule can be compared with narasimha raos rule. i for
one beleive that 100 years from now when some body writes the
history of our country they would call n. raos rule of 5 years the
turning point.
i personally feel the cholas matured in uthamas period.
it was an important phase in their evolution.

regards
venketesh

There are lots of doubts about Uththama Chozhan. KAN Sastry
doesn't
absolve him. Sadasiva Pandaraththar defends him as innocent. In
view
of the above, Kalki played it safe by making Sendhan Amudhan as
Uththama Choazhan.

We don't know what were Maduranthakan's motives. He might not have
planned to kill AK, but he would have coveted the throne. I don't
see anything wrong in this.

After he ascended the throne, he wasn't such a bad ruler. Look at
all the katralis in Chozha nadu. Mother and son have built and
renovated so many temples.

No major wars. A very peaceful time in the country. At least, I
haven't heard about any battles, revolts or unrest during this
period.

I wouldn't term it as a 'colossal waste'.

LOL. Sounds like the AU (Alternate Universe) fiction I and other fanfiction
writers often attempt. Let me count the ways...
I think I might - I **might** let Adhitha Karikalan live. Takes away the
plot twist - but I think I would make it an assasination *attempt* , instead
of outright murder. I can't reconcile myself to a Chola primce dying, yet.
And I would make Adhitha Karikalan come to his senses, and announce that
Arulmozhi is to be the next Emperor, thus paving the way for Rajaraja,
without bloodshed.
*belong* together.
And oh.........I would not, not, *not* marry off Vanathi, with her die-away
airs, to our magnificent Arulmozhi. Gah. The Kodumbalur Princess she might
be, but a more droopy heroine I've never seen. Give him a Poonghuzhali, now.
Why would he marry 13 other wives, then?? She'd rip him apart if he did.
{grin}
Although that mightn't be good for politics...
But hey, this is an Alternate Universe.
My 2 paise, folks. :-)
Cheers,
