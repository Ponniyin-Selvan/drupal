Dear All,

Since, there were some discussions and finger pointing based on an
article published in our varlaaru.com, we feel it is our
responsibility to explain. So, I take this opportunity to clarify
certain things happening for past 1 year on behalf of varalaaru team.

1. Regarding Buddha and Rajaraja photos

When we made trip to thanjavur and pazhaiyaarai last year by this
time, some of us accepted that the person in thanjavur painting was
Rajaraja and the person in panchavan madhevi pallippadai was
Rajendra based on the location and 'who else it could be' basis. But
for some of us, it was not convincing proof. So, i just wanted to
let them understand what all they have to accept if they start
accepting without considering other negative evidences. So, I posted
a picture of Buddha statue with chakra mark in it. As Rajaraja also
had sangu chakra rekhai in his palms according to Thiruvalangadu
copper plates, i told it has to be considered as Rajaraja. People
started disproving the statement and gave valid points why it cannot
be Rajaraja. I was happy and hoped that people would apply the same
rules for the issues raised during the trip too. So, I ended the
drama with explanation of my actual intention.

Swetha oru dheerkka dharisi. Her words

"Kamal, neenga Japan ponalum intha pavam ungalai uruthu vanthu
oottum!"

has become true now. I have changed the description of the pictures.
So, new members won't get misguided.

2. Rajaraja and Karuvoor thevar painting

It was told that the young man and old man in thanjavur painting
were Rajaraja and his spiritual guru Karuvoor thevar. But from
inscriptions of Rajarajeswaram, we knew that Rajaraja's guru
was 'Eesana siva pandithar' and not Karuvoor thevar. Karuvoor thevar
was not mentioned in any of Rajaraja period inscriptions. It clearly
proves that there was no relationship between Rajaraja and
Karuvooraar even if a person by name Karuvoor thevar lived during
that period. Thiruvisaippaa was quoted for proof. When i went
through that, i misunderstood Thirubhuvanam with Thiru poovanam.
When Venkatesh notified that, I couldn't reply immediately as i
became busy at work and some personal commitments. After that, new
threads were started and i forgot to reply to that. Anyway, I accept
that it was my mistake.

5. Who else it could be? Rajaraja and Rajasimma

In his second mail to VK, Satish quoted that when Dr.Kalaikkovan
said that the statue of a king in Dharmaraja radham is Rajasimma
on 'who else it could be' basis, we accepted. In the same way, when
he said that painting in periya koyil is didn't accept. With this, he came to a conclusion that
Dr.Kalaikkovan accepts assumptions indirectly.

Dear Satish,

Do you really think both 'Who else it could be' are same? I am not
sure if you have read the book 'Adhyandha kaamam' completely.
Otherwise, you wouldn't have raised this point. I give the summary
of points considered before concluding it is Rajasimma. If you are
not convinced still, it is my inability to explain as I don't have
the book with me right now and please go through the book.

Labeled Royal Portraits are usually met with only on prominent parts
of the temple such as the walls or the niches. You may refer to
Mahavaraaha temple at Mamallapuram and several other temples in the
cholamandalam (Thiruvaavaduthurai, Thiruvisaloor, Konerirajapuram,
etc). Dr. Kudavoil Balu himself has mentioned a few such labeled
portraits carved on the walls of the temple towers at Chidambaram in
his book entitled 'Goburakkalai marabu'.

Though labeled portrait sculptures are detected so far in the greeva
naasikaas or koodu in the kapodha, male figures worshipping linga
are depicted in many temples. Thiruvalanjuzhi is a good example
where one can identify many such figures. Don't you think that one
should take much care before identifying a figure as a particular
royal person? Is this not his duty to provide enough evidences to
substantiate such identity before it is questioned?

In the case of adhyandha kaamam, the creator of the temple was
identified after a thorough analysis of its architecture, iconology
and epigraphic records. This and the titles (Srimega, Sri Trilokya
vardhana and Vidhi) engraved on the niche where the sculpture is
placed, helped to confirm the portrait figure as Rajasimma.

Ram is translating the chapter 'Nizhalum Nijamum' from the
book 'Adhyandha kaamam' as Dr. Michael Lockwood asked for the
English version. We will send it soon.

Now, let us see the case of rajarajeswaram. In the saanthaaram
paintings, two persons are said to be Rajaraja.

a. Person worshipping Nataraja

Appearance of the person worshipping Nataraja has no dissimilarities
with Cheraman peruman naayanar portrayed in Sundarar panel. Cheraman
peruman was also a king. We cannot say only Rajaraja worshipped
Nataraja. So, it can be any king. All we can say is one king is
portrayed. That's all. 'Who else it could be' argument is
inappropriate here. We know for sure that two persons in other side
panel is Sundarar and Cheraman because we know the theme behind it
and other pieces of paintings in that panel are also supporting
this. This worshipping panel may also have some theme behind it.
Unless a complete study like 'Adhyandha kaamam' is carried out, we
cannot say who is who. Not only panel, which has that person, entire
panel in the wall has to be studied properly without any
preconceived notion. Even when the well known sundarar panel was
studied by Dr.Kalaikkovan, we encountered lot of stunning details.
They are going to be published in his book 'Vaanan vandhu vazhi
thandhu' shortly.

b. Young man and old man

The two persons are said to be Rajaraja and his guru karuvoor
thevar. Already it is proved that there is no relationship between
these two. The attire and hairstyle doesn't match with a king.
Moreover, Dr.Kudavoil Balu proved that they are 'Sanakaadhi
munivargal'. I am not sure if he has written any article on this.
But when varalaaru team met him in thanjavur for second or third
time, he explained how he concluded that they are sanakaadhi
munivargal. All his points were very much valid and acceptable. You
can ask him when you meet next time.

So, from this, we can conclude that both cannot be said to be
Rajaraja and both 'who else it could be' are not same.

Here, i would like to have a disclaimer. We are in the beginning
stage of learning historical research methodologies and subject. Our
explanation capabilities are limited. So, please do not go by our
statements which are not complete or quoted in different scenario.
When you encounter such things, please look at the source or ask for
more explanation. Because, already such confusion occurred in the
past regarding podhikai. In Pullamangai and Thiruvalanjuzhi, we had
less time and had lot of things to see other than podhikai. So, I
couldn't explain well. In that case, consulting the articles of
Kattadakkalai series would be helpful. Hope you understand my point.

Venkatesh used to mention about "School of thought" in couple of his

Dear Venkatesh,

Which one do you mean by 'School of thought' here? If you
say 'Rajaraja was ego-centric person. So, he might have projected
him somewhere in his masterpiece temple', that is school of thought.
If you say 'Tholkappiyam has words like 'enmanaar pulavar'. So it
may belong to 7th century AD', that is school of thought. If you
say 'Thirukkural uses some of Jainism concepts. So, thiruvalluvar
may be a Jain', that is school of thought. But how can Magudagamam
and pon vimaanam concept be considered as School of thought when we
have evidences to disprove the role of Magudagamam in big temple?

And you said 'at last the clash of the egos has come to the
forefront'. It is not so. As i stated in the point 3, this issue was
brought out around 10 years before when the reference of magudagamam
was first quoted in Big temple Kumbabishekam malar. Many scholars
witnessed this. What do you mean by 'behind the scene and proxy
squabbling'? What is the necessity for us to hide and fight? Didn't
we ask these questions directly to Dr.Kudavoil Balu? If our
questions were answered already, why are we asking again and again?
Moreover, none of our questions are unanswerable during our times or
as difficult as finding Tutankhamen tomb.
