Sometimes, the king also would take the field in person.
There are several instances about this.

We also hear about kings fleeing from the battle field with rags.
And we also hear about kings winning wars in the battle field or
getting killed.

The Battle of Talikkotta was lost because of two causes.
One of them was because, RamaRaya, the dictator of VijayaNagar
took the field in person.
He was ninty seven years old at that time.

The Rajendra II had two sons.

After him, his eldest son became the emperor.
There was a war with the Western Chalukyas.
The Cholza Emperor was killed.
His younger brother who was also in the battlefield, was
immediately crowned and the battle continued. A disasterous
defeat was turned into a victory.

We read about the child Nedunjcelizyan wearing the 'ainggudumi' -
???׵? = of childhood, ascending the war chariot and going towar
against several kings and vELirs, swearing a terrible oath
that should he be defeated, let the poets led by the master
Manggudi Marudhan depart his realms apart from other shames.

Refer to PuRanAnURu.

All Tamil youths should must read the PuRanAnURu.
Now, more than ever.

It all depended on circumstances and need.

Sending a general or a prince on a war expedition was an elaborate
ceremony.

It was known as 'thaNdu eduththal' -??? ?ו??'.

According to Chinese practice, even the king could not alter an order
made by the general or any action taken by him.

There is an interesting event in the story of General Sun Tzu.
