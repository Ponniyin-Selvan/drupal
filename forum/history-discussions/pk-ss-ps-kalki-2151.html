Re Aall Maarattam - DISGUISING - was a practice adopted by the Rulers
in those days .. Can see in Rajaraja Chozhan movie also.. Read Porkai
Pandiyan Story .. To protect the subject's interest the Just Pandya
King - in disguise - knocked a door in the night and when found he
made a mistake, he repeated knocking on other doors and severed his
hand in the Court and was rewarded a golden arm ..

Re Madurantakan matter, it is different .. When Historian - Sastry -
suspected Uthama as a co-conspirator - but highlighted him as "Sembiyanmadevi thiruvaryiruditha Uthama
Chozhan .. " Kalki tried to accommodate both the versions ...

Kabaligam - Laguleesam - Siva / Vishnu exchanges were much discussed
in those days and so Kalki tried to highlight the pictures through
his vivid descriptions ...

Are you not able to visualise an "Annapakshi" padagu (boat) when you

Don't you hear the stone-chiselling sound when visiting

Dear SPS ,

A good comprehensive mail. But I think Krupa wont be
convinced. He wrote three mails about SS and its
romance. Here are my views.

Unlike PS, SS is a streight forward story - son
taking revenge for father's defeat. Kalki added
masaalaa to these historical events mainly by this
love affair. A simple equation

History + Romance = SS.

But here Kalki shows his own class by limiting the
romance element well within the limits.(At least
within my limits!). Saandilyan romance elutha innikki
aarambichchaa naalannaikkuthaan mudippaar. It is very
easy for me read his works simply skipping all his
'varunanais' - be it on romance or otherwise. But with
Kalki I want to dwell along his varunanais. Even I
have read some chapters in PS repeatedly for two three
times simply for varunanais.

Generally we men dont like egoistic ladies. In SS
Sivakaami's words and deeds hurt Maamallaa many a
times. So we are biased and poor Sivakaami though she
got all the sympathies of her creator Kalki, she
miserably fails to earn the sympathies of the readers.

In PS many kinds of loves and romances are descibed.
Everything is different and at the same time
absolutely enjoyable. If SS is not compared with PS
then it is a very good historic novel. Here Kalki
himself could not resist describing the discomfort
felt by Maamalla by Sivakaami's behaviour.

Mahendraavaa Sivaajiyai ninaichchukallam.
Maamallaavaa Kamlai ninaichchukallam. Sivakaamiyaa
yaarai ninaikkarathu? allaaraiyum namakku romba
pudikkume?

In Padayappaa Nilaambari is a villi. But Sivakaami is
a heroin having some elements of Nilaambari. So her
love is not much appealing. Kalki chose the name
Sivakaami bcas she is an expert dancer. But the name
itself neithr different nor historic like KUNDAVAI.

The most likable charector in SS is Paranjothi. Kalki
started the SS story with a heroic deed of Paranjothi
but this charector was not much elaborated. Being a
real historic charector his romance and other
activities if elaborated would have been a real treat.

I appreciate Krupa - he wants good men to love only
good girls - a good heart !

I have forgotten the whole of PK. I have to read it
again.

yours
Natarajan .
