Thanjai Trip

Dear All,

A big thankyou to each and every one who was there in
the Thanjai Trip. Calling it a “memorable” or
“unforgettable” is belittling it…… should look for a
good word beyond these…

4 days absence from work kept me busy. Now Ganpathi
Bappa is in our office and home. He comes for 5 days
every year. He also kept me busy with all the
preparation for his grand stay.

Today I got time to open my non-official mails, and
then it took all this time to finish reading all the
mails that have poured in from 2nd onwards. I couldn’t
help going through www.varalaaru.com for those
articles which were fresh in my memory and those I had
kept aside for future reading. Especially, the one on
“Ekaveeri Pidari” and Lavanya’s articles.

Mr.Sundar Bharadwaj is known to Varalaaru.com readers
through an article by Dr.Kalaikovan; still, nothing
like experiencing his hospitality in person. Noone
would have imagined that he would come to rescue us
from the mini-bus, that too, driving it all himself
all the way from Thanjavur. When we stopped for
dinner, he wouldn’t focus on his food till he gets
news about those who were still travelling by the
mini-bus. His love for on-going work at Thiruvalanchuzi……. No words…. I am
yet to meet another person who can be so enthusiastic
about Tamil History. Hats off to you SB Sir!

Mr.Ram, the young guy all of only 25, who started this
wonderful group. His love for Kalvettu Aayvu , music
and Tamil ( Ram, I re-read your article on
Thiruvaiyaru Thiagarajar Aradhanai) …. Amazing guy!
Gives me lots of hope on his generation! Good show!
Keep it up!!

The multi-lingual specialist with an eye for temple
architecture – Mr.Kamalakannan. His lingo on
architecture became more meaningful for me after I saw
them and met him. Such a soft-spoken gentleman with
firm views and deep conviction!

Ms.Lavanya, the Kalvettu Karigai. Amazing enthusiasm!
I am sure she will get her doctorate in epigraphy very
soon. All we have to do is divert her attention from
the “kudumi anidha aanmagan” in the fresco painting
in the Big temple!!!

Mr.Krupa Shankar, Mr.Ramnath, Mr.Satish……. the young
team working hand-in-hand with Ram, Kamal and Lavanya
in making Varalaaru. The inspiration for Satish’s
interest in history could be seen from the interest of
his mother in history and

Ms.Swetha, the mobile history encyclopaedia …….. the
number of books she has read and remembers, and also
recollects within fraction of a second…….. not to
forget her family’s hospitality in their farm-house.
Yes, Nirai Kudam Thazhumbadhu!!

Mr.Venkatesh with his knowledge on Padal petra sthalam
and sense of humour added a different, enjoyable
flavour to the whole trip. I really wish to visit him
in his office to personally check whether he can ever
be sober !

Mr.Sriram (Kamla Hasan Fan!) Mr.Shankaran, Mr.Rajni
Ramki, Mr.Raj Pandiyan, Mr.Ganesan…….. thanks to Kalki
for bringing all of us together!

Mr.Dhiwakar, the one who just missed the
“thennai-maram treatment”. Vallalar alone is capable
of making him sound little serious…..

Mr.Muruganandam with his observation on feudal system
in Thanjai district; Ms.Anuradha with information on
Krishna Premi, Veda Patasalas etc., Ms.Lavanya the
“Patti – converted – to- Bangalore” girl,
Mr.Seetharaman with the original copy of 1950 Maniyam drawing…… one more thanks to Kalki and Ram for

Now coming to Thanjai…Some one could conceive such
massive beautiful construction in those days, is
enough to visualize how civilized our ancestors were
even 1000 years back! Dr.Kudavoil Balu’s session was a
treat! His explanation on Lingam, Kailasam… what to
mention and what to leave! The climax of Thanjavur
trip was the Darshan of “Kumzhil Siripppu” Nataraja at
that time of the day to the accompaniment of Thevaram
sung in such “venkala Kural”. Namachivaya Vazhha,
Nathan Thal Vazhha!!

Pulla Mangai, with its miniature sculptures deserves
another 2 days trip to Pulla Mangai alone. Gangai
Konda Cholapuram Gopuram is still haunting me…. SB
Sir, when is

Thiruvalanchuzhi Vellai Pillaiyar, Ekaveeri Pidari are
the ones I am thinking of before going to bed for the
last 3 days…..the wonderful chanting of the Mantras by
the young Archahar in that temple….. My hope on
India’s youth is increasing multi-fold after seeing
this innocent, uncorruptable young man. One could see
the purity of his heart in his eyes!! Can he ever be
bothered about America-Iraq war or oil price increase,
or even Sonia becoming Congress Head! He is capable of
having trust in God and lead a peaceful life.

Again, the “kudumi-anindha- aanmagan” chased us at
Panchavanmadevi Pallipadai. Who is he? What made
Rajendra build it for his step-mother? How many
secrets it is holding in its stones???? Is this life
enough even to grasp all these???

At Kumbakonam, in Swetha’s bungalow, picture of Netaji
made me so happy, I know not why!

On our way back to Chennai, the lively discussion on

I have not written in Tamil for a real long time. I am
going to try writing the travelogue in Tamil in a day
or two.

Once again, Thanks Kalki! Thanks Ram! Thanks SB Sir
and Swetha! Thanks Satish! Thanks to one and all for
those lovely 2 days!

With warm regards,
Uma
