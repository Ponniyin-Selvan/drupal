Hello Arun

Sound cannot travel thru vacuum - i agree, but is space a perfect
vacuum? In 2003, Astronomers had detected the deepest note ever
generated in the cosmos, a B-flat flying through space like a ripple
on an invisible pond. No human will actually hear the note, because it
is 57 octaves below the keys in the middle of a piano. Detection was
made in Nasa's Chandra X Ray Observatory. Astronomers were not
surprised to find the supermassive black hole making a strong sub-bass
sound. Collectively, the cosmos produce, scientists believe, a
cacophonic symphony of inaudible tunes.

Sound can travel through space, because space is not the total vacuum
it's often made out to be. Atoms of gas give the universe a faint
atmosphere of sorts, even if a very thin one.

Ether cannot be proved that easily, but nevertheless cannot be
disapproved.

Ever try hoisting a ball in vacuum? Try and let me know, I will be
curious to know more details.
