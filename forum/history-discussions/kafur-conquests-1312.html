Kafur Conquests

It is recorded as one of the most unfortunate incidents in TN history.

The pandiyas always ruled in groups - all brothers as well as
relatives use to rule different parts of the region. This has always
been the case - the main one remaining in madurai.
This constantly resulted in lot of internal wars. Probably this
continuously weakened the empire as well.
One such situation occured towards the tail end of later pandiya
period (around 14 century). Not knowing what he was really doing, one
of the brothers(sundara pandiya) who wanted to take over the empire
sought the aid of delhi sultanate.
Malik kafur was already eying south and was moving towords it. May be
he could have been stopped had there been a powerful ruler.
When he saw opportunity in this confusion, he gained confidance and
moved further south.

Soon the whole pandiya group was wiped away.
Malik kafur went away with huge booty looted from temples and
his chiefs took over the madurai(AD 1311). Lack of leadership,
spirit and unity among the locals were to their greatest advantage.

Total destruction of peace in the community started with this
invation and for the next one century or so the whole social fabric
of TN was completely torn apart. Almost all temples had to close down
and people had to bury the idols. Even now the bronze idols which are
discovered here and there were buried only in those times. No public
worship could be performed. Famous temples like madurai and srirangam
came under heavy destruction. (Many portions of the madurai temple we
see today are not really ancient structures but renovated ones)

Terror reigned everywhere and people saw cruelty beyond words can
express.The last king to resist islamic invaders was Veera vallala. A
bitter battle ensued in Kannapuram (today's samayapuram).He was
defeated and his corpse was filled with hay and hung in front of the
madurai gates.

A whole generation lost the values of their religion and culture. It
was at a very late stage that vijayanagar ruler kambana defeated this
terror regime and bought madurai under his control. But much was
lost - not only in terms of material wealth, but spiritual, cultural
values as well.

Sri.Venugopalan's 2 books - Thiruvarangan Ula (2 parts) and Madura
Vijayam (2 parts) records this bloody history with amazing accuracy
and narration. Though it records probably the worst phase of TN
history, it is a must read for people interested in knowing their
past.

The MA history book on TN history from Madras university simply skips
this period of unspeakable turmoil !

Anbudan
Gokul.S
