Chola Pandya branches..

Lets start from the period of Vishwanatha Nayak (1529 to 1564AD).he
is the founder of Poliga system and he divided the southern region
into 72 Palayams.He brought the PANDIYANS OF THENKASI & KAYATHAR
under the Poligar system.

The 2 Pandiyans whom we identify are
1.Sadayavarman srivallaba Pandiyan(THENKASI)
2.Vettum Perumal Raja (KAYATHAR)

Thus its clear that these 2 Pandiyans attained the status of
Poligars.So they must be one among the 72 Poligars.The possibility is
that these 2 Pandiyans might have ruled much larger territory than
the rest of the
poligars.
But the difficulty is to know the names of the chiefs of all the 72
Palayams formed.Anyway we have the list
of Pandiyans who ruled till 1753 AD.Now the big question is what
happened to them after 1753 AD?
We can try in one way.Its by going through the initial records of the
72 poligars and also through the prabandhams sung in praise of the
Poligars.My idea is those poligars who were not the Pandiyas will be
mentioned by their own caste name or caste title but not with a
Pandiyan family name or the title Pandiyan.
I heard that most of the Poligars started using the title Pandiyan at
a much later stage.I came to know that there is a temple somewhere in
Tirunelveli where there is a statue of Adhiveerarama Pandiyar.Also i
heard of a family in Tirunelveli who do shiratham for Adhiveera rama
Pandiyar(1552-1564AD). I don't know what shiratham is.Does this mean
Thivasam?

A correction...

The Cheiftain who claimed to be the descendants of Chola, was 'Veerashekaran' and ruled Kambam, he was defeated by Naagama Naayakar, the father of Vishwanaatha nayakar.

There was another Chola descendant, who ruled Kambam after Veerashekaran and I have not got his name. He was defeated and killed to Ariya naaga mudhali, a associate of Vishwanaatha Naayakar. This war holds importance as Vishwanaatha Naayakar, after this war waged war against Pancha - Paandiyas individually and put a stop to Pandya Dynasty. the place where this war took place is the famous 'Paanchaalankurichchi'.

banks of Veeranam @ Veeranarayana Chaturvedi mangalam - which figures in Chapter 1. Thirumunaippadi naadu ends at Sethiathope (Toll Gate even
