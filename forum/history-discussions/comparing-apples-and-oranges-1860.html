ArunKrishnan,

Kamba Ramayana too is a story.We only are comparing similarities
between both works,not dissimilarities.We compare the common themes
like characterization,description,script,handling of controversial
events etc.

Kalki fares better than kamban in many aspects.As Drsridhar Rathnam
pointed out kalki created the whole story from his creative mind.His
works reached masses and made even ordinary man to enjoy
literature.I got interested in history only becasue i read Ponniyin
selvan.Kamban's work remains in an ivory tower till date.

There are many people better than kamban in many aspects.Vyasa's
charaectirization beats every other work in the world(as far as I
know)The plots and twists he uses in mahabharatha with such huge
number of characters and with giving unique nature and role to each
of them and the coherence with which he moves the story is
unparalleled.

The character of purichravas comes just in the last part.He has only
2 pages.But the importance purichravas gets in those two pages is
mindboggling.Similiar is bhagadathan character ,a 90 year old man
whose eyesight is blocked by wrinkles on his forehead.He ties up his
wrinkles with a silkthread and sits on his elephent supradeepam and
wreaks havoc in pandava army.

In end arjuna cuts off his silkthread and bhagadatha loses his
eyesight.Then arjuna sends an arrow into the bowels of the
elephent.Vyasa then narrates what happened as follows.

"The blind bagadatha not knowing what happened to his elephent
supradeepam forced it to fight with arjuna.But like how a wife wont
listen to the words of a husband who has lost the family
fortunes,supradeepam for the first time in life refused to listen to
the words of bagadatha"

I will write more on this in some other time.

I will write a funny incident.He was a saint.One day he saw two lustful birds
copulating.He was very happy to see the two birds being happy.But
suddenly an arrow killed the male bird.Seeing this the female bird
also died.A hunter came to pick up the birds.Valmigi went mad in
rage.He immediatly said in sorrow to the hunter as follows

"Oh the one equal to a tree,you killed a lustful creature.Till the
sun and moon exists you will be remembered for this"

He did not curse the hunter though.He was very sad and came back
home.He then thought of the words he said at the hunter.He thought
on the first words "equal to a tree"(mara) He pronounced it
continuously mara mara mara ....and it became rama,rama.

Then suddenly the verse he used to scold the hunter became the verse
to praise rama.Rama was the hunter.The lustful male bird was
ravana.The suffering female bird was mandothari.The hunter killed
the lustful bird and for that he will be remembered till the sun and
moon existed.

The verse suddenly had a new meaning

"Oh Rama,you killed the lustful creature.Till the sun and moon exist
you will be remembered for it"

Thus was born the epic of ramayana.

Ofcourse I am not saying that Ramayana was a fictional creation of
Valmigi.Legend has it that Valmigi wrote Ramayana only after god
appeared before him and said that he wanted valmigi to write
Ramayana and that is why this whole drama was played.

we compare works across time only to pay our homages to the dead
authors and to be a guide to authors of future and to develop our
literary knowledge.Some do it for ego serving too,which I dont blame.
Without "vidhya garvam" a scholar isnt a scholar.But when it becomes
"padicha thimir" he will be knocked down to earth sooner or later.

That is when literary meetings come in handy.They not only point out
what the faults are with the authors but also point out the fault in
the approach of the debaters.

When nakkerar said that "Even women from devalokam have no natural
fragrance in their hair" he was knocked down by shivaperuman himself.
Becasue nakkerar couldnt have known the fragrance of the hair of
women in devalokam.He went to the extent of saying "Even the Uma
devi whom I worship doesnt have a natural fragrance in her hair"

Here he was making an unsubstantiated claim.So he was knocked down
by shivaperuman himself.This is just a legend though.Valluvan also
wrote this as avaiadakkam.

regards
sampath
