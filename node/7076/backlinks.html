Babies with Tamil names to get gold ring

Staff Reporter
http://www.hindu.com/2009/02/23/stories/2009022358810400.htm
CHENNAI: Babies born in Corporation maternity hospitals will be given
a one-gram gold ring each if they are given Tamil names, announced
Mayor M.Subramanian. On production of the birth certificate to the
Corporation, the parents could receive the ring, he said.

Speaking at the council meeting on Saturday, he said that the
Corporation had also taken initiative to improve the healthcare of the
weaker sections.

This includes installation of ECG facility in all dispensaries of the
civic body, starting of laboratories in dispensaries and laparoscopy
equipment in eight 24-hour hospitals. Centralised oxygen storage
facilities would be set up in eight zones at the cost of Rs. 40 lakh
to provide uninterrupted oxygen to hospitals. He said that maternity
hospitals in all 10 zones would be provided equipment to the tune of
Rs. 14 lakh. Operation theatres would be upgraded at the cost of
Rs.1.5 crore.

The Tondiarpet Communicable Diseases Hospital would be modernised at
the cost of Rs.13.53 crore.

equipment in eight 24-hour hospitals. Centralised oxygen storage
facilities would be set up in eight zones at the cost of Rs. 40 lakh
to provide uninterrupted oxygen to hospitals. He said that maternity
hospitals in all 10 zones would be provided equipment to the tune of
Rs. 14 lakh. Operation theatres would be upgraded at the cost of
Rs.1.5 crore.

The Tondiarpet Communicable Diseases Hospital would be modernised at
