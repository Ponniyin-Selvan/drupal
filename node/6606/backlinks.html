Reply to query No - 1

The inscription of RRC under reference says "??.Yandu
Irupaththaingjavathu, Naal Irunootrelupaththu-thainginaal Udaiyaar
Sri
Rajarajathevar Sri Rajarajeehwaram Udaiyaar SriVimaanaththu sembin
isthoopith-thariyil "vaikka kuduththa" seppukudam ontru nirai
moovayirathuenpathu, mupalaththil surukkina thahadu pala pon
adavallaan ennum kallalnirai irandaayiraththu tholaayirathu
irupthaaru
kanlanj sarai?."

(A.D.1010) RRC gave the kudam to "vaikka" (i.e.given to place on
Thari
but not yet placed) and donated gold for coating the top finial. It
is
after this day and year - the gold plating and the final finish on
the
Finial was done - with 90 days left to complete same in the year -
on
ground level, and then to erect same at the top of the 216 ft high
Vimanam with final finish, and dressing. This would have definetely
covered this period of 90 days. Only after fitting the Finial on
the
Vimanam and other final finishes the Kumba-abishekam would have been
done, which would have definitely fallen in to the year A.D.1011.

This is why the 26th year Inscription in A.D.1011(985 + 26) of RRC
in
the same place in this temple mentions "....(at) Thanjavur in
Thanjavur Kootram the 'sacred granite-stone temple' (Thiru-Kat-
trali)
I got it erected, the Sri Rajarajeeswaram Udaiyar....."

Reply to query No - 2

Historically we know RRC had a lawful Queen Vaanavan Maadevi and
10 -
other
Theviayar. Rajendra Chola - 1 was the lawful heir to the throne by
Vanavan Maadevi. The sons of other Theviyar of Rajaraja Chola - 1
too
are considered as brothers (more precisely step brothers) of
Rajendra
Chola ? 1 who took part in active roles - as Chola commanders
(generals) on various war expeditions, and also as chieftains of
various regions of the Chola empire under the ruling Chola kings.
They
were given titles bearing the name of the regions they successfully
captured on behalf of the respective Chola kings.

Prince
who went on war expedition to Ganges River successfully, was his
"?.Siriya Thaathai meaning Siriya Thanthai or Father's younger
brother?." who has been given the title by the father of Rajendra
Chola - 2 being the Rajendra Chola - 1 as "Gangaikonda Cholan".
Rajendra Chola - 1 hence assumed the title "Gangaikondaan" for
himself.

Likewise from the same Mikeerthi we note one of the sons (Kathalar)
of
Rajendra Chola - 2, being the grandson of Rajendra Chola -1, on
behalf
of him went on the Kadarem expedition which he successfully captured
and was given the title as Kadaramkonda Cholan. Hence Rajendra
Chola ?
1 himself assumed the title the Kadarem Kondaan.

Reply to query No - 3

Separate research paper is being prepared by me o be published soon
under the title " The medieval Chola Empire and its relations with
Malaysia and Indonesia", which will give you much informations on
same. Await same.

Reply to query No - 4

GKC finished his Gangetic expedition in A.D.1023 for sure.
Immediately
after his return to Chola country he started the construction of his
new capital city Gangaikonda Cholapuram, the Siva temple
Gaingaikonda
Cholaeswarem and the Chola Gangam.
It took seven years for Rajaraja Chola to construct the
Rajarajeswarem
temple. But we have positive dates from nthe inscriptions. But in
respect of Gangaikonda Cholaeswarem we have no such positive
evidence.
But being an Engineer myself I could approximately studying the
extent
of both complexes estimate the time frame for construction of
Gangalkonda Cholaeswarem as five years. Hence Ib arrived at an
approximate date of completion of construction of Gangaikonda
Cholaeswarem as A.D.1028 (1023 + 5).
