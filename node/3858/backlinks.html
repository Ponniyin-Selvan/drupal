hi all! today seems to be the day of vsiting dilapidated temples for me. i
visited to on way to and back from Kanchi. Sadly both are so conspicuous and
near to main roads.
just today i ahd created a album of photos - dilapidated chola temple. who
ever comes across such temples may add to the album. hope how may are going
to be added. let's restrict to chola temples alone.
in the album i have added the photos of a late chola apsidal temple - so
many of them are in Tondaimandalam. the temple is at Sorpanacheri (?)
between Padappai and Walajabad, just 100m from the road. It belongs to
Vikrama Chola period (inf from Dr.TS / Rajavelu. and another on the near
Palaya sivaram on Kpuram-Chengai road. a saptamatrika temple with pallava
period matrikas.
it seeems that Dr TS took some initiative by advising the locals to do
something. thye promptly provided a asbestos roof over the linga.
if a restoration is attempted the temple can be completed in full as all
the stone members are available in situ.
but how?
the Dharmasthala trust by Vereendra Hegade in Karnataka is running a
organisation which is renovating dilapidated temples intially on 50/50
financing pattern, they did the work as per the norms laid out by asi with
the help of many retired asi officers chipping in with voluntary
service.Dharmasthala trust took care of their travel/hospitality.
the programme was so dedicated that their work became extremely popular,
the karnataka govt now sharing the 40/50 ofpublic portion.
so much so that the quality of restoration is so good that they are being
askled by the government to repair the "protected temple monumnets".
their simple work can be seen at www.sdmdharmothanatrust.org. it gives a
very simple account. but having seen and interacted with the organisers and
advisors, their work is hoghly commendable. they must have restormed many
ornate hoysala monumnets without bowing to modern repair practices of adding
unatural things to the old temple.
how is it possible? *SHEAR DEDICATION AND GOOD SUPPLY OF MONEY AND A LARGE
HEART OF VEREENDRA HEGADE, A JAIN BUT MAINTAINING A SIVAKSHETRA. HE NEVER
ASKED WHY ARE YOU CONSERVING THIS OR THAT TEMPLE. IF THE COMMITTE APPROVES
HE SAYS YES.*
IS THIS POSSIBLE IN TAMIL NADU? WE HAVE MANY DONORS. BUT THE PROBLEM LIES
IN THE FACT THEY GENERALLY REPEAT GENERALLY EXPECT MILAGE/VISIBILITY. SO
THEY PREFER A NEW TEMPLE RATHER THAN RESTORING AN OLD ONE AS PER ORIGINAL.
EVEN IF THEY REPAIR AN OLD ONE THEY FORCE TO ADD MODERN ENTITIES TO THE OLD.
IF HALF A DOZEN PERSON COMES OUT WITH THE CONVICTION OF HEGGADE THEN THE
SCENARIO WILL CHANGE DRASTICALLY.
HOW TO CHANGE THIS MIND SET? COME TO OUR EXPERIENCE. WHEN WE -ASI-
RESTORES A MONUMENT THE TEAM TAKES THE IMMEDIATE PLEASURE OF ACIEVEMENT. IT
IS A SIMPLE PSYCOLOGICAL THING. JUST THE CHILD IN OUR MIND. IF THE DONORS
ARE INCULCATED TO ENJOY THE PLEASURE ACHIEVEING SOMETHING THAT OTHERS CAN
NOT DO, THEY WILL COME FORWARD.
Secondly, we too must be professional like the active members of the
trust. dedicated and focussed. the money is there. the mind is waiting to be
"brainwashed". approach with dedication and focus. we will succeed. even if
we succeed in one case be happy. try next.
