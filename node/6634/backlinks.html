We are already aware that Rajaraja Chola - 1 was crowned as the king of
the Chola kingdom in the year A.D.985.

At the time when he became the king, the senior among his wives would
have been made his legitimate "Patta Mahishi" (Senior Queen).

We note it was Queen Panchavan Mahatheviyaar who figures as the earliest
in the first two Inscriptions, both being in the third year of his rule
at a time when he was referred to by his title "Mummudi Chola".

We also note among the inscriptions sequenced above, many of them refer
to Panchavan Mahatheviyaar than the other Queens

It is also noted from these inscriptions that the only Queen who was
known to have taken very keen interest in seeing the Sadaiyam Thirunaal
(the king's birthday) - in being celebrated in a big way, and
provided the required grants to the respective temples was the Panchavan
Mahatheviyaar.

It is not possible for the Queen DantisakthiVitanki alias LokaMahadevi
who has been referred for the first time in oneof the inscriptions of
the 9th year of his reign, and later only on inscriptions after the 21st
year of Rajaraja Chola - 1 to have been a "Patta Mahishi", when
we also know that three other Queens appearing in the inscriptions
before the 9th year of Rajaraja, withPanchavan Mahadeviyaar appearing
first.

Eventhe great Chola historian - NikantaSastri says "?.though we can
hardly be sure of it - Dantisakthi Vitanki also called Loka Mathadevi
appears to have occupied the most important place among them?", but
he doesnot say at all that Dantisakthi Vitanki was the
"PattaMahishi" - Chief Queen of Rajaraja - 1.

She having constructed a shrine in one temple, another complete temple
named Lokamahadeviswarem, and also having performed "Hema
Garba", all that happened only after the 21st year of Rajaraja's
rule, and she being prominent in the inscriptions only after this date
doesnot necessarily mean that she was the Chief Queen or "Patta Mahishi"
of Rajaraja Chola ? 1.

Furtherher donations to the Temple of Thanjai Rajarajeawaram
(Birahatheeswarer) temple was on the 29th year (A.D.1014), while the
donations made by the other "Penduhal" of Rajaraja - 1 belongs
to an earlier period of A.D.1011 clearly indicates she was not the
senior most among the Rajaraja's Queens.

Hence I am of the opinion that it was Panchavanmadeviyar who figures the
earliest among the Queens in the inscriptions of RajarajaChola -1 being
in his third year - was his"Patta Mahishi" or Chief Queen at the
time he became the Chola king in the year A.D.985.

She was followed by Vanavan Mahadevi the mother of Rajendra Chola - 1
who seems to be the second senior wife who appears for the first time in
the inscription of the 4thyear of Rajaraja Chola ? 1. The fact
remains that if he did not have a child by the "Patta Mahishi"
(Senior Queen), then it is the second senior wife's child normally
becomes the legitimate heir to the throne. As Rajendra Chola - 1 became
the next heir to the throne - with Panchavan Mahadeviyaar having no
children,in all probability his mother Vanavan Mahadevi has to be his
second senior wife of Rajaraja Chola ? 1.

Rajaraja Chola -1 in a tentative order of their seniority as follows.

Now coming to the subject of who was the "Patta Mahishi" of Rajaraja,
I have placed my views in my reply to Mr Sivapathasekaran "in the
light of the new Inscriptions discovered by him on Ulagamahadevipuram
and Vanavanmahadevi Chathurvedimangalam, etc", and brought to my
notice by him.

Further the Thiruvalanjuli Inscriptions you pointed out to me, on the
the "....royal status enjoyed by Danthi-sakthi and on her previleges,
(and that) she had her own karuma maarayam (officials) and was
also issuing royal orders on her own.... makes me tentatively think
that in the year of the Thiruvalanjuli Inscriptions being in the 29th
year of Rajaraja Chola, being the tailend of his rule and life time,
probably his health declined, and possibly Dantisakthi being in good
health and 'strong in mind' than Panchavanmahadevi or Vanavanmahadevi
was selected to assist Rajaraja in his administration along with the
Uvaraja the Rajendra Chola - 1, and possibly would have been given to
shoulder some of Rajaraja's responsibilities, which ended up with her
enjoying a royal status with much authority having her own karuma
maarayam and was issuing royal Orders on her own.

This could be the possible reason why she was figured only once for
the first time in the 9th year Inscription of Rajaraja Chola, and
suddenly from the 21st year onwards she was figured more prominantly
in the Rajaraja's inscriptions.

However this needs further critical study, to acertain whether my
above inferance is correct or not.
