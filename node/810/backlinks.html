Hi guys,

I don't know to write descrptively like pavithra but I just have made an attempt to. Hope this will not be boring for any of u.

Day before yesterday Myself, Pavithra and Kamalakkanan met near Besant Nagar Pillayar Koil by around 10’o clock in the morning. Then came Mr. Sundar Bharatwaj (SB) and Thiru Balakumaran. SB was trying to contact Mr. Nagasamy all the while thru’ his Mobile but then the phone was engaged. After sometime we all proceeded to Mr. Nagasamy’s house. Unfortunately, Nagasamy had left to attend one function. So we could not meet him then. We met his son and telling him that we will call up later and fix up an appointment in the evening, we left his house. We spent sometime chatting under the shade of few trees in front of his house. Then all of us except Thiru balakumaran gathered at SB’s office to discuss about the proposed activities of this group and about the proposed symposium.

SB told that we need to be clear in our thoughts as to what our goals are and what are we trying to achieve by all these activities like this symposium. He told this is not an easy task. If it is just a passing interest without any commitment or clear vision, then it is not worth to call all great historians and have a symposium. If we are all really willing to contribute something, then we should also prepare to manage our resources and time effectively so as to do something worthwhile amidst all our other commitments (family and work or education). Also we need to clearly list out what we are trying to achieve in more clear terms so that when we introduce about our group to all the learned persons, it should create some interest in them. Also we need to prepare ourselves by reading some books like Neelaganta Saastri’s and Varalaru (magazine published by kalaikaovan), so that we can talk with all the learned people. So finally we decided that we will postpone the symposium by few more weeks to get everything organized, and give some time to prepare ourselves for the event by reading some books. So probably we will have the symposium in June.

So finally we three managed to meet Mr. Nagasamy in the evening by 4.30. First thing that captured our eyes in his house was the numerous collection of books that were arranged in steel racks in one room. We entered his office like room, Even there except for the place where there was a door all the four walls had racks full of books ranging from history, culture to music and dance to linguistics to spiritual. You name a book and its there in his place.

He is a very interesting person. When we went there he was reading one book with the help of a magnifying glass. He is now 73 and even at this old age he seemed to be very enthusiastic and energetic. He told us a lot about Tanjore temple, kalvettus, cheppedugal etc., etc., I will list out in detail about our conversation later. He seemed to be interested to know about our group. He gave some ideas as to what we can do and promised to extend his help and give more ideas and suggestions whenever needed. SB bought some of his books and other old books that were reprinted by him, and gave two books for our group. One of that by name Vinayaka Puranam by Thiruvaadurai aadeenam Kachiappa Munivar, written 125 years ago is rewritten by Pungatthur Arumuga Mudhaliar and is reprinted now by Mr. Nagasamy. There ia a very beautiful picture of Vinayaga on a Peocock Vaaganam in it. The other book is Sol Maalai written by Mr. Nagasamy. This book gives some beautiful illustrations on various temples and the devotional songs (Paakkal) connected with these temples and its history.

We asked him, in what ways our group can help him and other historians. And what activities do we need to concentrate to make the people realize about our culture and history.

He told us that initially to start with as a learning process we can visit few of the near by temples (they need not be old) and draw a map about that temple and get whatever particulars we can get about it and give a detailed description about the temple. Also he told we can try and collect coins of our age pertaining to different period say from 1975 to 2003 and try to make a presentation on what is the significant happening in that year that can be inferred from the coin and what all can be inferred from a coin. That the coins of different age can be identified by looking at the material. For example old coins are thick and hard while new coins are more polished and smooth. Like this we can take some old coins pertaining to chola period and compare coins of different age. This way people will get interested. He told he will get us the old chola period coins.

He pointed to his collection of books and told that he does not know what to do with them or who will take care of them and put them to good use after his life time. In his opinion the interest to read all the great works has diminished to a great extent and he does not know what will become of them. Myself and Kamal thought that he should give up that feeling seeing our gorup's activities. We should make everyone realize that current younger generation is reliable and that all the great works will not go waste.

Normally he does not talk for a long time. Even to retain him for 10 minutes is difficult according to SB. But we had a very rare opportunity to interact with him for more than 3 1/2 hours.

After that we disbursed. When I went back to home it was almost 10’o clock in the night. And as expected I had a good scolding from my parents for suttufying the whole day. But I had a readymade answer. I am doing all this for a good cause. Foregoing just one days rest for this worthwhile cause is not a big thing.

Regards,

Poonguzhali
