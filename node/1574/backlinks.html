Sudharsanam
A Centre for Arts and Culture
Pudukkottai, Tamilnadu

Southern Centre for Jainology and
Sittannavasal Monument Complex,
Pudukkottai District
(A joint proposal by Sudharsanam and Research Foundation for Jainology)

A SKETCH

Southern Centre for Jainology, Sittannavasal Monument Complex
The project proposes building a HERITAGE COMPLEX in the vicinity of the
Sittannavasal monuments with the following.
1. Monument Complex
? Recreated Arivar-Koil
? Four Heritage Pavilions for permanent exhibitions
? Temple Architecture
? Evolution of Scripts
? Development of Painting
? Jain Contribution to Tamil Culture
? First two to be Open-Pungam trees (to start with under thatched roof)
v Flexible, can be rearranged, easy to add exhibits, add new themes forming
extra loops, natural background; part of heritage walk
? The last two to make use of the rear surfaces of the recreated Arivar-Koil
? Administrative Office
? Public Utilities
? Gift Shop
2. Research Centre and Campus
? Library
? DTP, Reprography and Internet Connection
? Lecture/Talk/Meditation halls
? Office
? Apartments and Dormitory
? Kitchen
3. Entertainment Complex
? Camp/Picnic Facilities
(Challenges: Attractive and suitable names for the pavilions)

Monument Complex
RECREATED ARIVAR-KOIL

1. Arivar-Koil recreated to full-scale in stone, cement and mortar done to
stone-finish, with paintings done as much as possible true to the original
through research.
(The rear of the Koil may house the two pavilions - Painting pavilion and
Pavilion of Jaina Contribution - and, in order to provide realistic
elevation, may accommodate all offices and other built up area of the
Monument Complex as well as the Research Centre.)
2. Preview Chamber, positioned at the entry to the Recreated Arivar Koil, to
provide a panoramic view of the original, using optical effect on a
photograph.

Monument Complex
HERITAGE PAVILION ONE
Mandiram
TEMPLE ARCHITECTURE

1. OPEN-Exhibits - Individual exhibits in High Relief
Done in Stucco on granite blocks taken from quarry
Models
Kailasanatha Temple of Ellora
Lingaraja Temple at Bhubaneswar
Darasuram
Venugopla Temple in Somnathpur (Karnataka)

Secondary Pavilion (for more serious visitors)
Iconographic Models
Agama/Sastra Illustration
Temples
Gopuram

Second Stage - in Open Arjuna's Penance from Mahabalipuram
Panels from Elephanta Caves

Monument Complex
HERITAGE PAVILION TWO
Aksharam
EVOLUTION OF INDIAN SCRIPTS

1. OPEN-Exhibits - Development of Scripts
Tamil Brahmi, Vattezhuththu, Tamil, Grantha & Devanagari
- Done in stucco on granite blocks
Historic Inscriptions:
Asokan Edicts from Dhaulia, Girnar Inscriptions of Rudradaman, etc
Tamil, Grantha and Sanskrit Inscriptions from South-east Asian Countries

Second Stage - in Open Air
Inscriptions from other Indian Languages

2. IN COVERED SHED
Development of each letter of Indian Scripts
Comparison among all Brahmi-derived Scripts
Development of Scripts of Tamilnadu (The Sudharsanam Panels)
Development Scripts of Ancient Cultures - Sumerian, Egyptian, Chinese,
Meso-american
Development of Esoteric Scrips - Tibetan and Siddhamatrika
Development of Arabic Script and Calligraphy

(Challenges: Writing Legends - Historical and Epigraphical; method and
medium. How about using coal tar? Stone or Metal?
How about donors for other languages and for Historical Inscriptions, for
example, Orissa Government for Dhaulia, etc.)

Monument Complex
HERITAGE PAVILION THREE
Chaitram
DEVELOPMENT OF INDIAN PAINTING

1. IN COVERED SHED
Representative Selections in Reproductions (laminated, framed and mounted)
of

Pre-historic paintings - Rock Art
2 cent. BC - 2nd century AD - SATAVAHANA: Ajanta (caves 9 & 10)
1 - 3 centuries - KUSHANA: Only example is found in Central Asia
4 - 6 centuries - GUPTA: Bagh caves in Madhya Pradesh
4 - 6 centuries - VAKATAKA; Ajanta (all except caves 9 & 10)
6 - 8 centuries - WESTERN CHALUKYA; Vaishnava Cave at Vatapi (the earliest
Hindu painting of King Mangalesan)
7 - 9 centuries - PALLAVA; Kailasanatha temple at Kanchipuram
7 - 9 centuries - EARLY PANDYA; Sittannavasal
8 - 10 centuries - RASHTRAKUTA; Ellora Caves
9 - 13 centuries - IMPERIAL CHOLA; Brihadiswara temple at Tanjavur
9 - 16 centuries - PALA OF BENGAL; Paintings on Palm-leaves
11 - 13 centuries - HOYSALA; Paintings on palm-leaves
11 - 13 centuries - KAKATIYA; Temple on Tripurantaka Hill, Andhra Pradesh
14 - 17 centuries - VIJAYANAGARA; Virupaksha temple at Hampi and Virabhadra
temple at Lepakshi
----
MINIATURE PAINTINGS
11 - 15 centuries - MEDIEVAL WESTERN; llustrations on palm-leaves (Beginning
of Miniature paintings)
16 - 18 centuries - MOGHUL; Miniature paintings
16 - 19 centuries - RAJASTHANI; Mewar, Bundi, Kota, Bikanir, Jodhpur,
Kishangarh and Kachhawa Schools - Miniature paintings
17 - 18 centuries - PAHARI; Basholi, Gular, Kangra, Jammu, Chamba and Tehri
Garhwal Schools - Miniature paintings
17 - 19 centuries - DECCANI; Ahmadnagar, Golkonda and Bhijapur - Miniature
paintings
----
17 - 18 centuries - NAYAKA; Temples at Thanjavur, Chidambaram and Madurai
(This style led to Thanjavur paintings and Kalamkari paintings)
Kalighat Paintings
Bengal School
Modern Painting

(Challenges: Mode of preparing? Reproductions laminated? Layouts: tightly
packed path or around open courtyard? Natural lights? Natural ventilation
(KaRRu-p-pandhal)? Avoid dust and moisture? How about a floor above to
improve elevation?)

Gnanam
JAIN CONTRIBUTION TO TAMIL CULTURE

Not thought of yet
