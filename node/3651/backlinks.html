Dear Venkat,

There are more to this :

1. Whether Avudayar of 54' perifery - with Ghomukahm (Abhisheka Water
outlet) is of Single piece OR made of multiple pieces ?

One Scholar says made of 8 pieces ..
Other says Single piece ...

2. If Single piece - how to put the Avudayar after constructing sanctum ?

One Scholar says Avudayar was constructed .. Lingam was erected before
construction of Vimanam .. But the Life / Godliness was not inculcated
.. instead this was done on a small Linga near by ... Sands filled
above Lingam to complete construction of Vimanam .. !! AMAZING ! ?

Another adds, there are inscriptional evidences that Pujas performed
during the period of construction itself because of this .... This
theory is also under Debate among Scholars ....

One Scholar says the Agamic process was followed in line with
MAGUDAGAMAM ... where it is permitted to erect Avudayar / Lingam - and
the Vimanam will look like a MAGUDAM / KREEDAM ! One Scholar
expresses reservations on Magudagamam.

I have personally come across of mentions of MAGUDAGAMAMs from the
Books of Thiruvavuthurai Adheenam and also another Scholar pointed out
that Magudamam is already exists in Print Media .. one has to search
and locate ..

These details assume relevance because KUMBABISHEKAM was less
prominent since already Pujas were in progress ..

RRC's Thiruvisalur inscriptions - Rj C's Thiruvlanchuzhi - RRC 29th yr
& RjC 3rd Yr inscriptions - 1014 clearly show RRC was alive till then
whereas Kumbabhishekam took place on the 25th year 255th day of RRC -
1010 AD ..

So the theory that he did not live to see Kumbishekam is Wrong.

Re unfinished portions of the temples .. will write my views again..

With best regareds,

sps

Dear Chandra,

That place is not a temple at all. Couple of months before
starting
varalaaru.com, we visited that place along with kalaikkovan and
nalini. Just lingam and avudaiyaar were there in the paddy field.
We
digged out the lingam and there were no structures found. There
were
many such lingams in paddy fields of that area. Probably that
lingam
might have come there because of flood.

========================
Kudanthai N. Sethuraman (of Raman & Raman Transport) wrote on this
place in his book about a 20 yrs back.

There was a press news by some local person 5 - 6 yrs back.

========================

Some historians (they didnt tell the name) visited that place
before
us told the land owner that govt will acquire his land for
building
Rajarajan memorial there. So, he was afraid and hesitated to allow
us inside his field. Nearly 1 hr of explanation that we are not
from
govt and wont do anything to his land, he allowed. At the end, we
convinced him that there is no evidence to prove it as a temple
and
nothing will happen to his land. He was relieved then.

This was reported in the very first issue of varalaaru.com. Follow
the link http://www.varalaaru.com/Default.asp?articleid=11

Thanks
Kamal
