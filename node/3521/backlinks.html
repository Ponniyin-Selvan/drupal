E-Mail service links Tamils away from state

published in The New Indian Express on 3.06.2005 Orissa Edition

When B.Parthasarathy,a government emplyee was transferred to his home city chennai after eight years of stay here, he wished to say bye to all the near and dear ones. But how could he reach out to so many people ?

similarly , when an ailing Tamil girl badly needed a bottle of a rare group of blood, here parents did not panic.

what came to the aid of these people in distress was the three year old kalingatamil email service being run by city based businessman s.balasubramani

A formar secretary of Bhubaneswar tamil sangam (BTS) , a communtity cultural organisation , balasubramani's humble efforts have linked nearly one lakhs email ids of tamils who are linked orissa and oriyas who are away in tamil nadu through 231 e groups including oriya soceites of Delhi , Chennai, Mumbai,Surat and USA.

The idea struck me when i opened an exclusive shop for the south indian community of the city at jayadev vihar, As the former secretary of BTS, I knew almost all the tamilshere and thus my shop became the meeting point for most communtiy members often asked for my help
for connecting with the fellow members.people went back to tamil nadu also wanted to touchwith us,therefore i started this mail service explained balasubramani.

what started as an experiment soon clicked with the community members wanted it to be an ideal pltform for exchange of information and ideas. people posted me thought provoking articles and creative writings to be shared by the users.organisers of different events also sent me information in advance .When Tsunamai struck the tamilnadu coast, people here could gather a lot of information through this service" he informed.

kaling_tamil@yahoo.co.in e mail service has earned appreciation from its oriya users by carrying regular features on orissa'life style,heritage and culture."Orissa has been my second home and my motto isa to project a positive image of the state for the tamils all over the world who are connected through e mail service." claimed balasubramani who spares atleast two hours daily to maintain the service.

Writing Tamil History: Post National Perspectives

More than two millennia of documentary evidences show several undercurrents in Tamil historiography: (a) classical ideas; (b) Colonialism, Nationalism and Orientalism; (c) the Dravidian Movement and (d) post colonial competition of linguistic, religious, caste and class identities. The legacies of all of them operate in varying degrees in determining the Tamil psyche today. The contemporary world witnesses redefinition of nationalism, emergence of culture-identities, need for a global outlook and above all, a globally spread Tamil population which is aware of its identity. This paper attempts to kindle a debate on the historiographical needs of changed realities.
