Dear Gr,

Thanks for making me read

In Vol 5, Ch 76 (or 78) during the conversation between Kundavai &
Vdthvn it is stated that:

1. Chakravarthy (Sundara Chola) has accorded to make Vanarkula prince
Vdthvn as King of Vallam;

2. Parthivendran desired independence to Pallavas & wanted to marry
Kundavai. Chakravarthy refers to K. She replies that she does not
want to Chozha land... Means Vallam as part of Chozha empire..
adjacent to Vaidhumbas...

The entire chapter is interesting - remonatic exchanges between K &
V. In fact, since I could not go to Cauvery belt - particularly
Ammamandabam on the 18th of Aadi, I took this opportunity and read
the opening chapters of

Kudanthai Sethuraman quotes two Vallam inscriptions in The Cholas
Chapter Madurantaka Kandradithan.

Re Tkvlr inscriptions : " Puliyai payantha pon maan" is the quote for
Vaanavan madevi. Kalki has quoted this w.r.t. VM. Shall notify
Chptr latter.

I got a glimpse of vandhiyathevan's past yesterday while reading
kudanthai's book. The rashtrakuta king Krishna III has been referred
to as "Vallavaraiyar" in one of the Aditya / Parantaka inscriptions.
Hence it is likely that vandhiyathevan might have been a descendent
of rashtrakutas. No marriage happened in cholas without political
gains, in striking contrast to the picture kalki paints ! Personally I
would like to believe the kundavai - vandhiyathevan
love, though history stands against it.

Will share the details of the inscriptions soon.
