Dear Kathie,

THIRUKANDIYUR is much discussed in our Group.

Siva temple puranam says : Brahma did not utter the meaning of OHM

and hence out of 5 heads, one was chopped off by Siva .. an act of

Bravery by Siva - Veera attanam ( out of 8 such temples) !

Just opposite there is a Vishnu temple : Puranam says : the Skull of

Brfahma got stuck to Siva who performend penance here and the skull

got detached. !

To cite it is cutomery to link the details ...

Like wise there is Thanthondri malai... Hillock .. wherein a Vishnu

(Venkatachalapthy) temple exists.. This is between Karur - Trichy.

The THANTHONDREESWARAR .. Siva story goes like this.. related to

THAYUMANVAR episode..

Thayumanavar Sannidhi is located in Golden Rock, Trichy. In this

Lelithangurapallvag raham by Mahendra I is caved (this is also much

discussed in our Group) .. after he changed faith as GUNABARA from

Jainism to Saivism at Thiruvadhigai (near Pandrutti) !

Here he calls Lord Siva as Gunabara !

nearby is Thayumanvar. . THAY... MOTHER.. Lord Siva has served as

MOTHER too. ( Thayum .. Mothher too !).

A pregnant lady - in advanced stage, unable cross river Cauvery, was

helped by an ELDERLY WOMAN ( Maruthuvachi ... Marundhu is Medicine..

Uneducated but experienced Maruthuvachi - WOMEN - with knowledge of

Medicine Science or Signs / symptoms !) - together crossed Cauvery

and gave birth to child and later on disappeared and the WOMAN

believed to be Lord Siva..

This episode of delivery of child is supposed to have taken place

near Uraiyur - and the Lord Siva who APPEARED there instantly is

named as THANTHONDREESWARAR ..

THERE is a separate Siva temple near Panchavarneswarar temple in

Uraiyur !~ Does not seem to be have been hymned in Thevaram ... !

Uraiyur Naachiyar temple is also having interesting epsidoes...

Nammazhawar is said to have represented Nachiar to Ranganathar' s

RANGANAYAKI in Stirangam - which forms basis for ARAYAR SEVAI..

which is conducted all these Centuries !

regards/ sps

town)[36] Tanchapuri (i.e., Tanjore) which was picturesque to the

eyes, graceful curls, a cloth covering (her body), and sandal paste as

(the weight of) the (whole) earth resplendent with (her) garment of
