Re Rajaraja Pallippadai : PAALKULATHAMMAN koil is near Ammankudi
(Krishnan Raman's place) - Udaylur - about 3 Kms South East of
Pazhayarai. Two stone pillars contain inscriptions: A commander of
Kulothunga, VIRADHAMIRUNDHU rebuilding a place - which was built by a
Commander of Rajendra and dilapated (Sidhalmadaindhu). These were
seen by Dr.Kudavoil - Dr. Kkvn - Dr. Nagasamy - Dr. Rajavelu & by
similar scholars! They do not accredit this place as Pallippadai of
Rajraja! As per Sundarapandian meykeerthi, he has destroyed every
single pillar of Chozha Kingdom after 400 yrs of vengence - except
the Mandabam from where Karikalan gave away the gifts to the author
of Pattinappalai! Still some scholars are working on finding this
unique place - about 3 Kms radius of Pazhayarai - near Panchavanmadevi's
Pallippadai - Thiruvalanchuzhi belt!

Maldieves island details are very informative .. Similarly
Brahmadesam inscriptions..!

Rajendra born in 971 & was about 40 yr old when became King .. This
may be the reason for Kalki to write that Vanathi descided not to sit
in the throne! She married Arumozhi - gave birth to Rajendra and
they were out of power till 985. Olagamadevi marriage would have
been a political marriage. A Deal (arrangement) might have been
made. Rajendra to succeed Rajaraja - but Olagamadevi would have been
Pattamahishi! Dr. Sethuraman's theory - Gangai kondan etc., - not
well supported. Including Dr. Nagaswamy, eveybody confirms that
there was ONLY ONE SON to Rajaraja. Dr.Kudavoil says three
daughters. Dr. Kalakkovan says two daughters.

It is Vanathi @ Vanamadevi - Rajendra's mother
and Danthisakthividangi @ Olgamadevi @ Thirubuvanamadevi - Pattamahishi.

Request Srihari to post more such informative mails...
That reminds me : Yesterday I visited Satthan Pazhiyili's temple in
Narthamalai ... near Pudukkottai.. It was excellent .. rare piece
constructed on the rocks...

Fondly, SB

In conversant with Actual Historical details and capable for highlighting
theories with evidence. My views are speculative to provide more ideas to
ponder on :
======================================

1. Aditha Karikala died in 969. Against mass opinion (Thiruvalangadu
copper), Arumozhi relinquished his Crown in favour of Uthama - who co-ruled
between 969 - 971. Sundara died in 971. Uthama's 16th year of Rule is 985 -
when Rajaraja ascended to throne along with Uthama till 987.

WHERE DID SUNDARA LIVE WHEN UTHAMA CO-RULED?

When Sundara died 971 AD.. he was denoted as "Pon Maligai Thunjiya Thevar"
(nothing to do with Kanchi). This Ponmaligai is supposed to have been in
NANDHIPURAM - According to Srivilliputhur inscriptions of Sundara PANDYA
(?!)- quoted by Dr. Kudavoil Balu. This is Nandhipuram is near
Thirukkandiyur - about 12 Kms - North-East of Thanjavur. Rettakkoil @
Veerasingampettai - is where Sundara + Vanamadevi pallippadais are supposed
to have been located - according to Thirukkandiyur inscriptions.

WHERE DID ARUMOZHI @ RAJARAJA LIVE UTHAMA RULED? (971 - 985 AD)

THIS IS THE GREATEST QUESTION - ANSWER TO WHICH WILL UNFOLD SEVERAL
INTERESTING FACTS IN THE GLORIOUS HISTORY OF TAMILS.
I LEAVE THIS QUESTION UNANSWERED - FOR OTHERS VIEWS.

Was he married then? Yes.
Did Rajendra born : Yes.

It is interesting to note that near MEALAKKADAMBUR - (where Adithakarikala
is alleged to have been murdered) - there is a "Thirumoolasthanam temple"
and a hamelt known as RAJENDRA CHOZHANAGAM !

Near by is Nanmbiandar Nambi's Pollappillayar! (Kadambur - Chidambaram road -
East of Veeranam - 6 Kms).

When Nambiandar Nambi was a child, his father - temple priest - asked him
to show Neyvethyam - offerings to Pillayar which the child did. When
Pillayar did not consume, the child cried & pillayar consumed. When father
returned he was disturbed that the child was telling lie after eating
offerings himself. When father and child went to the temple again, the
child offered and Pillayar consumed. Such was the Greatness of Nambiandar
Nambi and hence Pollappillayar!.

Nambiandar Nambi was supposed to have been instrumental in brining out
Thevarams by Rajaraja (Film Rajaraja) - who made 3 Golden idols of Sundarar -
Sambasndar and Appar - and convinced Thillai 3000 Dikshithars to open the
doors to find Thevarams.

There is disagreement that Nambiandar Nambi and some of the Scholars.
