WHERE OUR GROUP MAIL IS FORWARDED BY A MEMBER TO A FRIENDS' CIRCLE

UNRELATED TO OUR GROUP. THAT SOMEONE CORRESPONDED DIRECTLY WITH THE
ORIGINATOR OF OUR GROUP.

Tho' we agree that once we put something in the Web, THAT becomes
uncontrollable. Atleast we can take the precaution of NOT
HIGHLIGHTING THE SOURCE / ORIGIN..so that others will not react
directly.

This is mainly to avoid recurrence...

Well.. we do learn from our experiences more...

write to nataraj or not.

Hi,

If we feel any of friends would be interested in this kind of
discussions, we should get them to sign on to the group. Being in
the group gives them access to the entire discussion and lessens
chances of being quoted out of context. They can also play a healthy
part in the discussions and content can also be `moderated'

Idea is we are all part of a thoughts and ideas - and we should follow some basic rules. we are
sharing our email addresses, and even at times phone numbers. Why do
we do that - because in a forum, there is decorum maintained and you
are feeling safe within its confines. These are things that you
wouldnt want to share with all and sundry. I feel within the forum,
if you want to use something, you could always write to the author
and seek permission to reproduce.

rgds

--- In ponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, "S Sampath"

My views:

1. It is perfectly alright to share the message with friends and
like
minded lists as the message ends there.

2. One has to be careful if the message is posted in a different
group
or forum; the message may be mis/ partially quoted and out of
context; in turn the message may invite criticism and even abusive replies,

if

the issue is controversial. The original author of the message may
not
even be aware of of the responses or criticism his message is
getting
and he will not be in a position to rebut or offer explanations.
The
original message might migrate in cyber space and may become
unrecognisable after a few postings.

It is preferable to get the first poster's permission if the
message is
likely to generate discussion (hot!!). Of course it goes without
saying
that due credit is given to the author of the message.

Sampath
