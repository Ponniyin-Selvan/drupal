Ram Setu not man-made structure: NASA
Arup Chanda
Tribune News Service

Chennai, July 28
The NASA has stated in an e-mail that the series sand bars known as
Adams Bridge is nothing but natural rock-like formations. This has put
the Vishwa Hindu Parishad (VHP) in a tight spot as it had objected to
demolition of Ramar Bridge, which they believe was constructed by Lord
Hanuman and his Monkey's Army to help Lord Ram and others to cross
over to Sri Lanka to retrieve Sita, held captive by Ravana.

The fact that the series of sand bars beneath the sea between India
and Sri Lanka that form a rock-like formation - known as Adams Bridge
- is a natural phenomenon negates it being man made. This was stated
by CEO and managing director of Sethusamudram Corporation Ltd (SCL) N.
K. Raghupathy today.

The VHP, which is opposed to the Sethusamudram project, had been using
NASA's satellite pictures to prove that it was indeed the 'Ram Setu'
or the bridge that Lord Rama built.

An official of the SCL, which is dredging Adams Bridge as part of the
Sethusamudram ship canal project off the Tamil Nadu coast, had written
to NASA, seeking clarifications in this regard.

In a reply through e-mail, NASA's Johnson Space Centre stated: "The
chain of small islets connecting India and Sri Lanka are real
geographical features that have been mapped for centuries. Chains of
islands form from a variety of natural geological processes, and their
occurrence is not evidence of any human activity."

Some Hindu outfits have been objecting to the dredging of Palk Bay.

Raghupathy said the aeronautical agency's information confirmed the
argument that Adams Bridge was a natural formation.

He said he was just informing the media about NASA's e-mail and would
prefer not to comment on the Ram Setu campaign as the matter was
pending before the Supreme Court.

N. Ganesan

Ministry of Shipping, Road Transport & Highways

MERCANTILE MARINE DEPARTMENTS MODERNIZED
------------ --------- --------- --------- --------- --------- -
----------

17:30 IST
Rajya Sabha

The Government has taken several steps to modernize the
functioning of the Mercantile Marine Departments and also for
ensuring efficiency and transparency in the working of its five
Regional level Mercantile Marine Department (MMD) offices and 13
District level MMD offices covering all the major ports in the
country.

The Mercantile Marine Departments administer
various Merchant Shipping Laws and Rules under Merchant Shipping Act,
1958 and The steps to increase efficiency include:

(i) A Citizen Charter providing the details of various functions
and target periods within which various services are to be rendered
to the shipping industry without causing any inconvenience and delay
has been made available at all mercantile marine Departments and on
the website of Directorate General of Shipping.

(ii) A number of surveys and inspections have been assigned to
recognize classification societies
==============================================================

(iii) A redressal mechanism has been set up to look into the
genuine complaints of the shipping industry.

(iv) With the introduction of an e-Governance programme for the
Mercantile Marine Department, Mumbai, Directorate General of Shipping
and allied offices, most of the facilitative and regulatory approvals
can be applied online by intending enterprises. Further, an online
redressal system has also been introduced in order to reduce delays
in the disposal of complaints.

(v) A quality management system with the objective of providing
quick and timely service to the shipping industry has been introduced
in the Directorate General of Shipping.

In addition to above, the Right to Information Act has been
implemented in letter and spirit thereby ensuring greater
transparency to public. Customer friendly forms have been developed
and are available on the website. Most of the services can be availed
by online submission of forms.

This information was given by the Minister of Shipping, Road
Transport and Highways, Thiru T.R. Baalu in a written reply in the
Rajya Sabha today.

Ram Setu not man-made structure: NASA
Arup Chanda
Tribune News Service

Chennai, July 28
The NASA has stated in an e-mail that the series sand bars known as
Adams Bridge is nothing but natural rock-like formations. This has
put
the Vishwa Hindu Parishad (VHP) in a tight spot as it had objected
to
demolition of Ramar Bridge, which they believe was constructed by
Lord
Hanuman and his Monkey's Army to help Lord Ram and others to cross
over to Sri Lanka to retrieve Sita, held captive by Ravana.

The fact that the series of sand bars beneath the sea between India
and Sri Lanka that form a rock-like formation - known as Adams
Bridge
- is a natural phenomenon negates it being man made. This was
stated
by CEO and managing director of Sethusamudram Corporation Ltd
(SCL) N.
K. Raghupathy today.

The VHP, which is opposed to the Sethusamudram project, had been
using
NASA's satellite pictures to prove that it was indeed the 'Ram
Setu'
or the bridge that Lord Rama built.

An official of the SCL, which is dredging Adams Bridge as part of
the
Sethusamudram ship canal project off the Tamil Nadu coast, had
written
to NASA, seeking clarifications in this regard.

In a reply through e-mail, NASA's Johnson Space Centre stated: "The
chain of small islets connecting India and Sri Lanka are real
geographical features that have been mapped for centuries. Chains
of
islands form from a variety of natural geological processes, and
their
occurrence is not evidence of any human activity."

Some Hindu outfits have been objecting to the dredging of Palk Bay.

Raghupathy said the aeronautical agency's information confirmed the
argument that Adams Bridge was a natural formation.

He said he was just informing the media about NASA's e-mail and
would
prefer not to comment on the Ram Setu campaign as the matter was
pending before the Supreme Court.

N. Ganesan
