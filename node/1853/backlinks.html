That was quite an interesting analysis! I never noticed the parallels
between the two literary works before.

I must first confess that I have not really read kambaramayanam
(except for the odd passage that we studied in high school). I have
only attempted reading Rajaji's version (also called chakravarthy
thirumagan..?) thrice.. [attempted because i somehow got stuck at the
46th chapter every time, titled kamba chithiram, ironically.. :)] So
I guess I don't really have an opinion on it yet.

In any case, I am not sure if Kalki really wanted to "hero-worship"
Arulmozhi. Though his initial build-up to the character does seem
like he intended to portray Arulmozhi as a larger-than-life
personality, he does not dwell very long in it. He soon shows the
more down-to-earth, human nature of the character. He does not
hesitate to depict him as just another man with his own share of
follies and insecurities.

I suppose there have been several discussion threads on the same vein
in this forum regarding kalki's characterisations. I do believe Kalki
excels in portraying the good as well as the not-so-good facets of
his characters. I feel that is what makes them all the more human and
believable. Each character is fleshed out well with several shades of
goodness and badness (if there is such a word :P). The only other
author whom i know does that is Jane Austen. She produces the same
kind of multi-layered, nuanced characters whom you grow to love, as
much for their quirkiness as for their greatness. :)

So, I do not think they are really comparable on the "hero-worship"
front. What say? :)

Keep up the good work! :)

Hi members.I am sampath,a new member to this group.First thing I
did
after joining was to read all the previous mails and I am
fascinated
by the amount of knowledge this group members possess.

In my first post I would give my views on what made PS great.I have
read many epics in English and Tamil and in my opinion Ponniyin
selvan can sit at par with any epic in any language in the
world.The
closest competition to Ponniyin selvan will come from another tamil
epic kambaramayanam.

I have read most of shakespheres works and have always wondered
what
made them great.Most of them were ordinary movie stories,for
example
Romeo and juliet was nothing but a love story.Plain ordinary love
story.What made it great?

Its only the way in which the story was presented made it great.The
words and the play writing skills of shakesphere made it an epic.If
I can use a parallel from cinema,its not the story that makes a
movie succesful.Its the screenplay that makes the movie
succesful.We
have seen great writers transforming ordinary stories into great
epics by just presenting it in a beautiful way.

Similiarly Kalki has made this ordinary fight for asendency to
throne between 3 heirs a mega epic by presenting it in a beautiful
way.But the only pity is that it can be understood only by people
who know Tamil.Even if we translate it into English the charm goes
away.If we picturize it also the charm goes away.

Another aspect I love in Ponniyin Selvan is characterization.The
way
Kalki has created each characters has been magnificient.But in this
aspect I would place him a bit lower than Kambar(Sorry hardcore
Kalki fans,but I'm sure Kalki will agree with me.If we say he is
next to kambar he will feel proud)

The reason I say is as follows.I would like to compare both on
characterization.Kalki's creation of characters like Vandiya
thevan,
Ponniyin selvan,manimekalai was great.Even though he worshipped
arulmozhi in the novel it did not look artificial.Tamils have
worshipped MGR and know what hero worship is.Kalki also made the
Hero worship worthwhile.He made us believe that Arulmozhi deserved
the hero worship he had.

Kambar also had limits and he also had to hero worship.
But within this limits what a beautiful job he performed?What a
characterization he created for everyone?For example when Rama shot
vali hiding behind a tree valmigi writes as if vali abused Rama and
in that scence made rama appear as a villian and vali as a hero.But
kambar changes that.

Vali calls rama as "Why did you shoot me from behind?Kathir
mathiyam
pol mugathoye.."--Is this abuse?Even when Vali is down in earth he
couldnt stop admiring rama,who killed him.So kambar hails rama in
valis words.Will anybody hail his killer as "kathir mathiyam pol
mugathoye.."(Im not sure about exact wordings here,im sorry)

In every abuse of vali Kambar adds a praise for Rama.There he
glorofies Rama better than even valmigi.

And Valmigi wrote as ravana lifted seetha by his hands.But Kambar
couldnt tolerate the thought that anyone can even touch seeta.So
he
wrote as "Ravana lifted the hut along with the land and carried
seetha without touching her".He upholds tamil culture there.

The way he has characterized Bharatha makes us wonder whether Rama
is the hero of the epic or bharatha is.See the way in which he
builds the Bharatha character.

When Dasaratha tells Rama that Bharatha will be the king,Rama goes
and tells his mother kousalya that bharatha will be the new
king.See
what words Kambar puts in kousalyas mouth.

"Bharatha is three times better than you to rule this kingdom".

See this.Even after hearing that her only son is being robbed of
his
throne the words that come from kousalyas mouth are hailing
bharatha.See further how Kambar builds bharathas character.

When Bharatha goes to forest to meet ram he meets Guha.After
hearing
why Bharatha has come to the forest Guha is fascinated.He hugs
Bharatha,cries and says "Even thousand Rama's are not equal to you".

See this.Now Bharatha is hailed as 1000 times better than Rama,the
hero of the epic.But the climax is the pinching one.

When Rama doesnt return after 14 years Bharatha is about to jump
into fire.Then kousalya prevents him and says "Dont do it my son.I
can live without Rama but not without you.PALAKODI ramnum unakku
iniyavano?"

Here Bharatha is portrayed as better than "Palakodi Raman",the hero
of the epic.This is how Kambar built each character with the
limited
freedom he had.

Kambar Vs Kalki - another take

Dear Sampath

Kalki too must have fallen into the trap.He lifted arulmozhi very
high initially but i dont see him dropping arulmozhi down
anywhere.Arulmozhi is not shown with ordinary human weaknesses.He
never loses a sword fight,he shows the right emotions at the right
time,his aura is maintained till the end.He doesnt want to sit in
throne till the fag end and even that decision is taken only since
he wanted to free vanthiyathevan.

I dont remember arulmozhi doing any "mistakes" in PS.Maybe in last
parts he shows a more down to earth arulmozhi,but still srulmozhi
is elevated above other mortals.

One of the greatest feats of Kalki is his charectors are with
faults...Most happening in life will not occur if we all were

faultless...

All the other authors have hero worship but not kalki...

Ilanchezhian outwits Tiberious ,Ili Yazu,Yavana maruthuvan, Kadal
Kollaiyar and Irungovel et al ....thats hero worship..

Ilaya pallavan and Anabayan escape from kalingam in a daring
attempt...Ilayapallavan outwis beeman,balavarman and jeya varman and
all the other bad elements thats hero worship....

Let me remind you Vandiyadevan is as much a hero of the story as
Arulmozhi Kaki himself says so...He losses in fights,he gets trapped
in many occasions,he ends up in the prison etc etc....

Arulmozhi himself accepts he declines the lankan throne due to pre
arranged politics....He gets angry and irritated when people get
smart like in irandam paagam...he dreams of alaikadalum
oynthirukka....

he argues why a odakara penn may be as good as vanathi...

he accepts he understood his sister sending him to Lanka

he falls victim to Malaria..

He gets rescued by women time and time again....Ponni
amman...mandakini...poonguzhali....If Kalki wanted to make Arulmozhi
a in fallible hero he would have created a ship fight to rescue
Vandiya devan...
Or atleast made him swim with his freind all the way to the shore...

He states that he doesnt want to become King and chages his mind yes
of course to save his freind but...he does act like normal human
beings...

He does not get hero worshiped.....

Coming to Sivakamiyin sabatham Mahendra pallava is defeated
Narasimha pallava changes mood so often....

Kalki doesnt hero worship his charectors

I rest my case

Sri

Dear Sampath

I feel that you are falling into a trap and not kalki. You seem to
suggest that Arulmozhi should necessarily have been brought down in
th eyes of the readers at some point in the novel. But why should
that be? Arulmozhi is not a fictitious character but Rajaraja
himself, arguably among the greatest rulers from India. He should
certainly have been a super human to have achieved what he did. Kalki
cannot invent or subvert history to satisfy your expectation. With
all these constraints Kalki has done a marvelous job of etching early

I would say your analysis here is a rather faulty expectations regarding characterisation.

Regards. Ganesan.
