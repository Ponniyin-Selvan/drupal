hi venkat

my son goes to bhavans school here and last sat they had a global
indian harmony event . the event was sponsored by zee and featured
among others ....a stirring presenation by the boys and girls of
bavans school trichy...there were lot of other presentations, incl
north indian folk and popular bolly wood numbers...the audience
wasnt galvanised and you needed the anchors to get them involved
with some forceful pushing to calp etc....but then came the trichy
performance...as the performers walked for a karagam dance, i
thought they are going be some drab dance ( the likes of what we see
in our state ....day's) but then the tempo picked up....the karagams
were not the type which are tied to the heads....then we realised
these were serious performers...the boys and girls really
rocked....they did everything you would not expect them to do...they
danced on the tambalam, went flat on the floor to pickup a kerchief,
went up and down steps, crawled up a ramp.....for once despite a
heavily north indian audience here....they were the first to receive
a standing ovation that lasted a few minutes ( with no prodding from
the comperers)

the next inspiring one was a zee tv contest winner flown in from
india....a vision challenged boy...diwakar....sang many popular
bollywood numbers..but his last peice....maa thuje salam....vande
mataramm.....had the whole audience up and in tears,,,,sare jahan se
acha......vande mataram...

vj
(p.s i think they will telecast this show in zee this week sometime)

that. You should actually read why they shot in the court and what they
wanted to achieve. They wanted Poorna Swaraj and they wanted some sort of
attention to their declaration. When they were arrested and produced before
the court and the arguments gets the due attention of the press and
eventually the public. Then their case gets attention what they were looking
for. The English realize this later and from that point it was not a public
court it was private hearing.

As I said I wont equate Bhagat Sing and Vanchinathan. My point is Bharati /
vanchinathan should have been given due representation in the Indian
Independence History.

My heart bleeds for all the Tamizh Martyrs who didn't get their due.

Chandesa was an ardent devotee of Siva. Once when he was worshiping
shiva, his father distrubed him...and being sucha devot.. he severed
his father's legs with his axe. He then bathed the Lingam with the
milk of cows.

Currently reading below in a book, sps sir to help more:

On the east gopuram of the Chidambaram temple is a figure of
Chandesanugrahamurti in which Chandesa is represented with
the axe between his folded arms.

A group of images under the name Chandesvaraprasadadeva
was set up in the Tanjore temple by

(l) the god Chandesvaraprasadadeva with four arms,
(2) the demon Musalagan with two arms,
(3) the goddess Umaparamesvarl,
(4) Mahadeva (the linga worshipped evidently by the boy Chandesa),
(5) the devotee with two arms,
(6) his father also with two arms represented as having fallen down
and lying on the ground and
(7) Chandesa receiving with his two arms the boon of a flowergarland
from Siva.

This description agrees with the story related in the Periyapuranam,
a compilation of the thirteenth century A.D.

Historians say that by glorifying the Chandesa episode, Rajendra
Chozha chose to submit all his success through the military
conquests to Lord Shiva
