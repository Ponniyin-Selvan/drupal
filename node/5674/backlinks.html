my god, you have an excellent memory....not good for me. anyway
there were a few of these spots...where you could get lost inside
chennai...
apart from those listed above

the adyar river mouth ( broken bridge) - walk along oorur alcott
kuppam side from elliots beach to this place.

the muthukkadu sand bund ( just after mgm) you will see a nice drop
( before the bridge) - a lone palm tree standing - and then the
water edge - you can keep walking along the water side cross over in
knee deep water to the sand bund...poor mans mauritius

of course league soccer and hockey games inside the campus and it was a
spectacular sight to have the games interrupted by deers .....

on the other side of town - post old fishing harbour - manali side -
a among the crashing waves on the storm protection stones ....

pallavaram - butt grounds - well not a seculded place - but a haven
for cricket enthusiasts ( we used to have similar near the ayyapan
kovil /chettinad school ) just after the state bank traning centre -
every inch of available space would be occupied by budding
tendulkars and dravids - infact the stumps were dual -meaning you
would have two teams playing ( batsmen on either side of the
stumps) - with about thousand boys playing - it was amazing how the
fielders would still stay focussed and the game would go on....there
was another two grounds - abutting the airport road belonging to the
army...

for the lucky few who had their ` friends ' but unlucky enough to
have parents who wouldnt give them any pocket money - there was
always nageswara rao park, snake park and children park, well lets
call all parks, valluvar kottam.....alsa mall ( now place taken by
spencers)...madras university canteen...

now you know, the amount of time that was left for productive
pursuits....

Woodlands Drive in is no more

Naan veenakiya naatkalil megavum suvarasiyamaaga veenanavai .....

Of all my wasted days the most were at woodlands drivein...

U could order a cup of coffee ( the old white at times ceramic chipped cupsn saucer)share it ( one by three at times) n while away a couple of hours.
Sometimes u would feel sorry for the elderly gentlmen waiting on u but willdef miss them...the drivein concept trays .....when I first bought a car (a rickety old 800) the first stop was there , use those propped up trays.....ghee roast, naan ( butter naans with the orangish masala)

Amidst those lush green corners was heavenly.....was also a fav haunt to spend a few peaceful hours with your u know who.....

Chennai will never the same again....poor marketing execs ...they will be the worst hit. Vj
