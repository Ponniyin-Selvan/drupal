First of all, origins of sati are in oppression, not emotion. Women in
those days preferred death to the treatment they got as widows. It was not

out of love (does not mean lack of love either) but a choice of lesser
evil. Jauhar and all related acts were choice of death versus dishonor and
treatment as widows.

�
The name Sati originated from Siva's consort and Daksha's daughter who
committed suicide due to dishonor at her father's court. It had nothing to

do with dying with her husband. The Ramayana has one instance of Mandodhari
who dies with her husband becuause she lost it all, and the Mahabharat one
instance of Pandu's second wife Madri who again dies since she caused the
death of her husband. All other women in both epics lived long after their
spouses died.
