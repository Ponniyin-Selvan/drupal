vanakkam,

The situation was very bad yesterday. It was yet another 'Black
Friday'.
==================

Agreed.

What is surprising was the absence of National Media attention
unlike as it was shown during Tsunami. Sun TV covered extensively.
Jaya TV crew did show some serious situations. National Media treats
this as Regional subject / tragedy.

The shocking news of Two buses , One near Ramanad - on way to
Rameswaram with 100 (strandered - overloaded) passengers and another
at Pattukkottai with about 70 passengers - getting rolled down due
to the flash flood and loss of heavy life - was due to the fact that
the Bus Driver could not REALISE that the existing road was already
washed out and hence suddenly from the road level - about 4 feet
water - the bus plunged further to about 7 feet lesser level with
water flowing in full swing for about 10 feet above. This was
percievable from the video of Jaya TV.

National Media were not covering such events as they covered
Tsunami.

Therefore the magnitude of havoc is not really understood or
reported. In the Government "News Reel" prior to screening of the
Main Film in the Theatres we have been seeing "The Floods in
Brahmaputra or Mahanadhi" took place 5 / 6 months earlier.

These visuals never affected us. Now we are seeing such things
happening right in front of our eyes and what we see and hear is
really disturbing.

Cauvery - Thenpennai - Kedilam - Manimutharu - Vaigai in FULL FURY
affecting 15 Districts and about One Million people. The loss of
live, damage of crops etc., are yet to be assessed.

500 out of 800 State Transport Buses got stuck on the Road. There is
not a single State Highway in the Southern Districts left
unaffected.

People who boarded buses / trains 3/4 days back are yet to reach
their Destinations!

China has decided to REVIVE the 2600 Yr Old water Management
System.

I think it is time we also consider reviving our OLD WATER
MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS ! Recall the Canal running parallel to
Kumbakonam - Madras Road for about 5 / 6 Kms.
We find it disappear suddenly.

The NEW TOWNSHIPs on the banks of Lakes and Road Expansions without
caring for water outlets are some of the reasons for the present
havaoc.

Apart from connecting rivers through out India, Inland Water
Management also should be looked into in detail as it existed in
olden days atleast in Tamilnadu - Cauvery belt.

sps

SatishKumar Arunachalam Dear SPS,

Well said. I too felt bad when not even a mention about these flood is
done in any of the national channels. Worst thing, Sun TV complaining
about that nothing is being done, when everything possible is being
done. How inhuman, making politics out of such disasters.

My father reached home safely yesterday evening after a two day
ordeal. Dalmia cement company distributed food packets to all the
train passengers stranded in DalmiaPuram / Pullambadi area. The entire
belt looks like ocean, my father said.

Within hours the trains were halted in ariyalur, plenty of busses came
to the station to evacuate passengers to other cities like Tanjavur,
tiruchi etc so that the small village Ariyalur doesnt get overcrowded.
Pretty proactive measure....but this Sun TV a bane for our state.
Train drivers were very much cooperative and waited for nearly an hour
so that people can know about the departure of a train from ariyalur
to chennai and board it even late.

Line man gandhi who ran one km to save the Anathapuri express and the
other line man(forgot the name) who used his personal cellphone to
alert the authorities are incarnation of god who save thousands of
life.

Hope the rain stops and we can limp back to normalcy. As Swetha said,
the best thing we can do is PRAY..

with prayers for the suffering people,
Satish

In olden days, the individual and family responsiblities were in line
with the social responsibility. Hence, during the war, each family,
was willing to takepart in it, by making male members to fight.
Adhithi satkaram or virundhombal, was given a immense importance as
we read in stories and history.

Now a days, first is me, then my family, then my relatives and then
town, city, district, state, etc. This may be correct in many
occasions and not during disasters.

If there had been a regular, activity of cleaning the tanks, ponds,
etc. Then the situation may have been different now.

Good to hear about the intiative to clean the temple, that may
trigger this also. Actioning before the actual hit is commendable,
but, reaction atleast is a prudence.

Rajendhra Cholan!
