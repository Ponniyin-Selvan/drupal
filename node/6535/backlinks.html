Dear Sir,

Unkalukku therinchathu kooda enakku theriyaathu sir! I am sure you would have explored a lot for your novel.

However I am giving the details what I know...

The kollidam branches from kaviri at mukkambu, unlike the other branches ofkaviri it carries the surplus overflowing water from the kaviri. The gap between the kaviri and kollidam increased at srirangam (Creating an island) and getting closer at kallanai. Again the kollidam carries the flood overflow at kallanai. எவ்வளவு நீர் வந்தாலும் கொள்வதால் இது கொள்ளிடம் என அழைக்கப்படுகிறது (evvalavu neer vanthaalum kolvathalithu kollidam ena azhaikkappadukirath u).

At Kallanai one must be very careful if interested to take bath or walk on the sand in this river, because there are many quicksand areas and plenty of crocodiles. The crocodiles can be seen throghuout.. .

The alignment of the river is: Mukkambu - Sri Rangam -  Kallanai - Near lagudi (May be anbil too) - -  -Thirumazhabadi - Arnamanaikurichi - kaaraipakkam - Manchmedu - Thirumanur - - - - - -  Near Seerkazhi ----Bay of Bengal

The width of this river is 1 km at Thirumanur and Thirumazhabadi. Among thebranches of kaviri this should be the widest river.

The branch rivers of kaviri including kaviri dries up if the sluices at kallanai are closed, but the kollidam will have atleast a small stream of water always even during summer. Near Seerkazhi the water flow in kollidam willbe more evenif there is only small flow on the upstream side. This is due to the water table which is almost equal to or little

Between the year 1998 to 2000 some period the kollidam river was running full, I had seen that, it was looking like a sea not a river and it was running with a great sound hooooooooooooo. ... திருப்பாற்கடலே பூமிக்கு வந்ததுபோல் வெள்ளை நுறையுடன் ஹோவென்ற சத்தத்துடன் ஒடியது வெள்ளம். இது போன்ற சமயத்தில் தான் நம் கிழவர் பழுவேட்டரையர் படகிலிருந்து விழுந்து வெள்ளத்தில் அடித்துச் செல்லப்பட்டிருக்க வேண்டும்.
