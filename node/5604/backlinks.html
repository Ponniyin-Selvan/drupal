Thiruvidaimaruthur! MAHALINGAM surrounded by 9 Parivara temples 
for which Vinayaga is Thiruvalanchuzhi - Muruga Swamimalai and

===================
SOMASKANDA OF THIRUVARUR IS THE PARIVARA STHALAM FOR THIRUVIDAI 
MARUTHUR MAHALINGAM !

These figure within the 7 (SAPTHA) Vidanga Sthalams.

Dangam : Uli. Vidangam : Without using Uli. Created by Mystic 
effect (of Viswakarma). Musukundan, chola King (this is the earliest 
reference !)with his Capital in Thiruvarur, helped Indira in 
Devasura War in obtaining AMRITH and Indira wanted to reward him. 
Said to chose The Best, except Indirani, Indira pathavi and 
Airavatham ! Musukundhan wanted the LINGAM worshipped by Indira to 
establish in Thiruvarur. Taken aback (for not including this in his 
priority list !) Indira approached Viswakarma who created similar 
Lingam, which Indira gave to Musukundan. The Lingam told the truth 
and it was established in nearby place. Like wise six were 
established in Thirunallar, Thirukkaravasan, Thiruvelvikudi, 
Mayavaram etc., known as SAPTHA VIDANGA. In all these temples, 
there are (were) MARAGATHA LINGAMs (which is supposedly created by 
Viswakarma). In all these temples Navagrahas will be standing 
opposite to Siva (on the Eastern end, facing Siva, in row, one next 
to other).

The Seventh time, the ORIGINAL lingam worshipped by Indira has come 
down to earth on its own and established in Thiruvarur.

Hence This Lingam is having WINGS !

Here the THIAGARAJAR is SOMASKANDAR !

and unfortunately, the Priests fail to impress upon these details..

Thivarur Temple has certain very special details :

Hatageswararm - Place for performing Yoga; Erisinakotravai - Established by Rajendra
Kamambika - Muthuswamy Dikshidhar's Navabarana Keerthanas
Aazhi Theru
Sibi - Chariot.

Siva's dance in Thiruvarur is also special. It is known as AJABA 
NADANAM ! since Lord Vishnu also worships siva, while sleeping, the 
movement of Siva - over the chest of sleeping Lord Vishnu - is 
involuntary (Jaba: With dedication; ajaba - involuntary) and thus 
AJABA.

This picture of Nataraja over sleeping Vishnu - is depicted in the 
Temple while coming out of Thiyagarajan Sannidhi and turning right 
(North-wards) to move around the sanctum !
