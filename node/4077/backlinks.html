--- singanallur ramaswamy wrote:

---------------------------------

Hi all,

Am reading SS for the umpteenth time and still find it
a fascinating book. But what prompts this mail is the
similarities in some situations I find in SS and
Kalki's magnum opus PS.

1. Paranjoti entering Kanji fort in a surreptious
manner with help from budha bikshu naganandi just like
VD enters kadambur aranmanai and tanjai kottai with
his ingenious ways.

2. The sudden change and charge by mahendra pallavar
as veerabahu to attack paranjoti when the two travel
to nagarjuna hill with the latter carrying naganandi's
'olai', similar in manner to arumozhi's sudden brake
of his horse and lightning fast attack on VD in lanka
when the two meet for the first time.

3. Lightning strikes a tree when naganandi is creating
a breach in the banks of veeranam lake which is
noticed by gundotharan through the light cast by the
fire that has resulted following the lightning strike
which is similar to the lightning strike in mid sea
when VD ties himself around the flag post of the
abandoned ship on which lightning falls.

4. The floods after veeranam lake breaks its banks
thanks to naganandi, and mamalla pallavan, sivakami
and ayanar using the raft to reach the safety of
mandagapattu, is closely resembling a similar scene in
PS's last part when cauvery breaks its banks and
vanathi and poonguzhali are saved by arulmozhi varman
when they are swept away in flood waters.

5. The start and finish of PS is at veeranam lake and
what a poignant picture is painted by Kalki which
brings lumps to the throats of the readers (am talking
abt Manimegalai's passing away) which to me is similar
to the way he ends SS with Sivakami dancing to the
theveram 'Munnam avan naamam kettal' and mamalla
pallavar looking on with tears streaming down his face
even as sivakami is oblivous of her surroundings -
again an ending which touches the hearts of the
readers.

Kalki has repeatedly written about his characters
almost getting drowned in all his three novels, SS, PS
and Alai Osai and Sunda, who has written Kalki's
wonderful biography 'Ponniyin Pudalvar' which was
serialised in Kalki mag, had explained that this was
because the author himself had undergone a similar
near-drowning experience in his childhood.
