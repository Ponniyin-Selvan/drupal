Folks this is so funny, I ought to share it with you.
My grandmother was a very pleasant, affectionate
person who spoke only Tamil and a wee When we were kids some American student ( a class mate
of my chittappa) had visited us. His name was
"Squashbrookes". We kids went running in and aked
Patti where chittappa was and she very sweetly said
"antha SAUCUBOOSU oda vellila porikanga".
:)
It was a riot

--- Madan U Sreenivasan

White skinned people across the world seem baffled
at our comfort
with the English language

Are you trying to imply that english as a language
is currently seen as that
of 'white' people? This for an instant gave me great
elation, but soon as
realized this is as racist as it could get. Am sorry
to say this, but if
'brown' people talking a language as that of 'white'
people makes them any
better, the 'brown' people are demeaning themselves
and the 'whites'.

Did you forget that *only* unified britain was the
homeland of english until
a few centuries back? That the rest of the 'white'
world was speaking
languages varying from french to spanish to dutch?
why are you equating
english to 'white'?

Are you forgetting the spectacular english fluency
of the many african
colonies?

Language is a common tool. Especially english is
seen as a global business
language nowadays. Let us not try to flatter
ourselves at speaking what the
'white' man did. Its more demeaning than appraising.

Sorry to have such strong counter views - I might
seem the devil to strong
nationalists. I remember zig ziglar's audio in which
he pooh poohs american
movies in which european americans are shown as
broad minded in accomodating
african americans, and the african americans
brimming with gratitude. Ziglar
says that the very act of showing the 'white's
accomodating the 'black's is
discriminating. And I agree.

Regards,
Madan.

Warm Regards,

Deepa Prakash
