Prof.V.I.Subramaniam (1926-2009): /In Memoriam/

Prof.V.I.Subramaniam is the soul and spirit of the academic movement
and grand alliance of scholars called Dravidian Linguistics
Association of India which recently conducted its 37th Annual meeting
at Thiruvananthapuram with out his physical presence for the first
time. He is not only a teacher and scholar of international repute but
also an academic leader, visionary, guide, friend and philosopher for
a host of scholars in language and literature. He is a builder of
institutions in the words of late Kannada scholar Prof.R.C Hiremath.
He is a legendary crusader for the spread of right type of knowledge
and mentor of scholars of international standard. He comes in the
great lineage of grammarians like Panini, Agastya,
Tolkapapiyar,Kesiraja, Nannaya, Narayana Bhattatiri, A.R.Rajaraja
Varma, T.P.Meenakshisundarnar from India and Beschi, Caldwell,
Gundert, Kitttel, Brown and others from abroad, who have enriched our
understanding of our ?languages. As one of the leading academic
leaders in the field of linguistics and literature in this part of the
country he has brought in a great acumen and perspective vision in to
the study of Indian languages especially Dravidian Languages.

His founding of Dravidian Linguistic Association (DLA), International
School of Dravidian Linguistics (ISDL), International Journal of
Dravidian Linguistics (IJDL), and DLA News has paved the way for a
revival of interest in Dravidian studies at the national and
international level and kindled many allied activities like founding
of international institutes for languages and language universities in
India. Perhaps, the best example in his experiments with this grand
academic vision and cooperation was the establishment of the Dravidian
University at Kuppam in Andhra Pradesh. It was natural that he was
called upon to be the first Vice chancellor of the Tamil University
for two terms and the Pro Chancellor of Dravidian University. He was
behind the endeavors of founding and hosting of International
Conference Seminars on Tamil Malayalam and other languages.
Pondicherry Institute of Linguistics and Culture (PILC) was ?also his
creation.

His academic publications include research works in Tamil, Malayalam
and English among which ?an Index of Puranaanuuru , Dialect Survey of
Malayalam and a host of research papers which need to be collected and
published in volumes. Hundred and more publications of DLA and ISDL
some of which were prepared under his editorship speak of his vision,
hard work and planning and organizational command. The Dravidian
Encyclopedia, The Dravidian Tribal Encyclopedia, The Handbook of
Tamilnadu, Kerala and West Bengal, A Handbook of Jainism, Grammatical
theories in different Dravidian Languages ,the Dialect Survey Maps of
Kerala and the English translations of Katantra, Mugdabodham,
Litlatilakam, Balavyakaranam etc ?are some of the major works prepared
under his supervision. ?He is an advocate of interdisciplinary studies
and as an innovator he introduced folklore, place name studies,
anthology, and sociology, philosophy in the curriculum for language
and literature students. He directed projects in computer application
for languages like voicing of characters Malayalam, Tamil, Oriya,
Bengali etc.He developed the cognate language teaching method for
teaching Dravidian languages to the members of the family. He was also
a translator. He ?got the famous book on literary criticism by Rene
Wellek and Austin Warren the Literary Theory translated into Tamil by
Dr.Gloria Sundramathy under his supervision and he himself translated
a few novels from Malayalam to Tamil. He ?obtained degress from
Annamalai University and Indiana University and worked under great
scholars like Fred Householder,Vaiyapuri Pillai and had extensive
academic contacts with scholars like
M.B.Emeneau,Rev.Fr.Thaninayagam,Kamil Zvelebil, R.C.Hiremat,Donappa,
Mahadeva Sastri,T.P.Meenakshisundaranar,Sooranad Kunjan Pillai and
others. As a University Professor he has guided more than 50 scholars
for their Ph.D.in Tamil,Malayalam,Kannada,Telugu,English and Hindi.
Perhaps one of his best achievements is the grooming of so many fine
scholars in the subject who became leaders in their field. When he
retired from the University he could devote more time to the
institutions he created and sustain the momentum in his works.

As ? the nationalist to the core he is averse to get funds from
foreign agencies for academic research. The great Indologist late
P.V.Kane was his model in this regard and he prefers funding from our
own governments and he was successful in that too. The State
governments of Kerala ?Tamilnadu, Andhra and West Bengal were generous
enough to support him and the support from the Central government is
paltry ?the reason for ?which is know ?only to them. When one Central
HRD minister laid the condition of removal of the name Dravidian from
the name of his organization for central funding he had the courage to
taunt him to remove the same from our national anthem and spurned the
offer. For him Indian Grammatical tradition is an inclusive and
composite one and the naming of Dravidian Linguistic Association is
not an indication of divisiveness but rather a desire for legitimate
recognition and appropriation of the heritage. His vision of
nationalism is such he could bring in scholars from various Indian
languages under the banner of his association. His founding of Bengali
studies in ISDL and the exemplary work being done there will speak for
itself. He is an internationalist and one can see the portraits of
foreign scholars like M.B.Emeneau, Caldwell,Gundert,Kamil Zvelebil
unveiled by him in the ISDL and the seminars held on their
contributions to Indian studies.

At the personal level he is an awe inspiring teacher who taught
knowledge, hard work, punctuality, dedication, integrity, and service
to humanity, social accountability and many other noble qualities to
his students and others. He is always a role model for emulation.
Personally he is always self effacing. He is a man of few words but
many deeds and achievement to his credit. He is always unassuming,
deeply affectionate with out expressing in bombastic words and shuns
publicity and lime light. ' I prefer to remain unsung 'was his
declared motto.Till his last moment he discouraged ?and avoided any
felicitation function or volume on him. In the word of late
Prof.K.L.Janert, German Indologist he is a towering personality
unmatched by any but keeps a ?low profile. He ? has rightly been
accorded honors by many Universities in India and abroad.The
University of Jaffna,Rabindra Bharati University,The University of
Pune and Tamil University conferred on him honorary doctorates and the
Murasoli Foundation has conferred its award ?on him.As a legend in his
life time he became the main protagonist in a campus novel in Tamil
called Kakita manitarkal written by the well known Tamil novelist
Prapanchan.
He is concerned about the oppressed and downtrodden and spearheaded
activities and charities for education of the poor children. He had
organized research works and maintained a unit for the teaching of
mentally retarded children in ISDL. He is pained at the discrimination
and suffering of the Tamils in Srilanka and his support to ? their
rehabilitation of scholars from Srilanka who were displaced due to the
ethnic and freedom struggle is well known.
In the demise of such a colossus we all feel orphaned and grieve his
loss with a heavy heart. His exit created a vacuum which is difficult
to be filled up. The field of ?Dravidian studies including Tamil
Malayalam, Telugu Kannada, Tulu and other tribal languages and Indian
studies in general will ever cherish his memory for his endeavors to
develop them individually and collectively. He has ?created and
nurtured many institutions ?aiming at the revival of the ?Indian
grammatical tradition which had an edge over the other traditions ?in
the past and was an inspiration to the modern linguistics to develop
in its manifold fronts and to maintain our leadership in these fields.
It is the bounden duty of his successors who take over the mantle of
him ?to strive their best to carry forward the ideals and works which
were dearer to his heart.It would be a handsome tribute to him. The
academic community at large grieve his irreparable loss and offers its
condolences to the members of his family ?friends students ?the
fellows and colleagues in his institutions.

K.Nachimuthu

--
Palaniappan Vairam Sarathy
Associate RF Engineer
