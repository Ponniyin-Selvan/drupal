Dear Friends,

Our Professor Swaminathan and his team are launching a new website aimed at spreading awareness for the preservation of our priceless monuments. To know more about this, I am reproducing the message in Swaminathan Sir's own words.

Interested members, please attend this program. 

------------Start Quote-----------------
--------
Tamil Heritage Group
Chennai
 
Dear friends
We are a few friends who have recently formed an informal group called Tamil Heritage Group. The Group met a few times and discussed among ourselves on our heritage and the need for its preservation and documentation and alsosensitizing the public about it. We are aware a number of individuals and groups are also concerned about these issues and contributing in their ways. We too would like to do our mite.
 
In this direction we decided that we may host a website similar to Wikipedia as a means of documenting the Tamil heritage.   We plan to launch the site as per the following details.
 
Date, Day and Time:  4th October, Saturday, 5-00 PM
Venue: Vinobha Hall, Takkar Bapa Vidyalaya Complex, 53, T Nagar
 
RSVP: Annamalai (94441-83198); S. Swaminathan (24611265)  
 
 
Men behind the movement
1. Annamalai. (Diectore, Gandhi Study Centre, Chennai)
Sa. Kandasamy (Tamil writer and Documentry producer, Chennai)
VN Seetharam (Book-seller)
KRA Narasiah (Marine Engineer and Historian)
Ajithadass (Professor [Rtd], Presidency College, Chennai)
Badri Seshadi, (MD, New Horizon Media, Chennai)
Datchinamoorthy (Botanist, Chennai)
Udhayakumar (Botanist, Chennai)
S Kannan (Bank of Baroda)
M. Manivannan (MD, Symantec, Chennai)
R. Ramaswami
TK Ramachandran IAS (MD, TN Slum Clearance Board, Chennai)
Sundar Bharadwaj (MD, Adithya Shelters, Chennai)
Ravi Subramanian (Writer and Documentary maker, Chennai)
etc
 
 
 
Wikipedia
WIKI is an online collaborative tool. For long, the only encyclopedias available to public were in the form of printed volumes - Encyclopedia Brittanica and the like. Then, with the advent of compact disc technology, we saw Microsoft Encarta, a CD based encyclopedia. With so much happening in the world, with new inventions, wars, cyclones, displacements, momentous events creating new countries and change of regimes, all printed and CD versions ofencyclopedias were becoming obsolete.
A collaborative people's encyclopedia came up under the name Wikipedia (http://en.wikipedia.org/), in 2001. The contributors were people from the public. Poorly written or factually incorrect articles were quickly corrected and replaced by more knowledgeable people. Initially, most experts doubted that Wikipedia will ever become a respected reference point. But by around 2006, almost everyone has accepted that the world's most eminent knowledge database is Wikipedia. It still has some errors, incomplete articles, poorlywritten pieces and downright junk. But people are improving - for free - every aspect of this resource.
Subsequently, the idea of collaborative Wiki has been taken up by others tocreate more specialised databases - for science, for religion and for sports.
 
Tamil heritage Wiki
We propose to use the same model to build a Tamil Heritage Wiki. This will attempt to bring together all aspects of Tamil heritage - arts, literature,music and dance, language, grammar, heritage sites etc. This will consist of linked online articles, photographs, audio/video content, in both Tamil and English.
On the day of inauguration, we will have some content. This will be augmented on a regular basis.
---------------End Quote----------------
 
Regards,
Venkatesh

Then, with the advent of compact disc technology, we saw Microsoft Encarta, a CD based encyclopedia. With so much happening in the world, with new inventions, wars, cyclones, displacements, momentous events creating new countries and change of regimes, all printed and CD versions ofencyclopedias were becoming obsolete.
A collaborative people's encyclopedia came up under the name Wikipedia (http://en.wikipedia .org/), in 2001. The contributors were people from the public. Poorly written or factually incorrect articles were quickly corrected and replaced by more knowledgeable people. Initially, most experts doubted that Wikipedia will ever become a respected reference point. But by around 2006, almost everyone has accepted that the world's most eminent knowledge database is Wikipedia. It still has some errors, incomplete articles, poorly written pieces and downright junk. But people are improving - for free - every aspect of this resource.
Subsequently, the idea of collaborative Wiki has been taken up by others tocreate more specialised databases - for science, for religion and for sports.
 
Tamil heritage Wiki
We propose to use the same model to build a Tamil Heritage Wiki. This will attempt to bring together all aspects of Tamil heritage - arts, literature,music and dance, language, grammar, heritage sites etc. This will consist of linked online articles, photographs, audio/video content, in both Tamil and English.
On the day of inauguration, we will have some content. This will be augmented on a regular basis.
