--- In ponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, Venkateswaran Kasirajan

There were 4 Rishis - Sanakar, Sanadhanar, Sanathkumarar & other -
supposed to be the Prime disciples of Lord Siva.

In every Siva temple you would have noticed Siva - sitting under
the Aalamaram - with the hymn " KALLLALAN PUDAI AMARNTHU .. "
indicating that the Aalam was of Stone.

Recall Ode on Grecian Urn by John Keats. On a embossing found in
the Urn, a Lover, under a tree, was holding the hand of his Lass.
Keats exclaimed : Lucky guy - the Lover will never leave, you will
hold her hand for ever and the tree will never be barren.

It is the similar concept. Indicating ETERNITY.

So, there was always a kind of opting for Preference .. One of the
Prime disciples of Sanakaadhi Munivars (Disciples).

The Painter or the Sculpture takes the Liberty making the semblence
of a MORTAL in such position so that such mortal gets the
satisfaction of being considered as one of the Prime Disciple.

Uthama .. Hara Sarana Sekaran (Konerirajapuram inscrpn)
Rajaraja .. Siva Paatha Sekaran (Big temple -do- )
Rajendra .. Siva Sarana Sekaran (Tvlchzhi -do- )

Their desire to be underneath the Auspicious Feet of Siva is
explicit. Art Maker converts / gives shape to such desire.

Such desire was inculcated into them through Appar - Pls Ref
messages on NALLUR PANCHAVARNESWARAR TEMPLE " AVVANNAME AVAN (Appar)
MUDI MEL THAN (Sivan) PAATHAM VAITHAN .. "

anbudan / sps

Dear Sri,

A young person with Jadamagudam (Hairdo in the form of Crown) and
another elderly person with a Beard - PAINTED 1000 yrs back, one (a
pair) below the other (another pair) - on a SOUTH WEST Corner
Pillar - at the Western end of the Southern corridor (Sandaaram -
pls recall Dr. Kalaikkovan's interview in Sun TV re Thanjai temple)
running East West turning towards Westen corridor.

WHY SANAKADHI MUNIVARS ::

Earlier Dr. Nagaswamy identified one of them as intepreted as Karuvarar. There were difference in opinions whether
long. Scholars applied the Aagamic definitions of showing Kings and
Saints differently and arrived that the pair resembled Sanakaadhi
Munivars aagamically!

One in his Book "The Big Temple" that there is a VEERAKKAZHAL in his leg
and so could not have been Sanakadhi Munivars.

Dr. Kudavoil opined that Aagamically they resemble Sanakaadhi
Munivars - 4 of them !

Dr. Kalaikkovan also concurrs with the same line of thinking.

THEN WHO SAYS IT IS persons - like me and you - feel atleast one should be

Why ? Because it is the feel. The Lakshnam - Gambeeram etc.
Exactly as Kalki described in

But I do not want to claim better knowledge over the Scholars. The
compromise I am willing to make with myself is that FOR THE PURPOSE
OF PAINTING THE SANAKAADHI (FOUR) MUNIVARS - the PAINTER MIGHT HAVE
CHOSEN THE KING/s AS THE MODELS ! BECAUSE OF THE RAJALAKSHANAM.

Now who is Right? BOTH ARE RIGHT.

How? Rajarajan was calling himself Siva Paatha Sekaran.
Rajendra was calling himself Siva Sarana Sekaran (Thanks to Dr.
Kalaikkovan). And both of them had their own Sivites Gurus - Easana
Siva Pandhithar and Sarva Siva Pandithar - respectively.

The person with Beard should have been the Gurus!
The person with Rajalakshnam should have been the King - BUT HERE
AGAIN THE DESCIPLE OF LORD SIVA! These FOUR should have formed the
MODEL (MODULE) FOR DEPICTING THE FOUR DISCIPLES OF LORD SIVA -
Sitting in front of Dakshinamurthy!

We see these pairs in Chozha Dakshnimurthys as well ..

Personally I feel - the younger image should have Rajendra and the
older one - with Beard could have been Rajaraja - because from 1006
to 1010 - from the commencement to consecration - Rajaraja @ Siva
Paatha Sekaran would have definitely grown Beard - as seen from
various stone images - including the one from Tiruvisaluar!

Do you have proof that the pair appears in Tiruvisalur is that and Danthi Sakthi Vidangi? Connecting Hirnya Garbha - Cow etc., they
are. If they are NOT then who are they?

Then another serial to be commenced on Tiruvisalur ..
======================================
I have a tip for those interested in working more on this ::

The First LONGEST List of Grants / Personnel - appeared from the
Konerirajapuram inscriptions of Sembian Maadevi - consecrated in the
3rd Yr of Uthama! Surprisingly The female diety is BHOOMI DEVI !

There are four icons - resembling Munivars - sitting in front of
Dakshinamurthy! The Main Diety was named after KANDRADITHA THEVAR
by Sembian Madevi. Like our "Rajaraja" Perunthachan, the CHIEF
ARCHITECT - CUM- ENGINEER of this Temple was called HARA SARANA
SEKARAN - evidently named after UTHAMA!

I feel Sembiyan Madevi's Haracharana Sekaran - first introduced the
IDEA of putting KANDRADITHAR - UTHAMA - (May be He himself) as the
Sanakadhi Munivars in the form of Icon and improving the ideas in LAVISH GRANTS - APPOINTMENT OF PERSONNEL -
would have also followed the same footsteps and put he and the 4 Sanakadhi Munivars.

Yes.. I am putting a point to ponder :: Konerirajapuram should have
also been a forerunner to temple of Kaanchi!
=========================================
