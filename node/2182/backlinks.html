Dear Mr.Venkateswaran,

Please dont take references from legends and sthalapuranams of temples.Most of them are created when Nayaks ruled.They created them to unite Hindus and to give importance to the temples so that the people started coming to the temples and it will be protected (That's why Indran being a vedic God did so many sins!? and got "Pabhavimosanam" from most of the temples!!) .Nayaks are the real protectors of Old temples.

There are references about Pandya kings in Mahabharatham by Vyasar prier sankam age.

Being a born Hindu Malikafur's damages and looting from Hindu temples are really surprising and cruel.

Regards Gandhi

Venkateswaran Kasirajan As per legends, the core ( I dont know how to call it
in English ) of the temple, was built during the
Sankam era. There is one story that the Sundareswarar
shrine ( it is a beautiful structure that is holded by
massive elephants on all sides - there is one more
story that during Malikafur's ruthless regime in
Madurai, he wanted to verify the authenticity of hindu
gods. One saint promised him that one of the stone
elephant that guards the shrine will eat a sugarcane
and it happened ) was built by the great shirpi the
Mayan himself.

Later Pandya kings built the massive towers that
surround the temple. Many complexes and halls were
constructed by Nayaks.

On the the temple pond, the "sankappalakai" ( sankam
float ) was floating which gave seat to qualified
poets. Even today lots of poetic people gather there
to discuss literature etc., This was the place where
"Thirukkural" was released to the public.

It is a temple that holds nearly 2000 years of history
( which nobody cared to record properlty )

It is a massive temple complex with several ancient
engineering marvels...

Legends and sthalapuranams

In my personal opinion, Sthalapuranam should also be taken into consideration. Sometimes it leads to begining of a actual history.

Lord Shiva, or Vishnu had never been un approachable by us, as they had been Iraiyanar and Thiuvaraikoman for Tamils. Never to speak about Muruga and his big belly sweet brother. No need to speak about the first. The second one has indeed, given us the script and help Raja Raja to find Thirumuraigal and he also helped Thiruthondar on this Vatapi war.

So looking at sthalapuranam of Thiruchenkatankudi, I guess, will also speak about his Pillayar, came to this temple.

Sthalapuranam of Siruvachur Madurakali, will speak about Kannagi and Saamiyar.

So, some times, they are worth, to be taken as a guide for investigations and reasearch.

Now a days, few Sthalapuranam books published, surpisingly speaks about the History of the temples and on its Kalvettu's. I found this in, Thirukarukavoor Garbarakshambigai temple and Chandrachoodeshwarar, Hosur. This should be appreciated.

Rajendhra Cholan!
