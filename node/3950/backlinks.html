"Illusions" written by Richard Bach (Jonathan Livingston Seagul fame) -
a book I read long back, states that whenever we are in trouble, if we
open a book, we will get a solution. Even before readhing this book a
few years back, I have experienced this myself.

I am reading Kalki's Alai Osai (athu oru 8 masama koncham konchama
padikaren). After replying Venkatesh, i started reading Alai Osai.
One sentence struck me hard. In the fourth part, the hero goes to
England for some official work and the period is soon after World War
II. On his return journey, he realises how patriatic the Britishers
were. He used to be a govt. servent and never liked the congress or the
freedom struggle. After seeing the brits, Kalki writes, as if the hero
is thinking,' how patriotic these english are? They do everything for
thier country. ANd me working like a dog for the British govt. without
any feeling for my country. THE ENGLISH EDUCATION HAS CORRUPTED MY MIND"

Venkatesh sir, itha naan sollala....MAHAGANAM PORUNTHIYA KALKI
KRISHNAMOORTHY SOLLARAR. This story is a very good portrayal of the pre
independent india. A must read.

Also, yesterday I wrote that Indian politicians were successful in
changing the meaning of the word 'Secular'. But in actual at many
instance in Alai Osai, Kalki points this out, stating that the govt
(then the birt govt) is spoiling the country by pampering the minority.
So it all started even before the Independence.

Equal to what we have gained from Brits, we have lost also - divide and
rule, Hindu Muslim disunity, minority pampering etc. are due to brits
and we should be able to accept this as part of history.

Think of India with all its current potential minus the communal
dishormony - 100 US should be at the feet of India. Thanks to our
history, it didnt happen.

Regards,
Satish
