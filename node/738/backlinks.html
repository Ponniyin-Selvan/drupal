--- "S.Swaminathan"

Dear Mr Kripashankar

Prakrit, I thing, means natural, as against
Sanskrit, meaning cultured. The
general opinion among the scholars is that a variety
of dialects under the
commeon name Prakrit was being spoken by people in
the northern part of
India. Pali, Apabhramsha and Maharashtri are some of
them. Pulikesin spoke a
Prakrit.

You are right that Prakrit was spoken by people and
Sanskrit was the
official language. But I am not certain that you are
right when you say that
Prakrit is 'refined' Sanskrit. Perhaps it was
otherway about.

There is also one more point I want to mention. In
the course of discussion
in the group I find that some consider Grantha as a
language. Actually it is
only a script. It is held that the Pallavas who came
to rule a substantial
part of Tamilnadu came from the northern parts. They
needed Sanskrit to be
written, in suvadis and in inscriptions. They
adapted the Tamil script for
writing Sanskrit. This they did by creating letters
for those in Sanskrit
and not in Tamil.

It is also held that the present day Malayalam
script came out of it.

With best wishes
Swaminathan

The word "prakrit" kind of means something that is down-to-
earth...like that is used by common men/women in their daytoday life.
so, prakrit is nothing more than but the language that is a "refined"
Sanskrit.

While Sanskrit lived all along for its literary form, it was never
used in daytoday interaction among masses, but Prakrit was. Sanskrit
was sort of Official Language of the State, though not everyone
really understood it.

ippa namba chennai thamizh Prakrit-nnu vechchundaa, senthamizh vandhu
Sanskrit. Chennai thamizhukkunnu thaniyaa yezhuththu vadivamnu
yellaam yedhuvum kadayaadhu.
