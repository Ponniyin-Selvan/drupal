Yes, this does not look good. But the history behind this practice is
different. In older days those who sang the prabhandhams got only the
prasadham as food for the day. These people who sing prabhandhams are
called "adhyApakAs" .. They had no other means of earning a livelihood.
So in order to arrange for their food, they were given a large portion
of the offerings to Perumal. Well it was still not sufficiently large
for a family and that is a different story. These "adhyApakAs" will be
only 8 or 10 in number. Remaining reciters are all treated like normal
devotees. Also, the large portion was given in recognition of their
services of rendering the DivyaPrabhandams. This practice was started by
NathamunigaL during the 900s and today after 1100 years, with utmost no
relevance, is still being continued.REASON: Sheer Bureaucracy! ! Nothing
else.
