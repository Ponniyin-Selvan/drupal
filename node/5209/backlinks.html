Dear mr. Jagathesh

Well if you want a thread that links the sethu to the chola
clan...there is vengaiyin mainthan ( akilans sequel to of the naval assault by rajendra on king mahinda ( of rohanam) ...if
i remember correctly - rajendra's fleet was so massive that when the
first ship reached lanka the last ones were still in port in
india....so rajendra built a bridge of ships resplendant in all
their glory,as ram built the bridge of stones..

while the discussion on sethu started in this form long before my
post of the dialogue mentioned below ( came to me as a forward and i
personally found it a light hearted pun and hence put it into the
group) ...i apologise if it hurt any sentiments and will refrain
from doing so in future. However, what pains me is that depsite
there being hundreds of other interesting and thought provoking
articles put forth in this forum ( as i would assume in others as
well), an innocent no harm intended humourous post garners so much
of limelight and backlash. If you want my personal views on this,
you can search through our archives else write to me and we can have
a no holes barred spar.

What sps sir meant by giving you the credentials of some of the
people in this forum and their works, is just to remind us that
there is so much more constructive work that can be done if we can
channelise our energies towards them - we are only talking of book
publishing, archeology/research etc but simple things like temple
cleaning work. the members of this forum are doing yeoman service in
these diverse fields and not all of them are doing so in search of
money or fame. its our innate drive to restore the rich heritage and
lies forgotten - there are many ways to do this but it can only be
done by showing restraint and being patient. the cause is noble and
the path is tough - and hence the rewards are doubly satisfying.
Please do what little you can to this constructive cause like the
squirril did to sri ram....

rgds
vj
