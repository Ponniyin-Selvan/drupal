SUDHARSANAM

Pudukkottai

Dear friends

Greetings from Pudukkottai!

During the ‘Irandam Yaththirai’ we had an opportunity to interact. Briefly
the objectives of Sudharsanam was explained. We would like to describe in
some detail about our proposed activities and please help us with your
ideas.

=============================

We had mentioned the main objectives for our project.

To offer opportunities, particularly to the younger generation, to get an
exposure to our arts, culture and traditional knowledge. This is to be done
through residential summer camps on the campus and through workshops in the
villages.

Topics: Appreciation of arts, like dance, music, drama, painting, sculpture,
temple architecture, literature, folk traditions, philosophical systems,
heritage tours within the district and its immediate neighbourhood, etc.

To document the legacy of the district of Pudukkottai.

Outcome expected: Building a corpus of archival material in respect of
Pudukkottai. Publish, in Tamil and English, authentic and affordable guide
books, on monuments of the district, monographs on contribution of the
district in various fields, like music, dance, temple architecture,
literature, etc., and biographical sketches of distinguished personalities
connected with Pudukkottai.

To set up a library to act as a resource centre in selected fields of South
Indian arts and culture; collecting archival material on Tamil heritage, in
general, and on contribution of Pudukkottai to Tamil culture, in particular.

The Campus

A 7-acre plot in Sathyamangalam, 14 km from Pudukkottai on the
Pudukkottai-Thiruchy road is being developed into a residential campus with
apartments and dormitories, library and lecture-activity rooms.

The area is also being developed into a theme garden reflecting, among
others, the unique relation the Tamil culture has with nature. The heritage
garden will comprise akam-puram gardens, Ayurveda-Siddha gardens, in
addition to those trees which are not commonly found, like sandal, mahagony,
kadamba, sacred trees of temples, etc.

An energy forest is also being grown in order to generate electricity,
through gasification of biomass and through production of 'bio-diesel'.

We are hopeful of the campus being ready in about 8 months time.

=====================

While we are at the planning stage, we thought we would be benefited by your
suggestions.

Firstly please comment on our objectives. We anticipate one criticism, that
we are over ambitious and are including too many topics in our summer camps.
We have identified the topics which we though would be of interest. However
the actual coverage would depend on the interests of the participants and
the availability of resource persons.

Presently we are in a process of finalising a number of activities which we
can take-up during the next one year. Apart from the initial organisation
activities like establishing contacts with resources
persons/experts/likeminded people and setting-up infrastructure facilities,
we would like to take-up some other activities.

These proposed activities are of three kinds: heritage tours,
retreats and summer camps.

Heritage tours: We plan to organise conducted tours within Pudukkottai
district and also of the sites of importance around Pudukkottai. The later
could include Tanjavur, Gangai-konda-chozhapuram, Darasuram, Thiruchi,
Madurai and Chidambaram. These, we propose to be two to three days duration.
In our opinion a good preview, through slide-shows, and suitable literature
for reference later would add to the usefulness of the tours. We are in the
process of preparing suitable literature, in both Tamil and English. In
addition to the school children whose sensitisation is one among our social
objectives, we plan to organise such tours for other categories of people
also. This would be on commercial basis.

For example, we may attract those who come to Chennai during
music season in December. Long week-ends, vacations are probable time for
such tours.

Your comments and suggestions are invited on this. In particular suggestions
on places to be included, number of days, cost-structure and suitable period
would be useful.

Summer Camps: This is expected to be our main activity and we expect that
this would help us becoming financially self-supporting. I have outlined the
activities under this before. We propose to accommodate about 30
participants for each course. Since we are planning both apartments and
dormitories small families would be able to participate. Planning of
parallel programmes to suit different age groups are also being thought of.

The residential camps can be conducted three times in a year,
one for people in Tamilnadu in May, for Tamils from North India in June and
for NRI Tamils in July/August, coinciding with the vacation in the
respective regions.

A serious thought may be given to this in all aspects of the
programme. We need to know the methods of reaching the target population
within the country and outside.

Retreat: This programme is somewhat in between the above two. Essentially a
residentially programme of a few days, the activities may include, in
addition to heritage tours, exposure to selected fields of Indian/Tamil
heritage.

Please consider this and give your comments.

As I mentioned before your comments would help us tremendously. Specifically
we need your suggestions on:

1.. How attractive the programmes are
2.. On the duration of 3 weeks
3.. On suggested contents
4.. On how they can be made attractive/appealing/useful
5.. On suitable publicity
6.. On fees structure

I have added a draft programme details below which may be consulted if need
be.

May I suggest that you have discussion with the group and let us have the
benefit of such exercise. I also suggest that those in Chennai may meet
specifically with this as agenda. If we are informed in advance one of us
can participate if needed.

With best wishes

Swaminathan and the Sudharsanam team

Pudukkottai

================================

SUMMER RESIDENTIAL CAMP - DRAFT PROPOSAL

(for discussion)

1. Objective:

To provide an opportunity for the public, particularly younger generation,
to get an exposure to our heritage, Indian heritage in general and Tamil
heritage in particular.

2. Participants:

About 30 participants of all age groups. People of Tamil origin, who can
speak Tamil even if they cannot read, are likely to benefit better.

3. Duration:

3 weeks

4. Venue:

Residential campus of Sudharsanam in Pudukkottai district of Tamilnadu. (The
campus is situated in a thickly-wooded campus in the village of
Sathiyamangalam, about 14 km from Pudukkottai close to the
Tiruchy-Pudukkottai highway.) ***Details to be provided****

5. Proposed content:

The following may serve as the range of topics. But the actual content would
be decided on the basis of availability of resource persons and opinion of
the participants.

I. Performing arts:

v Music: Historical development of Indian Music; Music of Tamilnadu; appreciation of south Indian music through lecture-demonstrations and
performances

v Dance: Historical development and styles of Indian dance; appreciation
of Bharathanatyam through lecture-demonstrations and performances

v Folk traditions: Various folk traditions of Tamilnadu and their
contribution to Tamil culture; appreciation of folk traditions

These will be mostly in the form of lectures and lecture-demonstrations

II. Temple arts:

Temple architecture: Dravida, Nagara and Visara styles; Historical
development in Tamilnadu, from cave temples to structural temple; treatises
on temple architecture; role of temples in art and in society; Pudukkottai’s
contribution to temple architecture

III. Sculpture:

Development through the ages; iconography; bronzes

IV. Painting:

Indian painting tradition and Ajanta; History of South Indian painting -
Pallava, Chola, Pandya, Vijayanagara paintings

All these topics will be covered through lectures, slide-shows, power-point
presentation and complimented with site visits.

V. Tamil Literature:

1. Introduction to Tamil Literature

Appreciation of Tholkappiyam, the Poems of Love and War (The Sangam
literature), Thirukkural and other ethical works, the twin epics and other
epics (Chilapathikaram and Manimekhalai), Bakthi literature and devotional
poems, Kamba Ramayanam and other epic works, and Bharathi and Modern
Literature

2. Introduction to Sanskrit literature

Vedic and Classical literatures;

3. Essential features of Indian Philosophical Systems:

4. Modern Indian Thinkers

Ramakrishna Paramahamsa and Swami Vivekananda; Maharishi Ramana; Aurobindo; Theosophists; Jyotibai Phule; Gandhiji; JK;

It is proposed to cover the subject through lectures, interactive sessions
and evening performances, depending on the subject matter.

VI. Topics of General Interest

Apart from these major topics, it is proposed to include a number of topics
of current interest in the form of talks, presentations, discussions,
lecture-demonstrations, etc. A variety of topics can be selected for this.
Choice of topics would depend on the availability of resource persons and
interest of the participants. A sample of such minor topics are:

? History of Tamilnadu and heritage of Tamils

? Indian oral traditions

? Traditional health systems

? Inscriptions and development of scripts

? Numismatics

? A comparative study of Tamil and Sanskrit literatures

? Poetical metres of Sanskrit

? Physics and aesthetics of music

? Viralimalai Kuruvanji: a dance drama

? Folk arts and traditions of Pudukkottai
? Musical heritage of Pudukkottai

? Cave temples of Pudukkottai

? Tamil computing

? Internet resources on Tamil and Indian culture and heritage

? Discussions on

o Alternate development perspective

o A world View for a sustainable society - role of science and
religion

o How do we introduce taste for literature among the younger
generation

o A blueprint for saving our heritage

6. Proposed structure of the camp:

Duration of the camp: 3 weeks

Weekly programme:

On-Campus Programmes 5 days
Off-Campus Programmes 2 days

On-Campus Programme:

1. Day starts with broadcast of Mangala Isai and some other activities
like Demonstration of Kolam

2. Daily 5 formal sessions, 3 sessions in the morning and 2 in the
evening.

3. Informal and interactive sessions

4. Performance/Exposure sessions in the evening

5. Sufficient break/time for sport and entertainment

Off-Campus Programmes (6 days): Conducted tours to various places with
preview sessions.

Possible heritage tours (to be decided in consultation with the
participation)

1. Conventional Pudukkottai heritage tour (4 places, 2 days)

2. Detailed Pudukkottai Heritage tour (4 days 10-12 places)

3. SRB trial (3 days ending up with Brihadisvara temple Tanjavur) – being a tour following the development of Chola temples according to SR
Balasubramanian

4. Cave temples of Pudukkottai (2 days)

5. Devotional tour to temples ‘sung’ by saints: Avudiar Koil
(Manikkavachagar), Viralimali (Arunagirinathar) and Thirumayam Thirumangai
Azhwar)

6. Nature tour like the mangroves of Pichavaram, bird sanctuary at
Keezhakkarai, etc

7. Golden quadrangle (Tanjavur Brahadeesvara Temple, Chidambaram
Temple, Sreerangam temple, Madurai Meenakshi Temple)

8. Mahabalipuram, Kancheepuram, Rameswaram

Special attractions:

? Evening Performances: Music concerts, Dance, folk items, etc.

? Exposure Sessions on Ashtavadhanam, Divyanamam, Pravachanam,
Harikatha, Bommalattam, Lavani, etc

? Heritage tours

? Heritage food: (Chola food, Maratha food, some idea like this. Is
it possible????)
